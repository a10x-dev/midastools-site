## Prompt 1: Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

### Prompt

```
Write a detailed SOP for [PROCESS] at [BUSINESS NAME].

Context:
- Who performs this: [ROLE/TEAM]
- How often: [FREQUENCY — daily, weekly, per client, etc.]
- Tools used: [TOOLS/SOFTWARE]
- Current pain points: [WHAT GOES WRONG OR TAKES TOO LONG]

SOP format:
1. **Title**: SOP — [Process Name]
2. **Purpose**: Why this process exists (1 sentence)
3. **Scope**: Who this applies to and when
4. **Prerequisites**: What must be in place before starting
5. **Step-by-step procedure**: Numbered steps with:
   - Action (what to do)
   - Details (how to do it — specific enough that a new hire could follow)
   - Tool/system (where to do it)
   - Expected output (what "done" looks like)
6. **Decision points**: If/then branches for common variations
7. **Quality checklist**: 5-7 items to verify before marking complete
8. **Escalation**: When and how to escalate exceptions
9. **Revision history**: Template for tracking updates

Make it detailed enough that someone doing this for the first time could complete it without asking questions.
```

## Prompt 2: Job Description & Hiring Post

### Prompt

```
Write a job description and hiring post for a [ROLE] at [BUSINESS NAME].

Context:
- Company: [BRIEF COMPANY DESCRIPTION]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Role type: [Full-time / Part-time / Contract / Freelance]
- Location: [Remote / Hybrid / Office — CITY]
- Salary range: [RANGE]
- Reports to: [MANAGER TITLE]
- Team size: [TEAM SIZE]

Create:
1. **Job title** (SEO-optimized for job boards)
2. **Hook paragraph** (why this role is exciting — sell the opportunity)
3. **What you'll do** (5-7 responsibilities, action-verb format)
4. **What we're looking for** (5-7 requirements — separate "must have" from "nice to have")
5. **What we offer** (benefits, culture, growth — be specific, not generic)
6. **How to apply** (specific instructions + what to include)

Also write:
- LinkedIn job post version (shorter, more conversational)
- Twitter/X announcement (1 tweet to share the opening)
- 5 screening questions to filter applicants

Rules:
- No jargon or corporate fluff
- Include salary range (transparency builds trust and saves time)
- Focus on outcomes over credentials (what they'll DO, not just what degree they have)
```

### Prompt

```

1. [SKILL 1]
2. [SKILL 2]
3. [SKILL 3]
4. [SKILL 4]
5. [SKILL 5]

For each skill/trait, provide:
- 2 behavioral questions (STAR format: "Tell me about a time when...")
- What a GREAT answer looks like (key indicators)

Also include:
- Opening question to put them at ease

Total interview duration: [30/45/60] minutes
Include time allocation per section.
```

## Prompt 4: Customer Service Response Templates

### Prompt

```
Write customer service email templates for [BUSINESS NAME] selling [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Create templates for these 10 scenarios:
1. **Order confirmation**: Warm, sets expectations for next steps
2. **Shipping delay**: Apologetic but proactive, offers solution
3. **Refund request**: Empathetic, follows policy, retains goodwill
4. **Product complaint**: Acknowledges issue, troubleshoots, offers resolution
5. **Feature request**: Thanks them, explains process, sets expectations
6. **Positive review response**: Genuine gratitude, asks for referral
7. **Negative review response**: Professional, addresses concern, takes it offline
8. **Account/billing issue**: Clear explanation, step-by-step resolution
9. **Onboarding welcome**: Excitement + quick-start guide + support contact
10. **Win-back (inactive customer)**: Re-engagement with value, not guilt

For each template:
- Subject line
- Body (under 150 words)
- Tone: [BRAND VOICE]
- Placeholders for personalization: [CUSTOMER NAME], [ORDER NUMBER], [SPECIFIC ISSUE]

Rules:
- Every response must acknowledge their feeling BEFORE solving the problem
- Always include a clear next step
- Sign off with a real name, not "The [Company] Team"
```

## Prompt 5: Meeting Agenda & Follow-Up

### Prompt

```
Create a meeting agenda and follow-up template.

Meeting context:
- Purpose: [WHAT THIS MEETING IS ABOUT]
- Attendees: [WHO IS ATTENDING AND THEIR ROLES]
- Duration: [LENGTH]
- Meeting type: [team standup / client call / strategy session / 1:1 / board meeting]

Agenda:
1. **Pre-meeting email** (sent 24h before):
   - Purpose statement
   - Agenda items with time allocations
   - Pre-read materials needed
   - Questions to come prepared to answer
2. **Agenda document**:
   - Opening (2 min): Purpose + ground rules
   - Section-by-section: Topic → owner → time → decision needed (Y/N)
   - Parking lot: Space for off-topic items
   - Close (5 min): Action items + owners + deadlines
3. **Follow-up email** (sent within 2 hours):
   - Summary of decisions made
   - Action items: WHO does WHAT by WHEN
   - Parking lot items and their plan
   - Next meeting date/time

Keep agendas tight. If a meeting doesn't need to be a meeting, say so.
```

## Prompt 6: Financial Analysis — Monthly Review

### Prompt

```
Help me create a monthly financial review framework for [BUSINESS NAME].

Business context:
- Revenue model: [HOW YOU MAKE MONEY — subscriptions, one-time sales, services, etc.]
- Monthly revenue range: [APPROXIMATE]
- Main expense categories: [LIST TOP 5]
- Team size: [NUMBER]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]

Create a framework that covers:
1. **Revenue analysis**:
   - Total revenue vs. last month vs. same month last year
   - Revenue by product/service line
   - Revenue by channel/source
   - MRR/ARR if subscription-based
2. **Expense analysis**:
   - Total spend vs. budget
   - Cost breakdown by category
   - Largest month-over-month increases (flag anything >15%)
3. **Profitability**:
   - Gross margin by product
   - Net margin overall
   - Cash flow statement summary
4. **Key metrics**:
   - CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
   - LTV (Lifetime Value)
   - LTV:CAC ratio
   - Churn rate (if applicable)
   - Revenue per employee
5. **Action items**:
   - Top 3 financial wins to continue
   - Top 3 areas to optimize
   - Decisions needed before next month

Output as a template I can fill in monthly.
```

## Prompt 7: Project Brief & Scope Document

### Prompt

```
Write a project brief and scope document for [PROJECT NAME].

Project details:
- Objective: [WHAT WE'RE BUILDING/DOING]
- Why: [BUSINESS REASON/OPPORTUNITY]
- Owner: [PROJECT LEAD]
- Team: [WHO IS INVOLVED]
- Timeline: [START DATE — END DATE]
- Budget: [BUDGET]
- Success metric: [HOW WE MEASURE SUCCESS]

Document sections:
1. **Executive summary** (3 sentences): What, why, and expected outcome
2. **Background**: Context and problem statement
3. **Objectives**: 3-5 specific, measurable goals
4. **Scope**: What's IN scope and what's explicitly OUT of scope
5. **Deliverables**: List of tangible outputs with owners and due dates
6. **Timeline**: Phase breakdown with milestones
7. **Budget**: Line-item breakdown
8. **Risks**: Top 5 risks with mitigation strategies
9. **Dependencies**: What needs to happen before/during this project
10. **Approval**: Sign-off section

Keep it to 2 pages. If it's longer, the project needs to be split.
```

## Prompt 8: Performance Review — Self & Manager

### Prompt

```
Help me write performance review content.

Context:
- Person being reviewed: [NAME/ROLE]
- Review period: [TIMEFRAME]
- Key accomplishments: [LIST 3-5 WINS]
- Areas for growth: [LIST 2-3]
- Goals for next period: [WHAT'S AHEAD]

Create two versions:

**Self-review** (for the employee to submit):
- Accomplishments section: Turn each win into a STAR-format paragraph
- Growth areas: Frame constructively (not "I failed at X" but "I'm developing my ability to X")
- Goals: Specific, measurable objectives for next review period
- Support needed: What you need from your manager/team

**Manager review** (for the manager to deliver):
- Strengths assessment: Specific examples of each strength in action
- Growth feedback: Balanced, actionable, tied to specific behaviors (not personality)
- Rating guidance: Context for each rating area
- Development plan: 2-3 specific actions with timelines
- Career conversation starters: Questions to explore their growth path

Tone: Honest, specific, forward-looking. No corporate filler.
```

## Prompt 9: SWOT Analysis

### Prompt

```
Conduct a SWOT analysis for [BUSINESS NAME] in the [INDUSTRY] space.

Business context:
- What we sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Our target market: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
- Current revenue: [APPROXIMATE]
- Team size: [NUMBER]
- Years in business: [NUMBER]
- Key competitors: [LIST 2-3]
- Recent changes: [ANYTHING RELEVANT — new product, market shift, regulation, etc.]

Provide:
1. **Strengths** (5-7): Internal advantages. Be specific — not "great team" but "3 engineers with 10+ years in [technology]"
2. **Weaknesses** (5-7): Internal disadvantages. Be honest and actionable.
3. **Opportunities** (5-7): External trends, market gaps, timing advantages
4. **Threats** (5-7): External risks — competitors, regulations, market shifts

Then create:
- **SO Strategies**: How to use Strengths to capitalize on Opportunities (2-3 strategies)
- **WO Strategies**: How to overcome Weaknesses by pursuing Opportunities (2-3 strategies)
- **ST Strategies**: How to use Strengths to mitigate Threats (2-3 strategies)
- **WT Strategies**: How to minimize Weaknesses AND avoid Threats (2-3 strategies)

Prioritize: Which strategy should we execute FIRST and why?
```

## Prompt 11: Crisis Communication Plan

### Prompt

```
Create a crisis communication plan for [BUSINESS NAME].

Business context:
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Size: [TEAM SIZE]
- Public presence: [WEBSITE, SOCIAL MEDIA CHANNELS]
- Customer base: [APPROXIMATE SIZE AND TYPE]

Plan should cover:
1. **Crisis categories**: 5 most likely crisis types for my business (data breach, PR issue, service outage, legal, product failure)
2. **Response team**: Who does what (even if team is small — assign roles)
3. **Communication channels**: Priority order for each audience (customers, employees, media, partners)
4. **Template responses** for each crisis type:
   - First 1 hour: Acknowledgment statement
   - First 24 hours: Status update with facts
   - Resolution: What we did, what we learned, what changes
5. **Social media protocol**: Who posts, approval chain, what NOT to say
6. **Internal communication**: How to keep the team informed and aligned
7. **Post-crisis review**: Template for the retrospective meeting

Key rule: Speed beats perfection. Get a factual acknowledgment out FAST, then update as you know more.
```

## Prompt 12: Quarterly OKR Planning

### Prompt

```
Help me set OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) for [BUSINESS NAME] for [QUARTER/YEAR].

Context:
- Business stage: [STARTUP / GROWTH / MATURE]
- Last quarter's results: [BRIEF SUMMARY — what hit, what missed]
- Top priority this quarter: [MAIN FOCUS]
- Team size: [NUMBER]
- Revenue target: [TARGET]

Create:
- **3 Company-Level Objectives** (aspirational, qualitative)
  - For each: 3-4 Key Results (specific, measurable, time-bound)
  - Confidence level: 50% achievable = good (OKRs should stretch)

- **Department/Function OKRs** (if applicable):
  - Marketing: 1 objective, 3 key results
  - Sales: 1 objective, 3 key results
  - Product: 1 objective, 3 key results

For each Key Result:
- Metric + target number
- Current baseline
- Owner
- How it will be measured/tracked

Also provide:
- OKR scoring guide (0.0 — 1.0 scale)
- Weekly check-in template
- End-of-quarter review template
```

## Pro Tips

- **Document decisions, not just processes**: Every SOP should include WHY this process exists. When people understand the why, they make better judgment calls.
- **Automate before you hire**: If a task can be automated, automate it. If it can't, document it as an SOP. Only hire when you need human judgment.
- **Review monthly, update quarterly**: Set a calendar reminder to review all operational docs. Outdated SOPs are worse than no SOPs.
- **Keep it simple**: A 1-page SOP that people actually follow beats a 20-page doc that collects dust.
