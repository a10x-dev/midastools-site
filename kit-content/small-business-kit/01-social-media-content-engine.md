## Set Your Brand Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using any prompt below:

```
You are a social media content strategist for my small business.

Business: [BUSINESS NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Products/Services: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Target Audience: [WHO BUYS FROM YOU — age, interests, pain points]
Brand Voice: [TONE — e.g., friendly and approachable, professional, witty, luxurious]
Location: [CITY, STATE]
Website: [URL]
Key differentiator: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT FROM COMPETITORS]

Keep all content authentic, on-brand, and optimized for engagement.
```

## Instagram Posts

### Type 1: Product Showcase

```
Write an Instagram post showcasing [PRODUCT NAME].

Details:
- Key features: [LIST 2-3 FEATURES]
- Price: [PRICE]
- Who it's for: [TARGET CUSTOMER]
- What problem it solves: [PAIN POINT]

Requirements:
- Write a compelling caption (150-200 words)
- Start with a scroll-stopping first line
- Include a clear call to action
- Add 5 relevant emojis placed naturally within the text
- Suggest 20 hashtags (mix of broad, niche, and branded)
- Suggest a photo/reel concept to pair with this post
```

### Type 2: Behind the Scenes

```
Write an Instagram behind-the-scenes post for [BUSINESS NAME].

The behind-the-scenes moment: [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU'RE SHOWING — e.g., packing orders, designing a new product, team meeting, workspace tour]

Requirements:
- Write a relatable, human caption (100-150 words)
- Make followers feel like insiders
- Share one surprising or interesting detail about the process
- End with an engagement question
- Suggest 15 hashtags
- Suggest whether this works better as a photo, carousel, or reel
```

### Type 3: Tips and Value

```
Write an Instagram educational post for [BUSINESS NAME].

Topic: [TIP OR ADVICE RELATED TO YOUR INDUSTRY]
Target audience knowledge level: [BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / EXPERT]

Requirements:
- Write a caption that delivers genuine value (150-200 words)
- Start with "Most people don't know..." or a similar hook
- Break the tip into actionable steps if possible
- End with "Save this for later" or engagement CTA
- Suggest a carousel layout (what to put on each slide)
- Suggest 15 hashtags focused on education and your niche
```

### Type 4: Customer Testimonial / Social Proof

```
Write an Instagram testimonial post for [BUSINESS NAME].

Customer details:
- Name (or anonymous): [CUSTOMER NAME / "A recent customer"]
- Product/service purchased: [WHAT THEY BOUGHT]
- Result or experience: [WHAT HAPPENED — specific outcome if possible]
- Quote (if available): [DIRECT QUOTE OR "Generate a realistic one"]

Requirements:
- Write a caption that tells the customer's story (120-180 words)
- Frame it as a transformation or outcome
- Include the direct quote formatted with quotation marks
- Add a CTA encouraging others to share their experience or try the product
- Suggest 10 hashtags
```

### Type 5: Promotion / Sale

```
Write an Instagram promotional post for [BUSINESS NAME].

Promotion details:
- Offer: [DISCOUNT, DEAL, OR SPECIAL — e.g., 20% off, BOGO, free shipping]
- Valid dates: [START DATE] to [END DATE]
- Promo code (if any): [CODE]
- Products included: [WHICH PRODUCTS OR "everything"]
- Reason for sale: [SEASONAL, HOLIDAY, MILESTONE, FLASH SALE]

Requirements:
- Write an urgent, exciting caption (100-150 words)
- Create a strong hook in the first line
- Clearly state the offer, dates, and how to redeem
- Add urgency without being pushy
- Include a direct CTA (link in bio, use code, etc.)
- Suggest 15 hashtags
- Suggest a visual concept (graphic, product flat lay, countdown, etc.)
```

## Facebook Posts + Ad Copy

### Organic Facebook Post

```
Write a Facebook post for [BUSINESS NAME] about [TOPIC].

Post goal: [ENGAGEMENT / TRAFFIC / AWARENESS / SALES]
Key message: [WHAT YOU WANT TO COMMUNICATE]

Requirements:
- Write 80-150 words (Facebook sweet spot for engagement)
- Use a conversational, community-building tone
- Include a question or CTA to encourage comments
- No hashtags (or 1-3 max — Facebook doesn't rely on them)
- Suggest whether to include a photo, link, or video
```

### Facebook Ad Copy — Lead Generation

```
Write Facebook ad copy for [BUSINESS NAME] targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE].

Offer: [WHAT YOU'RE OFFERING — free guide, discount, consultation, etc.]
Goal: [LEADS / SALES / WEBSITE TRAFFIC]
Landing page URL: [URL]

Write 3 variations:
1. Short version (40-80 words) — punchy and direct
2. Medium version (80-150 words) — story-driven
3. Long version (150-250 words) — problem-agitate-solution format

For each version include:
- Headline (max 40 characters)
- Primary text (the ad copy)
- Description (max 30 characters)
- CTA button suggestion (Shop Now, Learn More, Sign Up, Get Offer, etc.)
```

### Facebook Ad Copy — Retargeting

```
Write a Facebook retargeting ad for [BUSINESS NAME].

Targeting: People who visited [SPECIFIC PAGE OR PRODUCT] but didn't buy.
Product: [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME]
Price: [PRICE]
Unique selling point: [WHY THEY SHOULD COME BACK]

Requirements:
- Acknowledge they were browsing without being creepy
- Address likely objections (price, timing, trust)
- Include social proof or a guarantee
- Add urgency or a limited-time sweetener
- Write 2 versions: casual and professional
```

## LinkedIn Business Updates

### Company Milestone / Achievement

```
Write a LinkedIn post for [BUSINESS NAME] announcing [MILESTONE].

Details:
- What happened: [SPECIFIC ACHIEVEMENT — revenue goal, hire, launch, award, anniversary]
- Why it matters: [SIGNIFICANCE]
- Who helped: [TEAM, CUSTOMERS, PARTNERS]

Requirements:
- Professional but personable tone
- 150-250 words
- Start with a bold or surprising statement
- Include gratitude and forward-looking vision
- End with a question or invitation to connect
- No hashtags in the body; add 3-5 at the end
```

### Thought Leadership / Industry Insight

```
Write a LinkedIn thought leadership post from [YOUR NAME], [YOUR TITLE] at [BUSINESS NAME].

Topic: [INDUSTRY TREND, OBSERVATION, OR LESSON LEARNED]
Your perspective: [YOUR UNIQUE TAKE OR EXPERIENCE]

Requirements:
- Write 200-300 words
- Open with a contrarian or surprising statement
- Share a specific example or anecdote from your experience
- Provide actionable insight readers can use
- End with a question to spark discussion
- Professional tone with personality
- Add 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end
```

### Hiring / Team Post

```
Write a LinkedIn post for [BUSINESS NAME] announcing we're hiring.

Role: [JOB TITLE]
Location: [REMOTE / CITY / HYBRID]
What makes this role exciting: [WHY SOMEONE WOULD WANT THIS JOB]
Team culture highlight: [ONE THING ABOUT YOUR CULTURE]

Requirements:
- Enthusiastic but genuine tone
- 100-200 words
- Sell the opportunity without overselling
- Include a clear CTA (apply link, DM, etc.)
- Ask your network to share
```

## Google Business Profile Posts

### What's New / Update Post

```
Write a Google Business Profile post for [BUSINESS NAME].

Update: [NEW PRODUCT, SERVICE, HOURS, EVENT, OR NEWS]
CTA: [CALL NOW / LEARN MORE / BOOK ONLINE / ORDER NOW / VISIT]
Link: [URL if applicable]

Requirements:
- 100-150 words (Google truncates longer posts)
- Clear and informative — people searching Google want quick answers
- Include one CTA button suggestion
- Mention your location or service area
- No hashtags
```

### Event Post

```
Write a Google Business Profile event post for [BUSINESS NAME].

Event: [EVENT NAME]
Date: [DATE]
Time: [TIME]
Location: [ADDRESS OR "Online"]
Description: [WHAT HAPPENS AT THE EVENT]
Registration link: [URL if applicable]

Requirements:
- 80-120 words
- Highlight what attendees will get out of it
- Include all logistics clearly
- CTA to register or attend
```

### Offer Post

```
Write a Google Business Profile offer post for [BUSINESS NAME].

Offer: [DEAL DETAILS]
Valid: [START DATE] to [END DATE]
Terms: [ANY CONDITIONS — minimum purchase, exclusions, etc.]
Redemption: [HOW TO CLAIM — in-store, online code, mention this post, etc.]

Requirements:
- 80-120 words
- Lead with the value proposition
- Make terms clear and simple
- Strong CTA
```

## 30-Day Content Calendar

```
Create a 30-day social media content calendar for [BUSINESS NAME].

Business details:
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Products/Services: [WHAT YOU SELL]
- Target audience: [WHO YOU SERVE]
- Upcoming events or promotions: [ANY PLANNED SALES, LAUNCHES, HOLIDAYS]
- Posting frequency: [e.g., Instagram 5x/week, Facebook 3x/week, LinkedIn 2x/week]

For each day, provide:
1. Platform(s) to post on
2. Content type (product, behind-scenes, tips, testimonial, promotion, engagement)
3. Topic/theme in one sentence
4. Caption draft (50-100 words)
5. Suggested visual (photo, carousel, reel, graphic)
6. Best posting time for [YOUR TIME ZONE]

Mix content types so no more than 20% is promotional.
Include engagement posts (polls, questions, this-or-that) at least twice per week.
Align with any upcoming holidays or industry events in [MONTH, YEAR].
```

### Weekly Theme Generator

```
Generate 4 weekly themes for [BUSINESS NAME] social media in [MONTH].

For each week, provide:
- Theme name
- 3 post ideas per platform (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn)
- One "hero" content piece (reel, carousel, or article) to anchor the week
- A behind-the-scenes moment to capture
- An engagement prompt (poll, question, or challenge)

Themes should balance education, entertainment, promotion, and community building.
```

## Platform Formatting Guidelines

### Instagram
- **Caption length:** 150-2,200 characters. Sweet spot: 150-300 for feed, shorter for Reels.
- **Hashtags:** Up to 30 allowed, 15-20 recommended. Mix sizes (large 1M+, medium 100K-1M, small under 100K).
- **Line breaks:** Use them generously. Walls of text kill engagement.
- **First line:** Must hook. It's the only line visible before "...more."
- **Reels captions:** Keep under 100 words. The video does the heavy lifting.
- **Stories:** Use polls, questions, and sliders for engagement.

### Facebook
- **Post length:** 40-80 words gets highest engagement. Long posts can work if the hook is strong.
- **Hashtags:** 0-3 maximum. Facebook's algorithm doesn't prioritize them.
- **Links:** Facebook deprioritizes posts with links. Put links in comments or use link post format.
- **Video:** Native video far outperforms YouTube links.
- **Best for:** Community building, events, local business promotion.

### LinkedIn
- **Post length:** 150-300 words performs best. Up to 3,000 characters allowed.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 at the end of the post.
- **Formatting:** Use line breaks, short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each), and occasional bold text.
- **Hook:** First 2-3 lines are visible before "see more." Make them count.
- **Best for:** Thought leadership, hiring, B2B, partnerships.
- **Avoid:** Overly salesy content. LinkedIn rewards value and conversation.

### Google Business Profile
- **Post length:** 100-150 words. Google truncates at about 100 words on mobile.
- **Images:** Always include one. Posts with images get more clicks.
- **CTA:** Always include a CTA button (Call, Learn More, Book, Order, Visit).
- **Frequency:** Post at least once per week to stay active in local search.
- **Best for:** Local SEO, offers, events, updates.
