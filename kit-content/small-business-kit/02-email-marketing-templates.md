## Set Your Email Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using email prompts:

```
You are an email marketing specialist for my small business.

Business: [BUSINESS NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Products/Services: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Target Audience: [WHO BUYS FROM YOU]
Brand Voice: [TONE — friendly, professional, witty, warm, etc.]
Website: [URL]
Email platform: [Mailchimp / ConvertKit / Klaviyo / Other]
Average order value: [AMOUNT]
Main competitor: [COMPETITOR NAME]

Write emails that are concise, scannable, and drive action.
Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max).
Always include a clear, single call to action per email.
```

## Welcome Sequence

### Email 1: The Warm Welcome (Send Immediately)

```
Write a welcome email for [BUSINESS NAME] sent immediately after someone subscribes.

Subscriber joined via: [LEAD MAGNET / NEWSLETTER SIGNUP / PURCHASE / OTHER]
What they were promised: [FREE GUIDE / DISCOUNT CODE / UPDATES / ETC.]

Requirements:
- Subject line: warm, personal (not generic)
- 150-200 words
- Thank them for joining
- Deliver whatever was promised (link, code, etc.)
- Set expectations: what emails they'll get and how often
- Brief introduction to who you are and why you exist
- One CTA: [DOWNLOAD GUIDE / SHOP WITH CODE / VISIT WEBSITE]
- P.S. line with a personal touch
```

### Email 2: Your Story (Send Day 2)

```
Write the second welcome sequence email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Share the brand story and build connection.

Include:
- The origin story: why you started [BUSINESS NAME]
- The problem you saw in the market
- How your product/service solves it differently
- A relatable, human moment (struggle, aha moment, early customer story)

Requirements:
- Subject line that creates curiosity
- 200-250 words
- Conversational, not corporate
- End with a question to encourage replies
- No hard sell — this is relationship building
```

### Email 3: Best Sellers / Most Popular (Send Day 4)

```
Write the third welcome sequence email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Showcase top products/services and build desire.

Products to feature:
1. [PRODUCT 1 — name, brief description, price]
2. [PRODUCT 2 — name, brief description, price]
3. [PRODUCT 3 — name, brief description, price]

Requirements:
- Subject line highlighting popularity or customer favorites
- 150-200 words
- Brief intro about why these are customer favorites
- Each product gets 2-3 sentences + link
- Include one piece of social proof (review quote, number sold, rating)
- CTA: Shop our best sellers
```

### Email 4: Social Proof / Testimonials (Send Day 7)

```
Write the fourth welcome sequence email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Build trust through customer stories and reviews.

Testimonials to include (provide what you have):
1. [CUSTOMER NAME / INITIAL]: "[QUOTE]" — [CONTEXT]
2. [CUSTOMER NAME / INITIAL]: "[QUOTE]" — [CONTEXT]
3. [CUSTOMER NAME / INITIAL]: "[QUOTE]" — [CONTEXT]

(If you don't have real testimonials yet, write: "Generate 3 realistic but clearly marked as example testimonials for a [INDUSTRY] business.")

Requirements:
- Subject line leveraging social proof
- 150-200 words
- Brief intro about why customers love you
- Feature 2-3 testimonials with names and context
- Include any notable numbers (customers served, 5-star reviews, years in business)
- CTA: Join them / See what others are saying / Shop now
```

### Email 5: Special Offer (Send Day 10)

```
Write the fifth and final welcome sequence email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Convert subscribers into customers with a limited offer.

Offer: [DISCOUNT / FREE SHIPPING / BONUS / BUNDLE DEAL]
Expiration: [TIMEFRAME — e.g., 48 hours, end of week]
Code (if applicable): [PROMO CODE]

Requirements:
- Subject line with urgency and clear value
- 120-180 words
- Reference the journey: "Over the past week, you've learned about..."
- Present the exclusive offer as a thank-you for joining
- Clear terms and how to redeem
- Strong urgency without being manipulative
- Single, prominent CTA
- P.S. with deadline reminder
```

## Promotional Campaign

### Email 1: Announcement (Day 1)

```
Write a promotional announcement email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Promotion: [DETAILS — what, discount amount, dates]
Reason: [WHY — seasonal, holiday, anniversary, clearance, new launch]
Featured products: [LIST 2-4 PRODUCTS OR "entire store"]

Requirements:
- Subject line that communicates the offer in under 50 characters
- Preview text that complements the subject (not repeats it)
- 150-200 words
- Lead with the value (what they save or get)
- Briefly explain what's included
- Social proof element (if available)
- Clear CTA with the offer
- Mention the end date
```

### Email 2: Reminder + Spotlight (Day 3)

```
Write the reminder email for [BUSINESS NAME]'s promotion.

Promotion: [SAME DETAILS AS EMAIL 1]
Days remaining: [NUMBER]
Spotlight product: [ONE PRODUCT TO FEATURE IN DEPTH]
Spotlight details: [FEATURES, BENEFITS, REVIEW, USE CASE]

Requirements:
- Subject line — different angle than Email 1 (focus on the product or social proof)
- 150-200 words
- Don't just repeat Email 1 — add new value
- Deep dive on one product with a mini-story or use case
- Include a customer review or testimonial for that product
- Restate the offer
- CTA to shop
```

### Email 3: Last Chance (Final Day)

```
Write the final-day urgency email for [BUSINESS NAME]'s promotion.

Promotion: [SAME DETAILS]
Ends: [TONIGHT / IN X HOURS / SPECIFIC TIME AND TIMEZONE]

Requirements:
- Subject line with clear urgency (last chance, ends tonight, final hours)
- 100-150 words — short and punchy
- No new information — just urgency and CTA
- Remind them of the best reasons to buy
- One testimonial or stat
- Bold or prominent CTA
- P.S. confirming exact cutoff time
```

## Re-engagement Campaign

### Email 1: The Check-In (Day 1)

```
Write a re-engagement email for [BUSINESS NAME] subscribers who haven't opened emails in [TIMEFRAME — e.g., 90 days].

Requirements:
- Subject line that breaks the pattern (funny, personal, or intriguing)
- 100-150 words
- Acknowledge the silence: "It's been a while..."
- No guilt — keep it light and warm
- Remind them of the value they signed up for
- Ask if they still want to hear from you
- CTA: "Yes, keep me on the list" (link to preference center or simple confirmation)
```

### Email 2: Win-Back Offer (Day 4)

```
Write a win-back email for [BUSINESS NAME] targeting inactive subscribers.

Incentive: [SPECIAL OFFER — exclusive discount, free gift, etc.]
This offer is: [EXCLUSIVE TO INACTIVE SUBSCRIBERS / AVAILABLE TO ALL]

Requirements:
- Subject line highlighting the exclusive offer
- 120-180 words
- Lead with "We miss you" or similar sentiment (without being desperate)
- Present the offer as a "welcome back" gesture
- Share one new thing that's happened since they last engaged
- CTA to redeem the offer
- Mention this is a limited-time incentive
```

### Email 3: Last Chance / Unsubscribe Warning (Day 8)

```
Write the final re-engagement email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Give inactive subscribers one last chance before removing them.

Requirements:
- Subject line that's direct: "Should we part ways?" or "Last email from us?"
- 80-120 words
- Be honest and respectful
- Explain you're cleaning your list
- Give them one click to stay subscribed
- Make it clear: no action = they'll be removed
- Keep the tone warm, not threatening
- Brief reminder of what they'll miss
```

## Cart Abandonment Sequence

### Email 1: Gentle Reminder (Send 1 Hour After Abandonment)

```
Write a cart abandonment email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: First touchpoint — simple reminder, no pressure.

Requirements:
- Subject line referencing their cart (not generic)
- 80-120 words
- "You left something behind" approach
- Show what's in their cart: [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER]
- One-click link back to their cart
- Helpful tone: "Still deciding? Here if you have questions."
- No discount yet
```

### Email 2: Address Objections (Send 24 Hours After)

```
Write the second cart abandonment email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Overcome objections and build confidence.

Common objections for your business: [LIST 2-3 — e.g., price, shipping time, quality concerns, not sure about sizing]

Requirements:
- Subject line that addresses a concern or adds value
- 120-180 words
- Acknowledge that deciding takes time
- Address the top 2 objections directly
- Include social proof (review, rating, guarantee)
- Mention your return policy, guarantee, or shipping details
- CTA back to cart
- Still no discount
```

### Email 3: Incentive (Send 48-72 Hours After)

```
Write the final cart abandonment email for [BUSINESS NAME].

Purpose: Convert with a small incentive.

Incentive: [10% OFF / FREE SHIPPING / BONUS GIFT / NONE — just urgency]
Expiration: [24-48 HOURS]

Requirements:
- Subject line with the incentive front and center
- 100-150 words
- Present the incentive as exclusive: "Because you showed interest..."
- Show cart contents again
- Include urgency: incentive or cart expires soon
- One strong CTA
- P.S. offering help: "Reply if you have questions — a real human will answer."
```

## Newsletter Template

### Weekly/Monthly Newsletter

```
Write a newsletter email for [BUSINESS NAME] — [MONTH/WEEK] edition.

Sections to include:
1. Personal note / update: [BRIEF TOPIC — what's happening in your business]
2. Featured content: [BLOG POST, VIDEO, OR TIP TO SHARE]
3. Product spotlight: [PRODUCT/SERVICE TO HIGHLIGHT]
4. Quick tip: [ONE ACTIONABLE TIP FOR YOUR AUDIENCE]
5. Community highlight: [CUSTOMER STORY, REVIEW, OR UGC]
6. What's coming: [UPCOMING EVENT, LAUNCH, OR PROMOTION]

Requirements:
- Subject line that teases the most interesting section
- 300-400 words total
- Scannable format with headers and short paragraphs
- Each section gets 2-4 sentences
- One primary CTA + secondary links for each section
- Personal, conversational tone — like a letter from a friend
- Sign off with your name, not just the business name
```

### Newsletter Subject Line

```
Generate 10 subject lines for [BUSINESS NAME]'s [MONTH] newsletter.

The most interesting thing in this issue: [HIGHLIGHT]
Secondary hook: [SECOND INTERESTING THING]

Requirements:
- Mix of styles: curiosity, value, personal, newsy
- Under 50 characters each
- Test one emoji version and one without for each style
- Rank them from most to least likely to get opens
```

## Subject Line Generator

### 20+ Subject Line Formulas

Use this prompt to generate subject lines for any email:

```
Generate 5 email subject lines for each of the following formulas. The email is about [TOPIC] for [BUSINESS NAME] targeting [AUDIENCE].

Formulas:

1. **The Question:** Ask something they can't ignore.
   Example: "Are you making this [INDUSTRY] mistake?"

2. **The Number:** Specific numbers build credibility.
   Example: "7 ways to [BENEFIT] this weekend"

3. **The How-To:** Promise a transformation.
   Example: "How to [DESIRED OUTCOME] without [PAIN POINT]"

4. **The Urgency:** Time-sensitive without being spammy.
   Example: "Ends tonight: [OFFER]"

5. **The Personal:** Use their name or "you."
   Example: "I made this for you, [NAME]"

6. **The Curiosity Gap:** Tease without revealing.
   Example: "The one thing I wish I knew about [TOPIC]"

7. **The Newsjack:** Tie into something current.
   Example: "[TRENDING TOPIC] + what it means for your [BUSINESS]"

8. **The Story Tease:** Start a story they need to finish.
   Example: "Last Tuesday, a customer told me something unexpected..."

9. **The Contrarian:** Challenge conventional wisdom.
   Example: "Stop doing [COMMON ADVICE] — here's why"

10. **The Social Proof:** Let others sell for you.
    Example: "Why 500+ [TARGET] switched to [PRODUCT]"

11. **The Direct Offer:** No games, just value.
    Example: "25% off everything — today only"

12. **The Exclusive:** Make them feel special.
    Example: "Early access: [NEW PRODUCT] — subscribers only"

13. **The Listicle:** People love lists.
    Example: "3 [THINGS] every [TARGET] needs this [SEASON]"

14. **The Before/After:** Promise transformation.
    Example: "From [PAIN] to [GAIN] in [TIMEFRAME]"

15. **The Challenge:** Dare them.
    Example: "I dare you to try [ACTION] for 7 days"

16. **The Confession:** Vulnerability builds trust.
    Example: "I almost gave up on [BUSINESS] — here's what changed"

17. **The Preview:** Tease what's coming.
    Example: "Sneak peek: something new is dropping [DATE]"

18. **The Re-engagement:** Win them back.
    Example: "We miss you — and we brought a gift"

19. **The Thank You:** Gratitude opens emails.
    Example: "Thank you — this means everything to us"

20. **The One-Word Wonder:** Bold simplicity.
    Example: "Wow." / "Finally." / "Oops." / "Ready?"

21. **The Emoji Accent:** One emoji adds personality.
    Example: "Your weekend plans just got better [relevant emoji]"

22. **The FOMO:** Fear of missing out.
    Example: "[X] people already grabbed this — have you?"

For each formula, generate 5 subject lines specific to my business.
Keep all subject lines under 50 characters.
Rate each on a scale of 1-10 for expected open rate.
```

### Subject Line Testing Prompt

```
I'm sending an email about [TOPIC] to [AUDIENCE SIZE] subscribers for [BUSINESS NAME].

Here are my 3 subject line options:
1. [OPTION A]
2. [OPTION B]
3. [OPTION C]

For each option, evaluate:
- Clarity: Is the value obvious?
- Curiosity: Does it compel opens?
- Length: Is it mobile-friendly (under 40 chars)?
- Spam risk: Any trigger words that might hurt deliverability?
- Audience fit: Does it match [TARGET AUDIENCE]?

Rank them and suggest 2 improved alternatives based on what's strongest about each.
```

### Email Preview Text Generator

```
Write preview text (preheader) for these email subject lines for [BUSINESS NAME]:

1. Subject: "[SUBJECT LINE 1]"
2. Subject: "[SUBJECT LINE 2]"
3. Subject: "[SUBJECT LINE 3]"

Requirements:
- 40-90 characters each
- Must complement the subject, not repeat it
- Add information or curiosity the subject line doesn't cover
- Together, subject + preview should tell a complete micro-story
```
