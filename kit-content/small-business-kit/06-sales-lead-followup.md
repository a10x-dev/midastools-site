## Set Your Sales Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using sales prompts:

```
You are a sales and outreach specialist for my small business.

Business: [BUSINESS NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Products/Services: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Target customer: [WHO YOU SELL TO — be specific: industry, company size, role, pain points]
Average deal size: [AMOUNT]
Sales cycle length: [SHORT — days / MEDIUM — weeks / LONG — months]
Key differentiator: [WHY SOMEONE SHOULD CHOOSE YOU OVER COMPETITORS]
Website: [URL]
Your name and title: [YOUR NAME, TITLE]

Tone guidelines:
- Professional but conversational — never salesy or pushy
- Focus on the prospect's problems, not your features
- Be concise — busy people scan, they don't read essays
- Always provide value, even if they don't buy
```

## Cold Outreach

### Cold Email — Problem-Focused

```
Write a cold outreach email from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] to a potential customer.

Prospect details:
- Name: [PROSPECT NAME]
- Title: [THEIR TITLE]
- Company: [THEIR COMPANY]
- Industry: [THEIR INDUSTRY]
- Known pain point: [WHAT PROBLEM THEY LIKELY HAVE THAT YOU SOLVE]
- How you found them: [LINKEDIN / REFERRAL / EVENT / THEIR WEBSITE / SOCIAL MEDIA]

Requirements:
- Subject line: under 40 characters, curiosity-driven, no spam words
- 80-120 words total (cold emails must be short)
- First line: personalized reference to them (not about you)
- One sentence about the problem you solve
- One sentence about a result you've achieved (specific number if possible)
- Clear, low-friction CTA: "Worth a 15-minute call?" or "Would it help if I sent [RESOURCE]?"
- No attachments, no links in first email (improves deliverability)
- P.S. line optional — adds personality
- Write 3 variations with different hooks
```

### Cold Email — Referral / Mutual Connection

```
Write a cold email leveraging a mutual connection for [BUSINESS NAME].

Details:
- Prospect: [NAME] at [COMPANY]
- Mutual connection: [WHO CONNECTED YOU AND HOW — e.g., "Sarah from the Chamber of Commerce suggested I reach out"]
- What you offer: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF RELEVANT SERVICE/PRODUCT]
- Why this prospect specifically: [WHAT MADE YOU THINK THEY'D BE A FIT]

Requirements:
- Subject line mentioning the mutual connection or their company
- 80-120 words
- Lead with the connection: "Sarah mentioned you might be interested in..."
- One sentence about what you do and how it relates to their needs
- Specific, low-pressure CTA
- Warm and genuine, not salesy
```

### Cold DM — LinkedIn

```
Write a LinkedIn connection request message and follow-up DM for [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME].

Prospect: [NAME], [TITLE] at [COMPANY]
Reason for connecting: [WHY — shared industry, mutual connection, their content, etc.]
What you sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
Goal: [CALL / DEMO / SEND RESOURCE / START CONVERSATION]

Requirements:

**Connection request note (under 300 characters — LinkedIn limit):**
- Personal, not pitchy
- Reference something specific about them
- No selling in the connection request

**Follow-up DM (if they accept — send 1-2 days later):**
- 60-100 words
- Thank them for connecting
- Add value: share a relevant insight, article, or observation
- Soft ask: "Would love to hear your thoughts on [TOPIC]" or "We help companies like [COMPANY] with [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] — curious if that's on your radar?"
- No hard pitch
```

### Cold DM — Instagram / Twitter / X

```
Write a cold DM for [PLATFORM] from [BUSINESS NAME] to a potential customer or collaborator.

Who: [NAME / HANDLE]
Context: [WHY YOU'RE REACHING OUT — they posted about X, they're in your target market, etc.]
Goal: [WHAT YOU WANT — sale, collaboration, partnership]

Requirements:
- 40-80 words (social DMs must be ultra-short)
- Feel like a real person, not a brand
- Reference something specific from their profile or recent post
- One clear value proposition
- One easy CTA (reply, check out a link, hop on a call)
- No "Hey [NAME]! I love your content!" (everyone says this)
- Write 2 versions: casual and professional
```

## Follow-Up Sequences

### 5-Touch Follow-Up Sequence (After Cold Email)

```
Write a 5-email follow-up sequence for [BUSINESS NAME] after an unanswered cold email.

Original email topic: [WHAT THE FIRST EMAIL WAS ABOUT]
Prospect: [NAME] at [COMPANY]
What you're selling: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
Key value proposition: [ONE-SENTENCE BENEFIT]

Write 5 follow-up emails with this structure:

**Follow-Up 1 (Day 3) — The Gentle Bump:**
- 30-50 words
- "Just floating this to the top of your inbox"
- Restate the CTA briefly
- No new information

**Follow-Up 2 (Day 7) — New Value:**
- 60-80 words
- Share a relevant insight, stat, or case study they'd find useful
- Reframe the value proposition from a different angle
- Soft CTA

**Follow-Up 3 (Day 14) — Social Proof:**
- 60-80 words
- Share a specific result from a similar customer/client
- "We helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] achieve [RESULT]"
- Ask if they'd like to see how

**Follow-Up 4 (Day 21) — The Question:**
- 30-50 words
- Ask a direct, relevant question about their business
- Goal: get ANY reply, even a "not interested"
- "Quick question: are you currently [DOING THE THING YOUR PRODUCT HELPS WITH]?"

**Follow-Up 5 (Day 30) — The Breakup:**
- 40-60 words
- "I don't want to keep filling your inbox"
- Give them an easy out: "If the timing isn't right, no hard feelings"
- Leave the door open: "If things change, I'm here"
- Surprisingly high response rate — people reply to breakup emails

For each email:
- Write the subject line (can be a reply to the original or new)
- Keep it short and scannable
- Never be passive-aggressive or guilt-trippy
```

### Follow-Up After Meeting / Call

```
Write a follow-up email from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] after a meeting or call with a prospect.

Meeting details:
- Prospect: [NAME] at [COMPANY]
- Meeting type: [CALL / IN-PERSON / ZOOM]
- Date: [DATE]
- Key topics discussed: [LIST 2-3 MAIN POINTS]
- Their main concern or need: [WHAT THEY SAID THEY NEED]
- Next steps discussed: [WHAT WAS AGREED ON]
- Anything you promised to send: [RESOURCES, PROPOSAL, INFO]

Requirements:
- Send within 2 hours of the meeting
- Subject: Re: [MEETING TOPIC] or "Great talking today, [NAME]"
- 100-150 words
- Thank them for their time
- Recap 2-3 key takeaways (shows you listened)
- Confirm next steps with specific dates
- Attach or link anything you promised
- Clear CTA for the next action
- Professional but warm
```

### Follow-Up After No-Show

```
Write a follow-up email from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] after a prospect missed a scheduled meeting.

Prospect: [NAME]
Scheduled meeting: [DATE AND TIME]
Meeting type: [CALL / ZOOM / IN-PERSON]

Requirements:
- Subject line: friendly, not passive-aggressive
- 50-80 words
- Zero guilt or frustration — things happen
- Assume they got busy, not that they're avoiding you
- Offer to reschedule: provide 2-3 alternative times
- Include your calendar link if you have one: [LINK]
- Option: "If priorities have shifted and this isn't a fit right now, totally understand"
- Write 2 versions: same-day follow-up and next-day follow-up
```

## Proposal and Quote Follow-Up

### Follow-Up After Sending Proposal

```
Write a follow-up email sequence for [BUSINESS NAME] after sending a proposal or quote.

Prospect: [NAME] at [COMPANY]
Proposal for: [WHAT — project, service, product package]
Proposal amount: [AMOUNT]
Sent on: [DATE]
Proposal expiration: [DATE IF APPLICABLE]

**Email 1 (Day 2) — Confirmation:**
- 40-60 words
- Confirm they received it
- Ask if they have any questions
- Offer to walk through it on a call

**Email 2 (Day 5) — Add Context:**
- 60-80 words
- Share something that strengthens your proposal (relevant case study, additional benefit, market context)
- Gently ask about their timeline for making a decision

**Email 3 (Day 10) — Direct Ask:**
- 40-60 words
- "Where are you in the decision-making process?"
- Offer to adjust the proposal if needed
- Restate that you're flexible and want to find the right fit

**Email 4 (Day 14+) — Last Touch:**
- 40-60 words
- If proposal has an expiration, mention it
- "I'd hate for you to miss [SPECIFIC BENEFIT] — this proposal is valid until [DATE]"
- Leave the door open regardless
```

### Handling "Your Price Is Too High"

```
Write a response from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] to a prospect who says the price is too high.

Prospect: [NAME]
What you quoted: [PRODUCT/SERVICE AND PRICE]
Their objection: [WHAT THEY SAID — "too expensive," "over budget," "competitor is cheaper," etc.]

Requirements:
- Acknowledge their concern without immediately discounting
- Reframe the value: what they GET, not what they PAY
- Use comparison: cost per day, cost vs. the problem it solves, ROI
- If applicable, offer alternatives:
  - Smaller scope or phase 1 approach
  - Payment plan
  - Adjusted package
- If a competitor is cheaper, differentiate without badmouthing
- 100-150 words
- Confident but not arrogant
- Never be defensive about pricing
```

### Handling "We Went with Someone Else"

```
Write a response from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] to a prospect who chose a competitor.

Prospect: [NAME]
What they chose: [COMPETITOR IF KNOWN / "another provider"]
Your offering: [WHAT YOU PROPOSED]

Requirements:
- Gracious and professional — no sour grapes
- Thank them for considering you
- Congratulate them on making a decision (genuinely)
- Ask for brief feedback: "If you have 30 seconds, I'd love to know what tipped the decision — it helps us improve"
- Leave the door open: "If things change or you need anything in the future, I'm here"
- 60-80 words
- Plant a seed for future business without being clingy
```

## Win-Back Campaigns

### Win-Back Email — Past Customers

```
Write a win-back email for [BUSINESS NAME] targeting customers who haven't purchased in [TIMEFRAME — e.g., 6 months, 1 year].

Customer segment: [ALL PAST CUSTOMERS / SPECIFIC SEGMENT]
Last purchase: [AVERAGE TIME SINCE LAST ORDER]
Incentive: [DISCOUNT / FREE SHIPPING / EXCLUSIVE PRODUCT / NONE — just reconnect]

Requirements:
- Subject line that stands out from regular marketing emails
- 80-120 words
- Acknowledge the gap: "It's been a while..."
- Remind them of the value you provide (don't assume they remember)
- Share what's new since they last purchased (new products, improvements, etc.)
- Include incentive if applicable
- CTA to return
- Warm, personal tone — from a person, not "the brand"
```

### Win-Back Sequence (3 Emails)

```
Write a 3-email win-back sequence for [BUSINESS NAME].

Audience: Past customers who haven't bought in [TIMEFRAME]
What's changed since they left: [NEW PRODUCTS / IMPROVEMENTS / NOTHING SPECIFIC]
Budget for incentive: [PERCENTAGE OFF / FREE ITEM / NONE]

**Email 1 (Day 1) — "We Miss You":**
- 80-100 words
- Personal, warm reconnection
- Highlight 1-2 new things since they last purchased
- No hard sell, no discount yet

**Email 2 (Day 4) — "Here's What You're Missing":**
- 80-120 words
- Showcase best-sellers or new arrivals
- Include a customer review or testimonial
- Mention the incentive: "As a thank-you for being an early supporter..."
- CTA with the offer

**Email 3 (Day 8) — "Last Chance":**
- 60-80 words
- Final reminder about the offer
- Urgency: "This expires [DATE]"
- One-line testimonial
- Clear CTA
- P.S.: "If you're no longer interested, no hard feelings. You can unsubscribe below."
```

## Referral Request Templates

### Asking Happy Customers for Referrals

```
Write a referral request email from [BUSINESS NAME] to a satisfied customer.

Customer: [NAME]
What they bought: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
How long ago: [TIMEFRAME]
Known satisfaction: [THEY LEFT A POSITIVE REVIEW / SENT A THANK-YOU / REPEAT BUYER / EXPRESSED SATISFACTION]

Referral incentive (if any): [DISCOUNT / GIFT CARD / FREE PRODUCT / MUTUAL BENEFIT / NONE]

Requirements:
- Subject line that doesn't scream "give us referrals"
- 80-120 words
- Open by referencing their positive experience specifically
- Explain that you're growing through word of mouth
- Ask clearly: "Do you know anyone who would benefit from [SPECIFIC OUTCOME]?"
- If there's an incentive, present it naturally (not transactionally)
- Make it easy: provide a referral link, or say "just CC me on an email introduction"
- No pressure — "Only if it feels natural"
- Grateful tone
```

### Post-Purchase Referral Request (Automated)

```
Write a post-purchase referral request email for [BUSINESS NAME] to be sent automatically [TIMEFRAME] after purchase.

Product: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
Timing: [7 DAYS / 14 DAYS / 30 DAYS AFTER PURCHASE]
Referral program: [DESCRIBE — e.g., "Give $10, get $10" / "Refer a friend, get a free product" / NO FORMAL PROGRAM]

Requirements:
- Subject line: casual, not "REFER A FRIEND!"
- 60-100 words
- Quick check-in: "How are you enjoying [PRODUCT]?"
- Segue naturally into the referral ask
- If there's a program: explain it simply with one CTA
- If no program: "If you know someone who'd love this, we'd be so grateful if you sent them our way"
- Include shareable link or easy referral mechanism
```

### Asking for a Testimonial (Leads to Referrals)

```
Write a testimonial request email from [BUSINESS NAME].

Customer: [NAME]
Product/service: [WHAT THEY BOUGHT]
Result they achieved: [WHAT OUTCOME OR EXPERIENCE — if known]

Requirements:
- Subject line: "Quick favor?" or "[NAME], would you mind?"
- 60-80 words
- Express that their experience matters to you
- Ask for a brief testimonial (2-3 sentences)
- Make it easy: provide questions they can answer:
  1. "What was your experience like?"
  2. "What result did you get?"
  3. "Would you recommend us? Why?"
- Offer to write a draft based on their experience that they can approve (reduces effort)
- Mention where it will be used (website, social media, Google review)
- Thank them regardless
```

## Partnership Outreach

### Strategic Partnership Proposal

```
Write a partnership outreach email from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] to a potential partner.

Partner: [COMPANY/PERSON NAME]
What they do: [THEIR BUSINESS]
Why they're a good partner: [COMPLEMENTARY AUDIENCE, SHARED VALUES, MUTUAL BENEFIT]
Partnership idea: [WHAT YOU'RE PROPOSING — co-marketing, bundle, affiliate, event, content collaboration]
What's in it for them: [SPECIFIC BENEFIT TO THE PARTNER]
What's in it for you: [YOUR BENEFIT]

Requirements:
- Subject line: clear and intriguing, not vague
- 120-180 words
- Open with a genuine compliment about their work (specific, not generic)
- Briefly introduce yourself and your business
- Present the partnership idea in 2-3 sentences
- Clearly articulate the mutual benefit
- Low-friction CTA: "Would a 15-minute call make sense to explore this?"
- Not desperate — you're proposing a mutual win, not asking for a favor
- Professional but warm
```

### Affiliate / Commission Partnership

```
Write an affiliate partnership proposal from [BUSINESS NAME].

Prospect: [INFLUENCER / BLOGGER / COMPLEMENTARY BUSINESS — name and context]
Your product: [PRODUCT AND PRICE]
Proposed commission: [PERCENTAGE OR FLAT FEE]
How it works: [AFFILIATE LINK / DISCOUNT CODE / REVENUE SHARE]
Why they're a fit: [THEIR AUDIENCE OVERLAPS WITH YOUR CUSTOMERS]

Requirements:
- Subject line referencing a mutual benefit
- 100-150 words
- Acknowledge their work and audience genuinely
- Present the opportunity clearly: what they do, what they earn
- Share a relevant metric: "[PRODUCT] converts at [X]%" or "[NUMBER] customers served"
- Provide a sample or free access to your product (if applicable)
- CTA: "Happy to send you a [SAMPLE/FREE ACCESS] — no strings attached"
- Professional and respectful of their time and audience trust
```

### Co-Marketing Collaboration

```
Write a co-marketing proposal from [YOUR NAME] at [BUSINESS NAME] to [PARTNER NAME] at [PARTNER COMPANY].

Collaboration idea: [DESCRIBE — joint webinar, co-authored guide, social media takeover, bundle, event, email swap]
Your audience: [SIZE AND DESCRIPTION]
Their audience (estimated): [SIZE AND DESCRIPTION]
Overlap: [WHY YOUR AUDIENCES WOULD APPRECIATE EACH OTHER]
Timeline: [WHEN YOU'D LIKE TO DO THIS]

Requirements:
- 120-180 words
- Lead with what's in it for them (audience growth, fresh content, revenue)
- Describe the collaboration clearly with minimal effort on their part
- Mention your audience size and engagement (prove you bring value to the table)
- Offer to handle the majority of the work
- Suggest a specific timeline
- CTA: "I put together a one-page outline — want me to send it over?"
- Confident, collaborative tone — two equals exploring a win-win
```

### Local Business Cross-Promotion

```
Write an outreach message from [BUSINESS NAME] to a local business for cross-promotion.

Your business: [WHAT YOU DO AND WHERE YOU'RE LOCATED]
Their business: [NAME, WHAT THEY DO, THEIR LOCATION]
Cross-promotion idea: [DESCRIBE — display each other's flyers, joint discount, shared event, social media shoutout, gift card exchange]
Why it makes sense: [COMPLEMENTARY CUSTOMERS, SAME NEIGHBORHOOD, SHARED VALUES]

Requirements:
- 80-120 words
- Friendly, neighborly tone (you're both local)
- Reference their business specifically — show you've been there or know their work
- Present the idea as simple and low-effort
- Suggest a coffee or quick meeting to discuss
- "No pressure — just thought it could be a fun way to support each other"
```
