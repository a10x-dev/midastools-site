## Set Your Operations Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using operations prompts:

```
You are a business operations consultant for my small business.

Business: [BUSINESS NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Business model: [E-COMMERCE / SERVICE / SAAS / RETAIL / CONSULTING / OTHER]
Team size: [NUMBER OF EMPLOYEES]
Annual revenue range: [RANGE OR "Pre-revenue"]
Location: [CITY, STATE]
Key tools we use: [LIST TOOLS — e.g., Slack, Google Workspace, QuickBooks, Shopify, Trello]
Biggest operational challenge: [WHAT'S YOUR MAIN PAIN POINT]

Write all content in a clear, actionable format. Prioritize practicality over theory.
```

## SOP Generator

### Create an SOP from Scratch

```
Write a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [BUSINESS NAME].

Process: [NAME OF THE PROCESS — e.g., "Processing a customer refund"]
Department/team: [WHO OWNS THIS PROCESS]
Frequency: [HOW OFTEN — daily, per order, weekly, as needed]
Current state: [IS THIS DOCUMENTED ANYWHERE? OR STARTING FROM SCRATCH?]

Describe the process in your own words (rough is fine, the AI will organize it):
"[DESCRIBE WHAT HAPPENS FROM START TO FINISH — e.g., Customer emails requesting refund. We check the order date. If within 30 days, we process it through Shopify. We email confirmation. We log it in our spreadsheet.]"

Requirements:
- Write a professional SOP document with these sections:
  1. **Purpose** — Why this SOP exists (2-3 sentences)
  2. **Scope** — Who this applies to and when
  3. **Definitions** — Any terms that need clarification
  4. **Responsibilities** — Who does what
  5. **Procedure** — Numbered step-by-step instructions
  6. **Decision points** — If/then scenarios (e.g., "If the order is past 30 days, go to Step 8")
  7. **Tools required** — Software, logins, or materials needed
  8. **Exceptions** — When to deviate and who can authorize exceptions
  9. **Related documents** — Links to policies, forms, or other SOPs
  10. **Revision history** — Version, date, author, changes
- Each step should be clear enough that a new employee could follow it on day one
- Include estimated time for each step where relevant
- Add tips or warnings for common mistakes
```

### Quick SOP (One-Page Version)

```
Write a one-page quick-reference SOP for [BUSINESS NAME].

Process: [PROCESS NAME]
Steps (from memory, rough):
1. [STEP 1]
2. [STEP 2]
3. [STEP 3]
4. [STEP 4]
5. [STEP 5]

Requirements:
- Title, owner, and last updated date at the top
- Numbered steps with brief descriptions (one line each)
- A "Do / Don't" box with 3 common dos and 3 common don'ts
- Fits on one printed page
- Can be printed and posted near a workstation
```

### Turn Messy Notes into an SOP

```
I'm going to paste my rough notes about a process at [BUSINESS NAME]. Turn them into a professional SOP.

My notes:
"[PASTE YOUR MESSY NOTES, BULLET POINTS, OR BRAIN DUMP HERE]"

Requirements:
- Organize the chaos into a logical, sequential SOP
- Fill in any obvious gaps (ask me if you're unsure about something)
- Add decision points where the process might branch
- Include a "Things That Can Go Wrong" section based on what you can infer
- Format it professionally with all standard SOP sections
```

### SOP Audit and Improvement

```
Review and improve this existing SOP for [BUSINESS NAME].

Current SOP:
"[PASTE YOUR EXISTING SOP]"

Requirements:
- Identify any missing steps or unclear instructions
- Flag potential bottlenecks or inefficiencies
- Suggest improvements for speed, clarity, or error reduction
- Add any missing sections (purpose, scope, exceptions, etc.)
- Rewrite the improved version
- Provide a summary of what you changed and why
```

## Meeting Agenda and Notes

### Meeting Agenda Generator

```
Create a meeting agenda for [BUSINESS NAME].

Meeting type: [TEAM MEETING / 1-ON-1 / CLIENT MEETING / BOARD MEETING / PROJECT KICKOFF / BRAINSTORM / QUARTERLY REVIEW]
Date: [DATE]
Time: [TIME AND DURATION]
Attendees: [LIST NAMES AND ROLES]
Location: [IN-PERSON / ZOOM / OTHER]

Topics to cover:
1. [TOPIC 1 — brief description]
2. [TOPIC 2 — brief description]
3. [TOPIC 3 — brief description]

Decisions needed:
- [DECISION 1]
- [DECISION 2]

Requirements:
- Professional agenda format
- Time allocation for each item (should total the meeting duration)
- Indicate who leads each topic
- Include "Objective" for each agenda item (Inform, Discuss, Decide, or Brainstorm)
- Add 5 minutes at the start for check-in and at the end for action items
- Include a "Parking Lot" section for off-topic items
- Format so it can be pasted into an email or Google Doc
```

### Meeting Notes Template

```
Create a meeting notes template for [BUSINESS NAME] that can be reused for any meeting.

Requirements:
Include these sections:
1. **Meeting Info** — Date, time, attendees, absent, facilitator
2. **Agenda Items Discussed** — Topic, key points, decisions made
3. **Action Items** — Task, owner, deadline (table format)
4. **Parking Lot** — Items raised but not addressed, to be scheduled
5. **Next Meeting** — Date, time, preliminary agenda items

Format as a clean, copy-paste-ready template with placeholder text.
```

### Turn Meeting Notes into Action Items

```
I'm going to paste my rough meeting notes from [BUSINESS NAME]. Extract and organize the action items.

Meeting notes:
"[PASTE YOUR ROUGH NOTES — can be messy, bullet points, stream of consciousness]"

Requirements:
- Extract every action item, commitment, or task mentioned
- For each action item, identify:
  - Task: What needs to be done (clear, specific)
  - Owner: Who's responsible (based on context clues)
  - Deadline: When it's due (if mentioned, or suggest one)
  - Priority: High / Medium / Low
  - Dependencies: Does this block or depend on anything else?
- List any decisions that were made
- Flag any unresolved items that need follow-up
- Format as a table ready to paste into a project management tool
```

### Weekly Team Meeting Agenda (Recurring)

```
Create a recurring weekly team meeting agenda template for [BUSINESS NAME].

Team: [TEAM NAME OR "Whole company"]
Meeting duration: [30 / 45 / 60 MINUTES]
Day and time: [DAY AND TIME]

Requirements:
- Design a repeatable weekly structure
- Include:
  1. Wins from last week (round-robin, 1 min each)
  2. Key metrics review (what numbers to check weekly)
  3. Priorities for this week (each person shares top 3)
  4. Blockers and help needed
  5. One spotlight topic (rotating, deeper discussion)
  6. Action items and owners
- Time allocations for each section
- Designed to stay under [DURATION] and maintain energy
- Include facilitation tips for keeping the meeting focused
```

## Business Plan Section Writer

### Executive Summary

```
Write an executive summary for [BUSINESS NAME]'s business plan.

Business: [BUSINESS NAME]
What we do: [PRODUCTS/SERVICES — 2-3 sentences]
Problem we solve: [WHAT PAIN POINT]
Target market: [WHO WE SERVE]
Revenue model: [HOW WE MAKE MONEY]
Current traction: [REVENUE, CUSTOMERS, GROWTH — whatever you have]
Funding goal (if applicable): [AMOUNT AND PURPOSE]
Competitive advantage: [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT]
Team: [KEY FOUNDERS/LEADERS AND THEIR RELEVANT EXPERIENCE]
Vision: [WHERE YOU WANT TO BE IN 3-5 YEARS]

Requirements:
- 400-500 words
- Compelling but factual — an investor or bank should want to read more
- Lead with the opportunity, not the product
- Include key metrics and projections
- End with the ask (funding, partnership, or just the vision)
- Professional tone with confidence but not hype
```

### Market Analysis

```
Write the market analysis section of a business plan for [BUSINESS NAME].

Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Target market: [WHO YOU SERVE]
Geographic focus: [LOCAL / REGIONAL / NATIONAL / GLOBAL]

What I know about my market (provide whatever you have):
- Market size: [ESTIMATED OR "Research this for me"]
- Growth trends: [WHAT YOU'VE OBSERVED]
- Customer demographics: [AGE, INCOME, LOCATION, BEHAVIOR]
- Key competitors: [LIST 3-5 COMPETITORS]

Requirements:
- 300-400 words
- Cover: market size (TAM, SAM, SOM if applicable), trends, customer segments, and competitive landscape
- Use a professional, data-driven tone
- If I didn't provide market data, note where I should find it (IBISWorld, Statista, Census Bureau, industry associations)
- Include a brief competitive positioning table (you vs. top 3 competitors on 4-5 criteria)
- Identify gaps or opportunities in the market
```

### Financial Projections Outline

```
Create a financial projections outline for [BUSINESS NAME]'s business plan.

Current state:
- Monthly revenue: [AMOUNT]
- Monthly expenses: [AMOUNT]
- Number of customers/units sold: [NUMBER]
- Average order value / ticket size: [AMOUNT]
- Growth rate: [MONTH-OVER-MONTH OR YEAR-OVER-YEAR]

Requirements:
- Create a framework for 3-year financial projections
- Include: Revenue forecast, COGS, gross margin, operating expenses, net income
- Show monthly for Year 1, quarterly for Years 2-3
- List assumptions clearly (growth rate, pricing, hiring, etc.)
- Provide the structure and formulas — I'll fill in exact numbers
- Include a break-even analysis framework
- Note: "These projections are templates. Work with an accountant for final figures."
```

## Competitive Analysis Prompts

### Comprehensive Competitor Analysis

```
Help me analyze a competitor of [BUSINESS NAME].

Competitor: [COMPETITOR NAME]
Their website: [URL]
What they sell: [PRODUCTS/SERVICES]
Their target audience: [WHO THEY SERVE]
What I know about them: [ANYTHING YOU ALREADY KNOW — pricing, strengths, weaknesses]

Analyze them on:
1. **Product/service offering** — What do they sell? How does it compare to ours?
2. **Pricing** — How do their prices compare? What's their pricing model?
3. **Brand and positioning** — How do they present themselves? What's their value proposition?
4. **Online presence** — Website quality, SEO, social media activity
5. **Customer reviews** — What do customers love and complain about? (Suggest where to find reviews)
6. **Strengths** — What do they do better than us?
7. **Weaknesses** — Where are their gaps that we could exploit?
8. **Opportunities** — What can we learn from them or do differently?

Requirements:
- Structured analysis, 300-400 words
- Be objective — acknowledge their strengths
- Identify 3 specific things we can do to differentiate
- Note: If you can't verify specific claims about the competitor, flag what I should verify myself
```

### Competitive Landscape Overview

```
Help me map the competitive landscape for [BUSINESS NAME] in the [INDUSTRY] space.

My business: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
Known competitors:
1. [COMPETITOR 1] — [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
2. [COMPETITOR 2] — [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
3. [COMPETITOR 3] — [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
4. [COMPETITOR 4] — [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]

Requirements:
- Create a competitive positioning matrix (2x2 grid)
- Suggest the best two axes for differentiation (e.g., Price vs. Quality, Niche vs. Broad, DIY vs. Full-Service)
- Place each competitor on the grid
- Identify the whitespace — where is there an underserved position?
- Write a 2-sentence positioning statement for [BUSINESS NAME] based on the analysis
- Suggest 3 ways to make our differentiation more visible to customers
```

### SWOT Analysis

```
Create a SWOT analysis for [BUSINESS NAME].

Business: [BUSINESS NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Current situation: [BRIEF — how things are going, any major changes, challenges, or opportunities]

Here's what I think (fill in what you know, the AI will expand):
- Strengths: [LIST WHAT YOU DO WELL]
- Weaknesses: [LIST HONEST CHALLENGES]
- Opportunities: [MARKET TRENDS, GAPS, IDEAS]
- Threats: [COMPETITORS, MARKET CHANGES, RISKS]

Requirements:
- Expand each quadrant to 5-7 bullet points
- Add items I might not have considered
- For each item, write one sentence of context or implication
- Prioritize: mark the top 2 items in each quadrant as "critical"
- End with 3 strategic recommendations based on the SWOT
- 300-400 words total
```

## Financial Report Summaries

### Monthly Financial Summary

```
I'm going to provide my monthly financial data for [BUSINESS NAME]. Write a professional monthly financial summary.

Month: [MONTH, YEAR]

Revenue:
- Total revenue: [AMOUNT]
- Revenue by source: [BREAK DOWN — e.g., product sales $X, services $Y, subscriptions $Z]
- Compared to last month: [AMOUNT — up or down]
- Compared to same month last year: [AMOUNT — if available]

Expenses:
- Total expenses: [AMOUNT]
- Top expense categories: [LIST — e.g., COGS $X, marketing $Y, payroll $Z, rent $X]
- Any unusual expenses: [DESCRIBE]

Other:
- Net profit/loss: [AMOUNT]
- Cash on hand: [AMOUNT]
- Accounts receivable: [AMOUNT — if applicable]
- Number of customers/orders: [NUMBER]

Requirements:
- Write a 200-300 word executive summary
- Lead with the headline: "Revenue was [up/down] X% month-over-month"
- Highlight the 3 most important trends or changes
- Flag any concerning trends
- Include 2-3 specific recommendations for next month
- End with a "Watch List" of 2 items to monitor
- Professional tone suitable for sharing with a partner, investor, or accountant
```

### Cash Flow Analysis Prompt

```
Help me analyze my cash flow at [BUSINESS NAME].

Monthly cash inflows:
- [SOURCE 1]: [AMOUNT] — received [TIMING — e.g., immediately, net 30, monthly subscription]
- [SOURCE 2]: [AMOUNT] — received [TIMING]
- [SOURCE 3]: [AMOUNT] — received [TIMING]

Monthly cash outflows:
- [EXPENSE 1]: [AMOUNT] — due [TIMING — e.g., 1st of month, varies, quarterly]
- [EXPENSE 2]: [AMOUNT] — due [TIMING]
- [EXPENSE 3]: [AMOUNT] — due [TIMING]
- [EXPENSE 4]: [AMOUNT] — due [TIMING]

Current cash balance: [AMOUNT]
Upcoming large expenses: [ANY KNOWN BIG COSTS IN NEXT 90 DAYS]

Requirements:
- Identify my cash flow pattern: When is cash tightest?
- Flag any timing mismatches (money going out before it comes in)
- Calculate my cash runway (how many months of expenses my current balance covers)
- Suggest 3 specific ways to improve cash flow
- Provide a simple 90-day cash flow projection framework
- Note: "This is an analysis tool, not accounting advice. Consult with your accountant for financial decisions."
```

### Year-End Financial Review

```
Help me create a year-end financial review for [BUSINESS NAME].

Year: [YEAR]
Total annual revenue: [AMOUNT]
Total annual expenses: [AMOUNT]
Net profit/loss: [AMOUNT]
Revenue by quarter: Q1 [$], Q2 [$], Q3 [$], Q4 [$]
Top revenue sources: [LIST]
Biggest expense categories: [LIST]
Year-over-year comparison (if available): Last year revenue was [$]
Major events: [KEY BUSINESS EVENTS — launches, hires, pivots, wins, challenges]
Goals from start of year: [WHAT YOU SET OUT TO DO]

Requirements:
- Write a professional year-end financial narrative (300-400 words)
- Executive summary at the top
- Revenue analysis: trends, best/worst months, seasonal patterns
- Expense analysis: where the money went, efficiency opportunities
- Goal assessment: which goals were met, which weren't, and why
- Key wins and key challenges
- 5 strategic priorities for next year based on this year's data
- Suitable for sharing with a business partner, board, or accountant
```
