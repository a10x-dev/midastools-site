## Collection Page Descriptions

### Prompt 1: Collection Page SEO Description

```
Write an SEO-optimized collection page description for [COLLECTION NAME] on [STORE NAME].

Collection details:
- Collection name: [COLLECTION NAME — e.g., "Women's Summer Dresses"]
- Number of products in collection: [X]
- Price range: [LOW - HIGH]
- Target keyword: [PRIMARY KEYWORD]
- Secondary keywords: [KEYWORD 2], [KEYWORD 3], [KEYWORD 4]
- Who shops this collection: [TARGET CUSTOMER PROFILE]
- What makes this collection unique: [DIFFERENTIATOR]

Requirements:
- Write 200-300 words (Shopify and most platforms display this above or below product grid)
- Structure:
  - Opening paragraph (60-80 words): Introduce the collection with the primary keyword in the first sentence. Speak to the shopper's intent — what are they looking for and why?
  - Middle paragraph (80-100 words): Highlight what makes this collection special. Include secondary keywords naturally. Mention popular subcategories or product types within the collection.
  - Closing paragraph (40-60 words): CTA and trust signals. Mention free shipping, returns, customer ratings.
- Include the primary keyword 3-4 times naturally
- Include each secondary keyword at least once
- Use one H2 subheading that includes a keyword
- Do NOT keyword stuff — every sentence must read naturally
- Tone: [BRAND VOICE]
```

> **Why this works:** Collection pages are often the highest-traffic pages on an e-commerce site (after the homepage). Most stores leave them with zero text, missing a massive SEO opportunity. Even 200 words of well-optimized copy can move a collection page from page 3 to page 1 for relevant keywords.

```
Products included: [BRIEF OVERVIEW OF PRODUCT TYPES]
Price range: [LOW - HIGH]

Requirements:

- Open with seasonal context that naturally includes the primary keyword
- Include gift-giving language if it's a gift-oriented season

- Include a seasonal urgency element (shipping deadlines, limited availability)
- Reference the year (seasonal SEO is time-sensitive — "2026" in the content helps)
- All seasonal keywords included naturally
- CTA: Shop the collection
- Note: This page should be published [X] weeks before the season and updated annually
```

## Blog Post Ideas Generator

### Prompt 4: SEO Blog Topic Generator

```
Generate 20 SEO-optimized blog post ideas for [STORE NAME].

Store details:
- Niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- Products: [WHAT YOU SELL]
- Target keywords to build authority for: [LIST 5-10 KEYWORDS]
- Target audience: [WHO READS YOUR BLOG]
- Competitor blogs: [1-3 COMPETITOR BLOG URLs]

For each blog post idea, provide:
1. Title (include target keyword, under 60 characters)
2. Target keyword
3. Search intent: [INFORMATIONAL / COMMERCIAL / TRANSACTIONAL]
4. Estimated keyword difficulty: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH]
5. Blog format: [HOW-TO / LISTICLE / BUYING GUIDE / COMPARISON / REVIEW / FAQ]
6. How it connects to your products: [WHICH PRODUCTS CAN YOU NATURALLY LINK TO]
7. Word count recommendation: [500-800 / 800-1500 / 1500-3000]

Organize the 20 ideas into:
- 5 "top of funnel" posts (awareness — people don't know your brand yet)
- 5 "middle of funnel" posts (consideration — comparing options)
- 5 "bottom of funnel" posts (decision — ready to buy)
- 5 "authority building" posts (establish expertise in your niche)
```

> **Why this works:** A blog is the #1 way to rank for informational keywords that your product pages can't target. Someone searching "how to choose a running shoe" isn't going to find your product page — but they will find your blog post, and from there you guide them to your products. This content funnel is how top e-commerce brands build sustainable organic traffic.

## Keyword Research Prompts

### Prompt 7: Product Keyword Discovery

```
Generate a comprehensive keyword list for [PRODUCT NAME] from [STORE NAME].

Product details:
- Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
- Category: [CATEGORY]
- Key features: [FEATURES]
- Target audience: [WHO SEARCHES FOR THIS]
- Competitors ranking for this product type: [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2]

Generate keywords in these categories:

1. Primary product keywords (5-10):
   - Exact product type searches
   - Format: "[product type]" — e.g., "organic cotton t-shirt"

2. Long-tail keywords (15-20):
   - Specific, lower-competition searches
   - Format: "[modifier] + [product type] + [qualifier]" — e.g., "best organic cotton t-shirt for sensitive skin"

3. Question keywords (10):
   - What people ask before buying
   - Format: "how to / what is / which / best way to" related searches

4. Comparison keywords (5):
   - "[product] vs [alternative]" searches

5. Commercial intent keywords (5-10):
   - Searches from people ready to buy
   - "buy," "shop," "best," "price," "review," "near me"

For each keyword, suggest:
- Search intent: [INFORMATIONAL / COMMERCIAL / TRANSACTIONAL]
- Best page to target: [PRODUCT PAGE / COLLECTION PAGE / BLOG POST]
- Priority: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW] based on estimated volume and competition
```

> **Pro tip:** AI can't give you exact search volumes, but it excels at generating keyword ideas you'd never think of. Use these lists as starting points, then validate volumes and competition in a dedicated keyword tool (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Ubersuggest, or even Google Keyword Planner).

## Meta Title / Description Templates

### Prompt 10: Product Page Meta Tags

```
Write SEO meta titles and descriptions for [NUMBER] product pages on [STORE NAME].

Products:
1. [PRODUCT 1 NAME] — [TARGET KEYWORD 1] — [PRICE 1] — [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]
2. [PRODUCT 2 NAME] — [TARGET KEYWORD 2] — [PRICE 2] — [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]
3. [PRODUCT 3 NAME] — [TARGET KEYWORD 3] — [PRICE 3] — [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]
4. [PRODUCT 4 NAME] — [TARGET KEYWORD 4] — [PRICE 4] — [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]
5. [PRODUCT 5 NAME] — [TARGET KEYWORD 5] — [PRICE 5] — [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]

For each product write:
- Meta title: Under 60 characters. Structure: [Primary Keyword] — [Benefit or USP] | [STORE NAME]
- Meta description: 150-155 characters. Include the keyword, a compelling benefit, and a CTA.
- URL slug suggestion: lowercase, hyphens, keyword-focused

Write 2 versions of each:
- Version A: Feature-focused (specs, materials, facts)
- Version B: Benefit-focused (outcome, feeling, transformation)

Mark which version is better for [YOUR PRODUCT TYPE] and explain why.
```

> **Why this works:** Meta titles and descriptions are your ad copy in search results. A well-written meta description can increase click-through rate by 20-30% without changing your ranking position. That's free additional traffic from the same ranking.

### Prompt 13: Category Landing Page Content

```
Write SEO content for the [CATEGORY NAME] landing page on [STORE NAME].

Category details:
- Category: [CATEGORY NAME]
- Subcategories: [LIST SUBCATEGORIES]
- Number of products: [TOTAL]
- Price range: [LOW - HIGH]
- Target keyword: [PRIMARY KEYWORD]
- Secondary keywords: [KEYWORD 2], [KEYWORD 3], [KEYWORD 4], [KEYWORD 5]
- Top competitor pages for this keyword: [COMPETITOR 1 URL], [COMPETITOR 2 URL]

Write the following sections:

Section 1 — Above the fold intro (80-100 words):
- Primary keyword in the first sentence
- What shoppers will find in this category
- Quick trust signal (number of products, free shipping, reviews)

Section 2 — Subcategory guide (100-150 words):
- Brief paragraph for each subcategory explaining what's in it and who it's for
- Each subcategory name links to its own page (internal linking)
- Include secondary keywords naturally

Section 3 — Buying advice (100-150 words):
- "How to Choose the Right [PRODUCT TYPE]"
- 3-4 factors to consider (size, material, budget, use case)

- Questions people commonly search about this category
- Answers in 2-3 sentences each
- Structured for FAQ schema markup

Total: 400-500 words
```

> **Pro tip:** Category pages are the workhorses of e-commerce SEO. They target mid-funnel commercial keywords ("women's running shoes") that product pages can't effectively rank for individually. Invest in strong category content and you create a rising tide that lifts every product page beneath it.

## Buying Guide Frameworks

### Prompt 15: Comprehensive Buying Guide

```
Write a comprehensive buying guide for [PRODUCT CATEGORY] for [STORE NAME].

Guide details:
- Product category: [CATEGORY]
- Target keyword: [LONG-TAIL KEYWORD — e.g., "how to choose the best [PRODUCT]"]
- Target audience: [FIRST-TIME BUYERS / UPGRADERS / GIFT SHOPPERS]
- Your products in this category: [LIST 3-5 OF YOUR PRODUCTS TO REFERENCE]
- Competitor products shoppers consider: [LIST COMPETITORS — for comparison, don't link to them]

Guide structure:
1. Introduction (100 words):
   - What this guide covers and who it's for
   - Why choosing the right [PRODUCT] matters

2. Types of [PRODUCT] (200 words):
   - Break down the main types/styles/categories
   - Who each type is best for
   - Price range for each type

3. Key Features to Consider (300 words):
   - [FEATURE 1]: What it is, why it matters, what to look for
   - [FEATURE 2]: What it is, why it matters, what to look for
   - [FEATURE 3]: What it is, why it matters, what to look for
   - [FEATURE 4]: What it is, why it matters, what to look for
   - [FEATURE 5]: What it is, why it matters, what to look for

4. Price Ranges and What to Expect (150 words):
   - Budget ($[X]-$[Y]): What you get
   - Mid-range ($[X]-$[Y]): What you get
   - Premium ($[X]-$[Y]): What you get

5. Our Recommendations (200 words):
   - "Best overall": [YOUR PRODUCT] — why
   - "Best value": [YOUR PRODUCT] — why
   - "Best premium": [YOUR PRODUCT] — why
   (Naturally references your products without being a blatant ad)

6. FAQ (5 questions):
   - Target "People Also Ask" questions
   - Structured for schema markup

7. Conclusion (50 words):
   - Summary + CTA to shop the category

Total: 1000-1500 words
Meta title and description included
```

> **Why this works:** Buying guides target informational and commercial keywords that drive qualified traffic. Someone reading "how to choose the best espresso machine" is 2-3 steps away from buying one. By positioning your products as the recommended options within genuinely helpful content, you capture traffic AND trust simultaneously.
