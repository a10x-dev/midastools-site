## Set Your Social Media Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using any prompt below:

```
You are a social media strategist specializing in e-commerce brands.

Store: [STORE NAME]
Niche: [YOUR NICHE]
Products: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Brand aesthetic: [VISUAL STYLE — minimalist, colorful, earthy, bold, luxe]
Brand voice: [TONE — playful, sophisticated, quirky, warm, edgy]
Target audience: [WHO FOLLOWS YOU — age, interests, lifestyle]
Primary platform: [INSTAGRAM / TIKTOK / FACEBOOK / PINTEREST]
Average engagement rate: [IF KNOWN]
Website: [URL]
Content goal: [SALES / BRAND AWARENESS / COMMUNITY / ALL THREE]

Create content that feels native to each platform, authentic to the brand, and drives measurable action.
```

## Product Showcase Posts

### Prompt 1: New Arrival Announcement

```
Write an Instagram post announcing a new product for [STORE NAME].

Product details:
- Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
- What it is: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- Price: [PRICE]
- What makes it special: [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]
- Available starting: [DATE or "now"]
- Limited edition: [YES — quantity / NO]

Requirements:
- Caption: 150-200 words
- Open with a line that creates anticipation (not "New arrival alert!")
- Describe the product through the lens of how it changes the customer's day/life
- Include 2-3 specific product details woven naturally into the caption
- If limited edition, mention scarcity without being pushy
- CTA: direct to shop link with specific instructions
- Suggest 20 hashtags (mix of product, niche, and discovery hashtags)
- Suggest photo/video concept: [PRODUCT FLAT LAY / LIFESTYLE / UNBOXING / STYLED]
- Best posting time suggestion for [TARGET AUDIENCE TIMEZONE]
```

### Prompt 2: Bestseller Spotlight

```
Write a social media post spotlighting a bestselling product for [STORE NAME].

Product details:
- Product name: [PRODUCT NAME]
- Why it's the bestseller: [WHAT CUSTOMERS LOVE ABOUT IT]
- Number sold: [UNITS SOLD or "over X"]
- Star rating: [RATING]
- Price: [PRICE]
- Customer quote: [A REAL OR REPRESENTATIVE REVIEW SNIPPET]

Requirements:
- Caption: 120-180 words
- Open with a social proof hook ("Our most-reordered product" / "[X] of you bought this last month")
- Include the customer quote formatted as a pullout
- Share one detail about the product most people don't know
- End with "Have you tried it yet?" or similar engagement question
- 15 hashtags
- Suggest a carousel layout: slide 1 (product hero), slide 2 (review screenshot), slide 3 (product in use), slide 4 (CTA with price)
```

> **Why this works:** Bestseller posts combine social proof with product education. When customers see that thousands of others chose this product, it reduces perceived risk. The "most people don't know" detail gives existing customers new reasons to engage and new customers a reason to buy.

### Prompt 3: Product Comparison Post

```
Write a social media carousel post comparing [NUMBER] products from [STORE NAME].

Products to compare:
1. [PRODUCT 1] — best for [USE CASE 1] — [PRICE 1]
2. [PRODUCT 2] — best for [USE CASE 2] — [PRICE 2]
3. [PRODUCT 3] — best for [USE CASE 3] — [PRICE 3]

Target audience confusion: [WHAT QUESTION DO CUSTOMERS FREQUENTLY ASK ABOUT CHOOSING BETWEEN THESE]

Requirements:
- Caption: 150-200 words
- Open with the common question: "Not sure which [PRODUCT TYPE] is right for you?"
- Brief comparison framework (each product gets 1-2 sentences)
- End with "Save this for when you're ready to decide"
- Carousel layout:
  - Slide 1: "Which [PRODUCT] is right for you?" title card
  - Slides 2-4: One product per slide with "Best for: [USE CASE]"
  - Slide 5: Side-by-side comparison chart
  - Slide 6: "Still not sure? DM us" + shop link
- 15 hashtags
```

### Prompt 4: Product in Action / Lifestyle Post

```
Write a lifestyle-focused social media post for [PRODUCT NAME] from [STORE NAME].

Lifestyle context:
- Setting: [WHERE THE PRODUCT IS BEING USED — kitchen, gym, office, outdoors, travel]
- Who's using it: [PERSONA — busy mom, remote worker, fitness enthusiast, student]
- Activity: [WHAT THEY'RE DOING WHILE USING THE PRODUCT]
- Time of day: [MORNING ROUTINE / AFTERNOON / EVENING / WEEKEND]

Requirements:
- Caption: 120-180 words
- Open with a relatable moment or scenario the audience lives every day
- Place the product naturally within the story (not the center, but essential)
- Make the reader think "that's me" within the first two sentences
- Mention the product name and one feature organically
- No hard sell — let the lifestyle sell the product
- CTA: soft ("Link in bio if this speaks to you")
- Photo/video direction: describe the ideal shot composition
- 15 hashtags mixing lifestyle and product tags
```

### Prompt 5: Price Transparency Post

```
Write a social media post about the pricing of [PRODUCT NAME] from [STORE NAME].

Price breakdown:
- Retail price: [PRICE]
- What similar products cost: [COMPETITOR PRICE RANGE]
- Why yours costs what it does: [QUALITY MATERIALS / ETHICAL LABOR / SMALL BATCH / R&D / ETC.]
- Cost to make (if comfortable sharing): [APPROXIMATE COST]
- Your margin context: [OPTIONAL — percentage or "less than you'd think"]

Requirements:
- Caption: 180-250 words
- Open with "Here's exactly why [PRODUCT] costs $[PRICE]"
- Break down the value transparently (materials, labor, shipping, packaging)
- Compare to what customers might otherwise spend (cost per use, longevity)
- Address the "I can find cheaper" objection directly
- Close with why the price reflects the brand's values
- This post type builds massive trust — keep it honest, never defensive
- 10 hashtags
- Suggest infographic layout for a carousel version
```

> **Pro tip:** Price transparency posts consistently generate the highest save rates for e-commerce brands. Customers respect honesty about pricing, and saved posts resurface to drive purchases days or weeks later.

## User-Generated Content Prompts

### Prompt 10: UGC Repost Caption

```
Write a caption for reposting a customer's photo/video of [PRODUCT NAME] from [STORE NAME].

Customer content details:
- Customer name/handle: [@CUSTOMER_HANDLE]
- What they posted: [DESCRIBE THEIR PHOTO OR VIDEO]
- Product shown: [PRODUCT NAME]
- What they said: [THEIR ORIGINAL CAPTION OR QUOTE, IF AVAILABLE]

Requirements:
- Caption: 80-120 words
- Open with appreciation for the customer (tag them)
- Celebrate their post and what they shared
- Weave in a product detail or benefit naturally
- Encourage other customers to share their photos using [YOUR BRANDED HASHTAG]
- CTA: "Tag us or use #[BRANDED HASHTAG] for a chance to be featured"
- 10 hashtags
- Note: suggest asking permission before reposting
```

### Prompt 11: UGC Campaign Launch Post

```
Write a social media post launching a UGC campaign for [STORE NAME].

Campaign details:
- Campaign hashtag: #[BRANDED HASHTAG]
- What you want customers to share: [PHOTOS OF PRODUCT IN USE / STYLING IDEAS / BEFORE-AFTER / REVIEWS / CREATIVE USES]
- Incentive: [FEATURED ON PAGE / MONTHLY GIVEAWAY / DISCOUNT CODE / ENTRY INTO CONTEST]
- Duration: [ONGOING / START DATE - END DATE]
- Prize (if contest): [PRIZE DETAILS]

Requirements:
- Caption: 150-200 words
- Open with excitement: "We want to see YOUR [PRODUCT] in the wild"
- Clearly explain how to participate (3 simple steps)
- Describe the incentive and what they could win/earn
- Include rules briefly (own photo, use hashtag, tag account)
- Show 1-2 examples of what great submissions look like
- CTA: "Show us your [PRODUCT] — use #[BRANDED HASHTAG]"
- 15 hashtags
- Suggest creating a highlight bubble "Your Photos" for collected UGC
```

> **Why this works:** UGC campaigns build a library of authentic content you can reuse in ads, emails, and your website — all created by real customers at zero cost. The customers who participate become brand advocates. The customers who see UGC convert at 4-5x higher rates than those who see brand-created content.

### Prompt 16: Holiday Countdown Series

```
Create a [NUMBER]-day holiday countdown content plan for [STORE NAME].

Holiday: [HOLIDAY]
Days until holiday: [NUMBER]
Products to feature across the countdown: [LIST 5-10 PRODUCTS]
Daily offers (if any): [DAILY DEAL / ADVENT CALENDAR STYLE / SAME OFFER THROUGHOUT]

For each day, provide:
- Post theme (e.g., Day 12: Bestseller reveal, Day 11: Customer favorite, Day 10: Behind the scenes)
- Caption: 80-120 words
- Visual concept
- One unique angle per day (no repetitive "X days left" posts)

Requirements:
- Mix content types: product, story, UGC, tip, countdown urgency
- Build excitement gradually (soft start, peak urgency at the end)
- Include shipping deadline reminder when relevant
- Final 3 days should have strong urgency/CTA
- Suggest a Stories companion strategy for each post
```

> **Pro tip:** Holiday countdown series work because they train your audience to check your page daily. Each day's post serves a different purpose — awareness, desire, trust, urgency — so by the final days, buyers are primed to act. Start your countdown at least 12 days before the holiday.

## Influencer Collaboration Templates

### Prompt 17: Influencer Outreach DM / Email

```
Write an influencer outreach message for [STORE NAME].

Influencer details:
- Name/Handle: [@INFLUENCER_HANDLE]
- Platform: [INSTAGRAM / TIKTOK / YOUTUBE]
- Follower count: [APPROXIMATE]
- Niche: [THEIR CONTENT FOCUS]
- Why they're a good fit: [SPECIFIC REASON — their audience matches yours, they use similar products, their aesthetic aligns]
- Specific post that caught your eye: [DESCRIBE A RECENT POST OF THEIRS]

Collaboration offer:
- What you're offering: [FREE PRODUCT / PAID PARTNERSHIP / AFFILIATE COMMISSION / PRODUCT + FEE]
- What you're asking for: [1 POST / STORY SERIES / REEL / ONGOING AMBASSADOR]
- Budget: [BUDGET IF PAID]
- Promo code for their audience: [CODE — DISCOUNT]
- Commission structure (if affiliate): [PERCENTAGE PER SALE]

Requirements:
- Write 2 versions: one for DM (short, 60-80 words) and one for email (150-200 words)
- Open with genuine compliment about their specific content (not generic)
- Explain the fit naturally (why your brand + their audience = perfect match)
- Be upfront about what you're offering and what you're asking
- Include a soft CTA: "Would you be open to exploring this?"
- Never be pushy or entitled — they're doing you a favor
- Professional but warm tone
```
