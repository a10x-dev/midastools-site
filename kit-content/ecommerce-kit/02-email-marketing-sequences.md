## Set Your Email Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using any prompt below:

```
You are an expert e-commerce email copywriter who specializes in automated sequences that drive revenue.

Store: [STORE NAME]
Niche: [YOUR NICHE]
Products: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Average order value: [AOV]
Target customer: [WHO BUYS FROM YOU]
Brand voice: [TONE — e.g., warm, playful, premium, minimalist]
Email platform: [KLAVIYO / MAILCHIMP / OMNISEND / DRIP / OTHER]
Website: [URL]

Write emails that feel personal, drive action, and respect the reader's inbox.
Always include a clear single CTA per email.
```

## Welcome Series

### Prompt 1: Welcome Email 1 — The Warm Introduction (Send: Immediately)

```
Write the first email in a welcome sequence for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Trigger: Customer just subscribed via [POPUP / FOOTER / CHECKOUT / LEAD MAGNET]
- Incentive promised: [DISCOUNT CODE / FREE SHIPPING / FREE GUIDE / NONE]
- Discount code (if applicable): [CODE]
- Discount details: [PERCENTAGE / DOLLAR AMOUNT / WHAT IT APPLIES TO]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (one curiosity-driven, one benefit-driven, one personal)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters, complements the subject line
- Body: 150-200 words
- Open with a genuine welcome (not generic "Welcome to our newsletter")
- Deliver the promised incentive immediately (above the fold)
- Share your brand story in 2-3 sentences (who you are, why you exist)
- Set expectations for email frequency and content
- Single CTA: [SHOP NOW WITH DISCOUNT / BROWSE BESTSELLERS]
- Sign off with a founder name for personal touch
- Tone: [BRAND VOICE]
```

> **Why this works:** Welcome emails have the highest open rates of any automated email (50-60% average). Delivering the promised incentive immediately builds trust. The brand story creates emotional connection before you ever try to sell.

### Prompt 2: Welcome Email 2 — The Best Sellers (Send: Day 2)

```
Write the second welcome email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- This email arrives 1-2 days after signup
- The subscriber has or hasn't made a first purchase yet
- Goal: introduce your best products and build desire

Products to feature:
1. [BESTSELLER 1 NAME] — [KEY BENEFIT] — [PRICE]
2. [BESTSELLER 2 NAME] — [KEY BENEFIT] — [PRICE]
3. [BESTSELLER 3 NAME] — [KEY BENEFIT] — [PRICE]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options focused on discovery / "what others love"
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 150-200 words
- Open with social proof framing ("Here's what our customers can't stop buying")
- Feature 3 bestsellers with a 1-sentence pitch for each
- Include star ratings or review counts if available
- If they haven't used their welcome discount, remind them with a soft nudge
- Single CTA: Shop Bestsellers
- Keep product sections visually distinct (suggest layout)
```

### Prompt 3: Welcome Email 3 — The Story (Send: Day 4)

```
Write the third welcome email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- This is the brand story / emotional connection email
- Goal: make the subscriber care about your brand, not just your products

Brand story details:
- Founder name: [NAME]
- How the brand started: [ORIGIN STORY — 1-2 sentences]
- The problem you set out to solve: [PROBLEM]
- What makes you different: [DIFFERENTIATOR]
- Values that guide you: [2-3 CORE VALUES]
- Customer impact: [HOW YOU'VE HELPED CUSTOMERS — a number or anecdote]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (personal, story-driven, curiosity)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 200-250 words
- Write from the founder's perspective (first person)
- Tell the origin story in a compelling arc: problem > discovery > mission
- Mention your values without being preachy
- End with an invitation to be part of the journey
- CTA: [SHOP / READ MORE / FOLLOW ON INSTAGRAM]
- Include a photo suggestion (founder at work, team, workshop)
```

> **Pro tip:** The brand story email consistently has the highest reply rates in welcome sequences. People reply to stories. Those replies improve your sender reputation and deliverability across the entire list.

### Prompt 5: Welcome Email 5 — Last Chance (Send: Day 8)

```
Write the fifth and final welcome email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Last email in the welcome sequence
- If they received a welcome discount, this is the final reminder before it expires
- Goal: convert remaining non-buyers with urgency

Offer details:
- Original offer: [DISCOUNT/OFFER]
- Expiration: [WHEN IT EXPIRES]
- Code: [DISCOUNT CODE]
- What to recommend: [TOP 3 PRODUCTS IN DIFFERENT PRICE RANGES]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options with urgency (not spammy)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 100-150 words (short = urgent)
- Open with a clear expiration reminder
- Restate the offer simply
- Show 3 product recommendations at different price points
  (removes the "I don't know what to buy" objection)
- Address common objections in one line: free returns, fast shipping, guarantee
- Single, prominent CTA: Claim Your [X]% Off
- If no discount was offered, replace urgency with a "we'd love to see you" personal tone
```

## Abandoned Cart Recovery

### Prompt 6: Abandoned Cart Email 1 — The Gentle Reminder (Send: 1 Hour After)

```
Write the first abandoned cart email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- The customer added [PRODUCT NAME] to their cart and left
- This email sends 1 hour after abandonment
- Goal: bring them back with a simple, no-pressure reminder
- Do NOT offer a discount in this email

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options (casual, not salesy — e.g., "Still thinking it over?" / "You left something behind")
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 80-120 words
- Tone: helpful, not pushy
- Include the product name, image placeholder, and price
- Acknowledge they were browsing: "We saved your cart"
- One line addressing a possible concern: [FREE SHIPPING / EASY RETURNS / SECURE CHECKOUT]
- Single CTA: Complete Your Order
- No discount, no urgency — just a friendly reminder
```

> **Why this works:** The first cart email should never lead with a discount. Many customers left due to distraction, not price resistance. A simple reminder converts 5-10% of cart abandoners without cutting into your margins. Save discounts for email 3.

### Prompt 7: Abandoned Cart Email 2 — Address Objections (Send: 24 Hours After)

```
Write the second abandoned cart email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- 24 hours since cart abandonment
- They didn't respond to the first reminder
- Goal: address the likely reason they didn't purchase

Common objections to address:
- Price concern: [YOUR RETURN POLICY / GUARANTEE]
- Trust concern: [NUMBER OF REVIEWS / STAR RATING]
- Shipping concern: [SHIPPING SPEED AND COST]
- Comparison shopping: [WHAT MAKES YOUR PRODUCT THE BEST CHOICE]
- Not sure about fit/size/color: [SIZE GUIDE LINK / CUSTOMER SERVICE CONTACT]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options (address objections — e.g., "Questions about [PRODUCT]?" / "Here's what others say about [PRODUCT]")
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 120-180 words
- Open with empathy: "Big decisions deserve careful thought"
- Address 2-3 common objections directly
- Include 1-2 short customer review quotes about the abandoned product
- Mention your guarantee or return policy prominently
- CTA: Return to Your Cart
- Still no discount
```

### Prompt 8: Abandoned Cart Email 3 — The Final Nudge (Send: 48-72 Hours After)

```
Write the third and final abandoned cart email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- 48-72 hours since abandonment
- Two previous emails didn't convert them
- This is the last attempt — time to bring the strongest offer

Incentive to offer: [10% OFF / FREE SHIPPING / FREE GIFT WITH PURCHASE / NONE]
Discount code (if applicable): [CODE]
Code expiration: [24-48 HOURS FROM SEND]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options with urgency + incentive
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 100-150 words
- Open with the incentive (lead with the new value proposition)
- Re-show the abandoned product with image placeholder and price
- If offering discount, show original price struck through and new price
- Add urgency: code expires in [TIMEFRAME]
- Include a single compelling review quote
- Stack trust signals: [FREE RETURNS] + [STAR RATING] + [GUARANTEE]
- Bold, urgent CTA: Complete Your Order — Save [X]%
- Final line: "After this, we won't bug you again" (builds trust)
```

> **Pro tip:** The 3-email abandoned cart sequence typically recovers 10-15% of lost sales. Email 1 gets the easy wins (distracted shoppers). Email 2 gets the hesitant shoppers. Email 3 gets the price-sensitive shoppers. Never skip email 1 to jump to a discount.

## Post-Purchase Follow-Up

### Prompt 9: Post-Purchase Email 1 — Order Excitement (Send: Immediately After Purchase)

```
Write a post-purchase thank you email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Customer just completed a purchase
- Goal: reinforce the buying decision and build excitement

Order details to reference:
- Product purchased: [PRODUCT NAME]
- Order number placeholder: [#ORDER_NUMBER]
- Estimated delivery: [DELIVERY WINDOW]
- Tracking: [MENTION TRACKING WILL BE SENT SEPARATELY]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (celebratory, confirming, personal)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 120-180 words
- Open with genuine excitement and gratitude (not a sterile "order confirmation")
- Confirm what they bought and when it arrives
- Share a product tip or "what to expect" for the product
- Reduce buyer's remorse with a reinforcing statement ("You made a great choice — here's why")
- Mention your customer support availability
- If first-time buyer, make them feel special
- CTA: [TRACK YOUR ORDER / SHARE ON SOCIAL / BROWSE RELATED ITEMS]
- Signed from a real person (founder, customer happiness team)
```

### Prompt 10: Post-Purchase Email 2 — Product Tips (Send: Day 3-5 After Delivery)

```
Write a post-purchase product tips email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Product has been delivered (or estimated delivered based on shipping time)
- Goal: help them get maximum value from their purchase

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Usage tips:
1. [TIP 1 — e.g., best way to set up, first-time use advice]
2. [TIP 2 — e.g., care instructions, storage, maintenance]
3. [TIP 3 — e.g., creative or unexpected use case]

Complementary products:
1. [RELATED PRODUCT 1] — [WHY IT PAIRS WELL]
2. [RELATED PRODUCT 2] — [WHY IT PAIRS WELL]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options focused on getting the most from their purchase
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 150-200 words
- Open by checking in: "How are you liking your [PRODUCT]?"
- Share 3 tips to enhance their experience
- Introduce complementary products as helpful additions, not a hard sell
- Mention customer support if they need help
- CTA: [SHOP COMPLEMENTARY PRODUCTS / REPLY WITH QUESTIONS]
```

> **Why this works:** Post-purchase emails that provide genuine value (tips, care guides, creative uses) have a dual benefit: they reduce returns by helping customers succeed with the product, and they set up the cross-sell naturally. Customers who feel supported spend 3x more on future orders.

### Prompt 11: Post-Purchase Email 3 — Cross-Sell (Send: Day 10-14 After Delivery)

```
Write a post-purchase cross-sell email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Customer has had the product for 1-2 weeks
- Goal: drive a second purchase with a relevant recommendation

Product they purchased: [ORIGINAL PRODUCT]
Recommended next purchases:
1. [CROSS-SELL PRODUCT 1] — [PRICE] — [WHY BUYERS OF THE ORIGINAL LOVE THIS]
2. [CROSS-SELL PRODUCT 2] — [PRICE] — [WHY BUYERS OF THE ORIGINAL LOVE THIS]
3. [CROSS-SELL PRODUCT 3] — [PRICE] — [WHY BUYERS OF THE ORIGINAL LOVE THIS]

Repeat buyer incentive: [RETURNING CUSTOMER DISCOUNT / FREE SHIPPING / LOYALTY POINTS / NONE]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (personalized recommendations feel)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 150-200 words
- Open with "Customers who bought [ORIGINAL PRODUCT] also love..."
- Feature 3 products with 1-sentence pitches
- Include star ratings or "bestseller" badges
- If offering a repeat buyer incentive, present it as exclusive
- Frame as personal curation, not mass marketing
- CTA: Shop Your Recommendations
```

## Win-Back Sequence

### Prompt 12: Win-Back Email 1 — We Miss You (Send: 30 Days Since Last Purchase)

```
Write the first win-back email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Customer hasn't purchased in [30-60 DAYS]
- They were previously an active buyer
- Goal: re-engage without seeming desperate

Customer segment details:
- Last product purchased: [PRODUCT CATEGORY]
- Average previous order value: [AOV]
- Number of previous orders: [ORDERS]

What's new since they last shopped:
- New product: [NEW PRODUCT 1]
- New collection: [NEW COLLECTION/CATEGORY]
- Store update: [ANY IMPROVEMENTS — faster shipping, new packaging, etc.]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options (personal, "miss you" without being cringe)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 120-180 words
- Open with acknowledgment that it's been a while (warm, not guilt-tripping)
- Share 1-2 things that are new since their last visit
- Remind them what they love about your brand (based on what they bought)
- No discount yet — just rekindling the relationship
- CTA: See What's New
```

### Prompt 13: Win-Back Email 2 — Personal Offer (Send: 45 Days)

```
Write the second win-back email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- First win-back email didn't convert them
- Time to offer a tangible incentive
- Goal: give them a compelling reason to return

Win-back offer: [PERCENTAGE OFF / DOLLAR OFF / FREE SHIPPING / FREE GIFT]
Code: [WIN-BACK CODE]
Expiration: [EXPIRATION DATE]
Applicable to: [ALL PRODUCTS / SPECIFIC CATEGORY / MINIMUM ORDER]

Products to recommend (based on their purchase history):
1. [RECOMMENDED PRODUCT 1] — [PRICE]
2. [RECOMMENDED PRODUCT 2] — [PRICE]
3. [RECOMMENDED PRODUCT 3] — [PRICE]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options leading with the offer
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 120-160 words
- Open with the incentive front and center
- Frame it as exclusive: "This is just for you" / "A little something for our valued customers"
- Show 3 personalized product recommendations
- Include the code prominently with clear expiration
- CTA: Claim Your [X]% Off
```

> **Pro tip:** Win-back emails with a specific offer convert 3-5x better than "we miss you" emails alone. But always send the relationship email first — customers who re-engage without a discount are your most valuable segment.

### Prompt 14: Win-Back Email 3 — Last Chance (Send: 60-90 Days)

```
Write the final win-back email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Customer hasn't responded to 2 previous win-back emails
- This is the last attempt before sunsetting them from active marketing
- Goal: one final conversion attempt, or a clean goodbye

Final offer: [BEST OFFER — bigger than email 2]
Code: [FINAL CODE]
Expiration: [48-72 HOURS]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options (urgency + finality — "Last chance" / "Before we go quiet")
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 100-140 words (keep it short and direct)
- Open with transparency: "We don't want to fill your inbox with emails you don't want"
- Present the strongest offer you're willing to make
- Give them two clear options:
  1. Shop with this exclusive offer
  2. We'll reduce emails going forward
- Make the offer feel genuinely final
- Include an unsubscribe option without making it punitive
- CTA: Shop One Last Time / Update Preferences
- Keep it respectful — a good goodbye builds more brand equity than a spammy last email
```

## Product Launch Announcement

### Prompt 15: New Product Launch Email

```
Write a product launch announcement email for [STORE NAME].

New product details:
- Product name: [NEW PRODUCT NAME]
- Category: [CATEGORY]
- Price: [PRICE]
- Launch date: [DATE]

- Development story: [BRIEF — how long in development, what inspired it]

Launch offer (if any): [EARLY BIRD DISCOUNT / FREE SHIPPING / GIFT WITH PURCHASE / NONE]
Limited quantity: [YES — HOW MANY / NO]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options (announcement, curiosity, exclusivity)

```

```

## Holiday / Seasonal Sale Campaigns

### Prompt 17: Black Friday / Cyber Monday Email Series

```
Write a 4-email Black Friday / Cyber Monday sequence for [STORE NAME].

Sale details:
- Discount: [PERCENTAGE / DOLLAR AMOUNT / SPECIFIC DEALS]
- Sale period: [START DATE] to [END DATE]
- Code (if needed): [CODE]
- Best deals: [TOP 3-5 PRODUCTS ON SALE WITH ORIGINAL AND SALE PRICES]
- Exclusions: [ANYTHING NOT INCLUDED]
- Free shipping threshold: [MINIMUM ORDER FOR FREE SHIPPING]

Email 1 — Early Access (Send: Day before Black Friday):
- Subject line: 5 options
- 150-180 words
- VIP early access framing for email subscribers
- Preview the best deals
- CTA: Shop Early Access

Email 2 — Black Friday Launch (Send: Black Friday morning):
- Subject line: 5 options
- 120-150 words
- Sale is officially live
- Highlight top 3 deals
- CTA: Shop the Sale

Email 3 — Reminder / Last Chance Black Friday (Send: Black Friday evening):
- Subject line: 5 options
- 80-100 words (urgency, short)
- Hours remaining countdown
- CTA: Don't Miss Out

Email 4 — Cyber Monday Extension (Send: Monday morning):
- Subject line: 5 options
- 120-150 words
- Extended sale or new online-only deals
- CTA: Final Day to Save

All emails should feel exciting but not desperate. Avoid ALL CAPS and excessive exclamation marks.
```

> **Why this works:** BFCM shoppers are overwhelmed with emails. Your edge is clarity and timing. Early access rewards loyalty. The reminder catches distracted shoppers. The Cyber Monday extension captures Monday-morning browsers. This 4-email framework typically drives 25-40% of annual email revenue.

## Review Request Emails

### Prompt 19: Review Request — Simple Ask

```
Write a review request email for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Customer purchased [PRODUCT NAME] approximately [X DAYS] ago
- Product has been delivered and used
- Goal: collect an honest review on your [WEBSITE / AMAZON / ETSY / GOOGLE] page

Review link: [DIRECT REVIEW LINK]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 5 options (personal, appreciative, easy ask)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 80-120 words
- Open with a check-in: "How are you enjoying your [PRODUCT]?"
- Ask directly but kindly for a review
- Emphasize it takes less than 2 minutes
- Explain why reviews matter (helps other shoppers + helps a small business)
- If offering an incentive: [DISCOUNT ON NEXT ORDER / LOYALTY POINTS / ENTRY INTO DRAWING / NONE]
- Make the review link prominent (big button)
- CTA: Leave a Review
- Thank them regardless of whether they review
```

### Prompt 20: Review Request — Photo/UGC Request

```
Write a review email that specifically asks for photo reviews for [STORE NAME].

Context:
- Customer purchased [PRODUCT NAME]
- You want reviews WITH photos (they convert 2-3x better than text-only reviews)
- Goal: collect visual social proof

Photo incentive: [FEATURED ON SOCIAL MEDIA / EXTRA DISCOUNT / LOYALTY POINTS / ENTRY INTO MONTHLY GIVEAWAY]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (focus on sharing, being featured)
- Preview text: 40-60 characters
- Body: 100-140 words
- Open with enthusiasm about seeing their [PRODUCT] "in the wild"
- Ask for a photo review with specific guidance:
  - "Show us your [PRODUCT] in your [HOME / ON YOU / IN USE]"
  - "Natural lighting works best"
  - "No need for perfection — we love real life photos"
- Explain the incentive for photo reviews
- If featuring on social, mention their handle could get tagged
- CTA: Share Your Photo Review
```

> **Pro tip:** Photo reviews increase product page conversion by 25-40%. Customers trust user-submitted photos far more than studio shots. The incentive doesn't need to be big — being featured on a brand's social media is often enough motivation.

```
Write a referral request email for [STORE NAME].

Referral program details:
- What the referrer gets: [DISCOUNT / CASH / STORE CREDIT / POINTS]
- What the referred friend gets: [DISCOUNT ON FIRST ORDER]
- How it works: [UNIQUE REFERRAL LINK / CODE / SHARE METHOD]

### Prompt 24: Subject Line Generator

```
Write 20 email subject lines for [STORE NAME] for the following email type: [EMAIL TYPE].

Email context:
- Purpose: [WHAT THE EMAIL IS ABOUT]
- Key offer or message: [THE MAIN THING TO COMMUNICATE]
- Audience: [WHO IS RECEIVING THIS]
- Urgency level: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH]

Write subject lines in these categories (4 each):
1. Curiosity — make them wonder what's inside
2. Benefit — lead with what they gain
3. Urgency — time-sensitive language
4. Personal — feels like it's from a friend
5. Social proof — what others are doing/buying

Requirements:
- Each subject line: 6-10 words max (under 50 characters ideal for mobile)
- No ALL CAPS words
- Use emojis sparingly (max 1 per subject line, and only on 5 of the 20)
- No spam trigger words (free, act now, limited time, buy now)
- Write a preview text (40-60 characters) to pair with the top 5 subject lines
```

> **Why this works:** Subject lines determine whether your email gets opened or deleted. Testing across categories (curiosity, benefit, urgency) lets you learn what YOUR audience responds to. Most e-commerce brands never test — that's your advantage.
