# 06: Headline & Hook Formulas

> 50+ proven formulas for blog titles, email subjects, social hooks, video titles, and newsletter headlines. Organized by psychological trigger.

---

## How to Use This File

1. **Find the right category** for your content goal (curiosity, fear, benefit, etc.)
2. **Pick a formula** and replace the `[BRACKETS]` with your topic
3. **Use the AI prompt at the bottom** to generate dozens of variations instantly

These formulas work across every platform. A great headline is a great headline whether it's a blog title, email subject, or tweet hook.

---

## Category 1: Curiosity Gap

The reader senses they're missing something. They MUST click to close the gap.

### Formulas

1. **The [UNEXPECTED THING] That Changed How I Think About [TOPIC]**
   Example: "The Spreadsheet That Changed How I Think About Content Strategy"

2. **I [DID SOMETHING] for [TIME PERIOD]. Here's What Actually Happened.**
   Example: "I Posted on LinkedIn Every Day for 90 Days. Here's What Actually Happened."

3. **Why [WELL-KNOWN PERSON/COMPANY] Stopped [COMMON PRACTICE]**
   Example: "Why Shopify Stopped Doing Meetings"

4. **The [TOPIC] Advice Nobody Gives You**
   Example: "The Newsletter Growth Advice Nobody Gives You"

5. **[NUMBER] [THINGS] You're Getting Wrong About [TOPIC]**
   Example: "5 Things You're Getting Wrong About SEO in 2026"

6. **What Happened When I [STOPPED/STARTED] [DOING SOMETHING]**
   Example: "What Happened When I Stopped Posting on Instagram"

7. **The Real Reason [SOMETHING] Isn't Working for You**
   Example: "The Real Reason Your Content Isn't Getting Views"

8. **I Asked [NUMBER] [EXPERTS/PEOPLE] About [TOPIC]. One Answer Changed Everything.**
   Example: "I Asked 50 Founders About Hiring. One Answer Changed Everything."

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Blog Post Titles
- Front-load keywords for SEO
- 60-70 characters max
- Include the year for evergreen topics ("... in 2026")
- Use "How to" and "Guide" for search intent

### Email Subject Lines
- Under 50 characters (under 35 for mobile)
- Lowercase often outperforms Title Case
- Questions and incomplete thoughts drive opens
- Personalization tokens ([First Name]) increase open rates 10-20%
- Never use ALL CAPS or multiple exclamation marks

### Twitter/X Hooks
- First 10 words determine everything
- Start with a bold claim, question, or number
- Leave a curiosity gap — don't give the answer in the hook
- Use "thread" or a down arrow to signal there's more

### YouTube Titles
- Under 60 characters (truncated on mobile)
- Include a number or timeframe when possible
- Pair the title with contrasting thumbnail text
- Front-load the most important words
- Avoid clickbait that the video can't deliver on — it kills retention

### Newsletter Headlines
- The subject line gets them to open; the headline keeps them reading
- Match the headline tone to the newsletter brand
- Bold and large — make it unmissable in the email body

## AI Headline Generator Prompt

Use this to generate unlimited headline variations on demand.

```
Generate 20 headline options for my content about [TOPIC].

CONTENT DETAILS:
- What the content covers: [BRIEF SUMMARY]
- Platform: [WHERE THIS WILL BE PUBLISHED]
- Target audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- Tone: [YOUR TONE]
- Content type: [BLOG POST / EMAIL / TWEET / VIDEO TITLE / NEWSLETTER]

REQUIREMENTS:
- Generate 4 headlines for each of these psychological triggers:
  1. Curiosity (create an information gap)
  2. Benefit (promise a specific outcome)
  3. Fear/Pain (highlight what they're losing or doing wrong)
  4. Social Proof (leverage authority, numbers, or examples)
  5. Contrarian (challenge a common belief)

- Each headline must be under [50/60/70] characters
- Include at least one headline with a number
- Include at least one headline phrased as a question
- Rate each headline 1-5 for: Click Potential, Clarity, and Niche Relevance
- Star (*) your top 3 recommendations
```

### Headline A/B Testing Prompt

```
I'm choosing between these headlines for my [CONTENT TYPE]:

OPTION A: "[HEADLINE A]"
OPTION B: "[HEADLINE B]"
OPTION C: "[HEADLINE C]" (optional)

My audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
Platform: [WHERE IT WILL BE PUBLISHED]
Goal: [CLICKS / OPENS / VIEWS / ENGAGEMENT]

For each option, analyze:
1. Psychological trigger used
2. Clarity — does the reader know what they'll get?
3. Curiosity — does it create a reason to click?
4. Emotional resonance — does it connect with the audience's pain/desire?
5. Platform fit — does it match how content performs on [PLATFORM]?

Then:
- Rank them from strongest to weakest
- Explain why #1 wins
- Suggest a HYBRID headline that combines the best elements of all options
```
