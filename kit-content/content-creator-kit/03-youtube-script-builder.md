## Section 1: YouTube Shorts Scripts (60-Second Format)

### Shorts Script — Hook + Value + CTA

```
Write a YouTube Shorts script (60 seconds max) about [TOPIC].

TARGET AUDIENCE: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
GOAL: [e.g., drive subscribers, promote a video, establish expertise, entertain]

STRICT FORMAT (must fit in 60 seconds when spoken):
- HOOK (0:00-0:05): One punchy sentence that stops the scroll. Use a bold claim, surprising fact, or direct question. The viewer decides to stay or leave in these 5 seconds.
- SETUP (0:05-0:15): Briefly explain why this matters or give context. Maximum 2 sentences.
- VALUE (0:15-0:45): Deliver 1-3 actionable points. Be specific. No fluff. Each point should be 1-2 sentences max. Use power words and concrete examples.
- CTA (0:45-0:60): Tell them exactly what to do next. "Follow for part 2" / "Comment [X] if you agree" / "Full breakdown on my channel."

RULES:
- Total word count: 130-160 words (that's roughly 60 seconds of speaking)
- Write for SPOKEN delivery — use contractions, short sentences, conversational language
- No jargon unless your audience expects it
- The hook must work with NO context (viewers won't read the title first)
- Include [PAUSE] markers where dramatic pauses would help delivery
- Include (visual note: ...) suggestions for on-screen text or B-roll

TONE: [YOUR TONE — e.g., high-energy and enthusiastic / calm and authoritative / casual and relatable]
NICHE: [YOUR NICHE]
```

### Shorts Script — Story Format

```
Write a 60-second YouTube Shorts script that tells a quick story about [TOPIC/EXPERIENCE].

THE STORY:
- What happened: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- The surprising or interesting part: [THE TWIST OR KEY MOMENT]
- The takeaway: [WHAT THE VIEWER LEARNS]

FORMAT:
- HOOK (0:00-0:05): Start IN the story. "So there I was..." or "This one mistake cost me..." — drop us in the middle of the action.
- STORY (0:05-0:40): Tell the story in 5-7 sentences. Build to the key moment.
- PAYOFF (0:40-0:50): Deliver the punchline, lesson, or twist.
- CTA (0:50-0:60): Tie the CTA to the story. "If you want to avoid the same mistake, follow for more."

RULES:
- 130-160 words total
- Use present tense to create immediacy ("I open my laptop and see...")
- Include sensory details — what you saw, felt, heard
- The story must have a clear beginning, middle, and end
- Include [PAUSE] markers and (visual note: ...) suggestions

TONE: [YOUR TONE]
```

## Section 2: Long-Form Video Scripts (10-15 Minutes)

### Full Script Generator

```
Write a complete YouTube video script for a [10/12/15]-minute video about [TOPIC].

VIDEO DETAILS:
- Title: [YOUR VIDEO TITLE — or ask me to generate one]
- Target audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
- Audience knowledge level: [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE/ADVANCED]
- Video style: [TALKING HEAD / TUTORIAL / ESSAY / DOCUMENTARY / LIST]
- My goal for this video: [e.g., educate, convert viewers to subscribers, promote a product, build authority]

KEY POINTS TO COVER:
1. [POINT 1]
2. [POINT 2]
3. [POINT 3]
4. [POINT 4]
5. [POINT 5]

SCRIPT STRUCTURE:

=== COLD OPEN / HOOK (0:00-0:30) ===
Write a hook that gives the viewer a REASON to watch the entire video. Use one of these formats:
- Bold claim + promise: "By the end of this video, you'll know [X]"
- Surprising stat or fact that creates a knowledge gap
- Before/after: "I went from [bad state] to [good state]. Here's how."
- Direct question that the viewer wants answered

=== INTRO (0:30-1:30) ===
- Quick context on why this topic matters RIGHT NOW
- Establish credibility (briefly — don't brag)
- Roadmap: Tell them what the video will cover (sets expectations, reduces drop-off)
- Pattern interrupt: Change the visual or energy here to maintain attention

=== SECTION 1 (1:30-4:00) ===
- Topic: [First key point]
- Open with a mini-hook for this section
- Explain the concept clearly
- Give a specific example or case study
- Transition to next section

=== SECTION 2 (4:00-6:30) ===
- Topic: [Second key point]
- Same structure as Section 1
- Include a contrarian angle or common mistake

=== MID-ROLL CTA (6:30-7:00) ===
- Natural transition to a subscribe/like ask
- Tie it to the content: "If you're finding this useful, subscribing helps me make more videos like this"
- Keep it under 20 seconds

=== SECTION 3 (7:00-9:30) ===
- Topic: [Third key point]
- This should be your BEST section — viewer retention drops after the midpoint, so deliver your highest-value content here
- Include an unexpected insight or a personal story

=== SECTION 4 (9:30-11:30) === [Include only for 12-15 min videos]
- Topic: [Fourth key point]
- Practical, actionable content
- Step-by-step if applicable

=== CONCLUSION (Last 1-2 minutes) ===
- Quick recap of the key takeaways (3-5 bullet points)
- One clear, memorable final statement
- Tease the next video or related content
- Final CTA: Subscribe, comment with their answer to a question, check out [related video]

SCRIPT RULES:
- Write word-for-word dialogue, not bullet points. This should be ready to read on camera.
- Include (B-ROLL: ...) suggestions throughout
- Include (ON SCREEN: ...) for text overlays or graphics
- Include (CUT TO: ...) for visual changes
- Write for speaking — use contractions, rhetorical questions, direct address ("you")
- Target [1,500/1,800/2,200] words for a [10/12/15]-minute video
- Include pattern interrupts every 2-3 minutes to maintain attention
- Add emphasis markers for key phrases: **bold text** = stress this word

TONE: [YOUR TONE]
CHANNEL NAME: [YOUR CHANNEL]
```

### Script Improvement Prompt

```
I have a draft YouTube script. Improve it for better retention and engagement.

ANALYZE AND IMPROVE:
1. HOOK — Is the first 30 seconds compelling enough? If not, rewrite it.
2. PACING — Identify spots where the script drags. Tighten them.
3. RETENTION — Add pattern interrupts (visual change suggestions, tonal shifts, questions) every 2-3 minutes.
4. CLARITY — Simplify any section that's confusing or overly complex.
5. EMOTION — Add moments that create an emotional response (surprise, curiosity, humor, empathy).
6. CTA — Is the call to action natural and compelling? Improve it.

MY DRAFT SCRIPT:
"""
[PASTE YOUR SCRIPT]
"""

Output the improved script with your changes clearly marked in [EDITOR'S NOTE: ...] tags so I can see what you changed and why.
```

## Section 3: Video Title + Thumbnail Text Generator

### Title Generator

```
Generate 10 YouTube title options for a video about [TOPIC].

VIDEO CONTENT SUMMARY: [2-3 SENTENCES ABOUT WHAT THE VIDEO COVERS]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
COMPETITOR TITLES THAT PERFORM WELL IN MY NICHE: [LIST 3-5 EXAMPLES — optional]

TITLE REQUIREMENTS:
- Under 60 characters (YouTube truncates longer titles on mobile)
- Must create a curiosity gap or promise a clear benefit
- Use power words: secret, mistake, actually, truth, proven, exactly, how
- Include a number if it's a list video
- Front-load the most important words (first 3-4 words matter most)

Generate titles in these categories:
- 2 "How to" titles
- 2 "Mistake/Problem" titles (e.g., "Why Your [X] Isn't Working")
- 2 "Number/List" titles (e.g., "7 [Things] That [Result]")
- 2 "Curiosity gap" titles (e.g., "I Tried [X] for 30 Days")
- 2 "Bold claim" titles (e.g., "The Only [X] Strategy You Need")

For each title, rate it:
- Curiosity (1-5): Does it make people NEED to click?
- Clarity (1-5): Can people immediately understand the video's topic?
- Recommended: Yes/No
```

### Thumbnail Text Generator

```
Generate thumbnail text options for a YouTube video titled: "[YOUR VIDEO TITLE]"

RULES FOR YOUTUBE THUMBNAILS:
- Maximum 4-6 words (must be readable on a phone screen)
- The text should ADD to the thumbnail image, not repeat the title
- Use contrast: the text should convey a different angle or emotion than the title
- All caps for key words, mixed case for others
- Create visual tension or curiosity

Generate 5 options, each with:
1. The text (4-6 words max)
2. Suggested text color/style (e.g., red bold, yellow highlight, white with shadow)
3. Suggested facial expression or image concept
4. Why this combination works

EXAMPLES OF GREAT THUMBNAIL TEXT:
- Title: "I Quit My 6-Figure Job" → Thumbnail text: "WORST MISTAKE?"
- Title: "The Algorithm Changed Again" → Thumbnail text: "we're cooked"
- Title: "How I Got 100K Subscribers" → Thumbnail text: "FREE method"
```

```
Write an optimized YouTube description for a video titled: "[YOUR VIDEO TITLE]"

VIDEO SUMMARY: [2-3 SENTENCES ABOUT THE VIDEO]
KEY TOPICS COVERED: [LIST 5-7 TOPICS/KEYWORDS]
LINKS TO INCLUDE:
- [LINK 1 — e.g., free resource, product, affiliate link]
- [LINK 2]
- [LINK 3]
MY SOCIAL LINKS: [LIST YOUR PLATFORMS AND URLS]

DESCRIPTION STRUCTURE:
LINE 1-2: Strong opening that includes the primary keyword naturally. This is what shows in search results — make it count.

LINE 3-4: Brief description of what the viewer will learn. Use the word "you" to make it personal.

TIMESTAMPS:
0:00 — [Section name]
[Generate timestamps based on a typical script structure]

KEY LINKS:
[Format the provided links with context]

ABOUT THIS CHANNEL:
[2 sentences about the channel — include primary keywords]

SOCIAL LINKS:
[Formatted social links]

TAGS SECTION (invisible SEO):
[Include 5-8 relevant hashtags at the bottom]

RULES:
- Front-load primary keywords in the first 2 sentences
- Include 2-3 secondary keywords naturally in the body
- The first 150 characters are the most important (search preview)
- Keep total length between 200-500 words
- Include at least 5 timestamps
```

## Section 5: Hook Writing (First 30 Seconds)

### Hook Generator

```
Write 5 different hooks (first 30 seconds) for a YouTube video about [TOPIC].

VIDEO TITLE: [YOUR TITLE]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [YOUR AUDIENCE]
THE MAIN VALUE OF THIS VIDEO: [WHAT THE VIEWER WILL GET]

HOOK STYLES — write one of each:

1. THE BOLD CLAIM HOOK
Start with a surprising or bold statement that challenges what the viewer believes. Follow with "and in this video, I'll prove it."

2. THE STORY HOOK
Drop the viewer into a moment of tension or curiosity. "Three months ago, I was [situation]..." Build to a cliffhanger, then pivot: "Let me show you exactly what happened."

3. THE QUESTION HOOK
Open with a question the viewer desperately wants answered. Follow with a quick tease of the answer. "The answer surprised me. And it'll probably surprise you too."

4. THE PROOF HOOK
Open with a result, screenshot, or specific number. "This is my [analytics/revenue/growth] from last month. [Number]. And I'm going to show you the exact system behind it."

5. THE CONTRARIAN HOOK
Challenge popular advice in your niche. "Everyone says you should [common advice]. They're wrong. Here's what actually works."

RULES FOR ALL HOOKS:
- Must be speakable in under 30 seconds (60-80 words max)
- The FIRST SENTENCE must work even without context
- Must create a reason to watch the REST of the video
- Write for spoken delivery — conversational, direct, energetic
- Include (visual note: ...) suggestions for what should be on screen
- End each hook with a smooth transition into the video body

TONE: [YOUR TONE]
```

### Hook A/B Test Generator

```
I'm making a video titled "[YOUR TITLE]" and I need to test different hooks.

Write 3 variations of the opening hook, each taking a COMPLETELY different angle:

VERSION A — EMOTIONAL ANGLE
Lead with a feeling, frustration, or desire that the audience relates to.

VERSION B — LOGICAL ANGLE
Lead with data, a statistic, or a rational argument.

VERSION C — SOCIAL PROOF ANGLE
Lead with what other successful people/companies are doing.

For each version, provide:
- The hook script (60-80 words, under 30 seconds)
- Why this angle might resonate
- What type of viewer it will attract
- Estimated retention strength (weak/moderate/strong opening)

I'll film all 3 and test them to see which performs best.
```

## Production Checklists

### Pre-Production Prompt

```
I'm planning a YouTube video about [TOPIC]. Act as my creative producer and help me prepare.

Generate:
1. RESEARCH QUESTIONS: 5 things I should research before scripting
2. UNIQUE ANGLE: What angle on this topic hasn't been covered by other creators?
3. VISUAL PLAN: 5 B-roll shots or on-screen graphics I should prepare
4. PROPS/SETUP: Anything I should have on camera
5. COMPETITOR GAPS: Based on what you know about YouTube content on [TOPIC], what's missing from existing videos?
```

### Post-Upload Prompt

```
My video "[TITLE]" just went live. Help me maximize its reach in the first 48 hours.

Generate:
1. 3 community post ideas to drive views from existing subscribers
2. A tweet announcing the video (with a hook, not just "new video!")
3. An Instagram story caption directing to the video
4. 2 relevant subreddits where I could share the content (with Reddit-native post drafts)
5. A newsletter snippet announcing the video to my email list
6. 3 comment responses I should pin to boost engagement
```
