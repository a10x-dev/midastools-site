Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Category: [PRODUCT CATEGORY]
What it does: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Target user: [PRIMARY PERSONA — role, company size, pain point]
Key competitors: [LIST 2-4 COMPETITORS]

## SEO Blog Post Outlines

### Bottom-of-Funnel Blog Post (High Intent)

```
Create a detailed SEO blog post outline for [PRODUCT NAME]'s blog.

Target keyword: [PRIMARY KEYWORD — e.g., "best project management tool for remote teams"]
Search intent: Bottom-of-funnel (reader is close to buying)
Target audience: [WHO IS SEARCHING THIS]
Word count target: [1,500-2,500 WORDS]

Requirements:
- Title: Write 5 title options optimized for the keyword (include the keyword in each)
- Meta description: 155 characters, includes keyword and CTA
- URL slug suggestion
- Introduction (3-4 sentences): Hook, establish credibility, preview the article
- H2 and H3 outline with 8-12 sections
- For each section: 2-3 bullet points describing what to cover
- Include these elements:
  - A "What is [TOPIC]?" section for featured snippet potential
  - A comparison or "how to choose" section
  - A section where [PRODUCT NAME] is naturally positioned as a solution
  - FAQ section with 5 questions (target People Also Ask)
- Internal link suggestions: [LIST 2-3 EXISTING BLOG POSTS TO LINK TO]
- CTA placement: Where to include product CTAs naturally (not forced)

SEO checklist:
- Primary keyword in title, H1, first paragraph, and 2-3 H2s
- Secondary keywords: [LIST 3-5 RELATED KEYWORDS TO INCLUDE]
- Image alt text suggestions for 3-5 images
```

> **Why this works:** Bottom-of-funnel content targets users who are actively evaluating solutions. These posts have lower traffic but dramatically higher conversion rates. One good BOFU post can outperform 10 top-of-funnel articles in revenue impact.

### Top-of-Funnel Blog Post (Awareness)

```
Create a top-of-funnel SEO blog post outline for [PRODUCT NAME]'s blog.

Target keyword: [PRIMARY KEYWORD — e.g., "how to manage remote teams effectively"]
Search intent: Informational (reader is learning, not buying yet)
Target audience: [WHO IS SEARCHING THIS]
Word count target: [2,000-3,000 WORDS]

Requirements:
- Title: 5 options (include keyword, make it compelling to click)
- Meta description: 155 characters
- URL slug suggestion
- Comprehensive outline with 10-15 sections (H2/H3)
- For each section: 2-3 bullet points describing the content
- Include:
  - Actionable advice the reader can use immediately (even without your product)
  - Data points or statistics to cite (suggest the types of data to find)
  - A subtle mention of [PRODUCT NAME] in 1-2 sections (not forced)
  - An email capture opportunity (content upgrade, newsletter signup)
  - Expert quotes section (suggest types of experts to reach out to)
- FAQ section with 5 questions targeting People Also Ask
- Content upgrade idea: [RELATED DOWNLOADABLE — checklist, template, guide]

SEO checklist:
- Primary keyword density guidance
- Secondary keywords: [5-8 RELATED KEYWORDS]
- Suggested internal and external links
```

### Programmatic SEO Template

```
Design a programmatic SEO page template for [PRODUCT NAME].

Concept: Create a template that can generate [HUNDREDS/THOUSANDS] of pages targeting long-tail keywords.

Page type: [CHOOSE ONE]:
- "[PRODUCT NAME] for [INDUSTRY]" (e.g., "Acme for Healthcare", "Acme for E-commerce")
- "[PRODUCT NAME] vs [COMPETITOR]" (e.g., "Acme vs Notion", "Acme vs Monday")
- "[USE CASE] with [PRODUCT NAME]" (e.g., "Sprint Planning with Acme")
- "[INTEGRATION] + [PRODUCT NAME]" (e.g., "Slack + Acme Integration")
- "Best [CATEGORY] for [ROLE]" (e.g., "Best CRM for Freelancers")

For the chosen page type, create:
1. Title tag template with variable slots
2. Meta description template
3. H1 template
4. Page sections (5-8 sections) with templates for each
5. Dynamic content areas (what changes per page)
6. Static content areas (what stays the same)
7. Internal linking strategy across programmatic pages
8. Schema markup suggestions
9. 20 example page titles using this template

Requirements:
- Each generated page must provide genuine value (not thin content)
- Include unique data, comparisons, or insights per page variation
- Explain how to source or generate the variable content
```

### Content Cluster Strategy

```
Design a content cluster strategy for [PRODUCT NAME] around the topic of [CORE TOPIC].

Core topic: [MAIN TOPIC — e.g., "project management for startups"]
Product relevance: [HOW THIS CONNECTS TO YOUR PRODUCT]
Target audience: [WHO CARES ABOUT THIS TOPIC]

Create:
1. Pillar page outline:
   - Title and URL
   - 3,000+ word comprehensive guide outline
   - 10-12 major sections
   - Where to link to cluster articles

2. 10 cluster articles:
   For each article provide:
   - Title (keyword-optimized)
   - Target keyword
   - Search intent (informational, navigational, transactional)
   - Brief outline (3-5 sections)
   - How it links back to the pillar page
   - Where [PRODUCT NAME] naturally fits

3. Suggested publishing schedule (which to write first based on keyword difficulty and business value)
4. Internal linking map showing how all pieces connect
5. Content upgrade for the pillar page (lead magnet idea)
```

> **Pro tip:** Content clusters signal topical authority to Google. A well-executed cluster (1 pillar + 8-12 supporting articles) can lift your entire domain's ranking for the core topic. Always publish the cluster articles first, then the pillar page that links to them all.

## Comparison Page Templates

### [Product] vs [Competitor] Page

```
Write a comparison page: [PRODUCT NAME] vs [COMPETITOR NAME].

Context:
- Your product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
- Competitor: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION OF COMPETITOR]
- Your key advantages: [LIST 3-5 THINGS YOU DO BETTER]
- Competitor's advantages: [LIST 2-3 THINGS THEY DO BETTER — be honest]
- Your pricing: [PLANS AND PRICES]
- Competitor pricing: [THEIR PLANS AND PRICES]
- Target audience for this page: [WHO'S COMPARING THESE TWO]

Write:
1. Page title (SEO-optimized: "[PRODUCT NAME] vs [COMPETITOR] — [YEAR] Comparison")
2. Meta description (155 characters)
3. Introduction: Acknowledge both products fairly (3-4 sentences)
4. Feature comparison table:
   - [LIST 8-12 FEATURES TO COMPARE]
   - For each: Rate as "Yes / No / Partial / Superior" for each product
5. "Where [PRODUCT NAME] wins" section (3 specific advantages with explanations)
6. "Where [COMPETITOR] wins" section (1-2 honest advantages — builds credibility)
7. "Who should choose [PRODUCT NAME]" section (ideal user profile)
8. "Who should choose [COMPETITOR]" section (when competitor is genuinely better)
9. Pricing comparison section
10. Verdict / recommendation section
11. CTA: Try [PRODUCT NAME] free

Requirements:
- Be honest — readers can smell biased comparison pages
- Include specific details, not vague claims
- Mention concrete use cases, not just features
- The page should rank for "[PRODUCT NAME] vs [COMPETITOR]" search queries
```

> **Why this works:** Comparison pages capture high-intent traffic from users actively choosing between you and a competitor. Honest, detailed comparisons build trust. If someone is comparing, they're close to buying — your job is to help them decide (even if the answer isn't always you).

### Competitor Alternative Page

```
Write an "[COMPETITOR] Alternative" page for [PRODUCT NAME].

Target keyword: "[COMPETITOR NAME] alternative" or "best [COMPETITOR] alternatives"
Context:
- Why people look for alternatives: [COMMON COMPLAINTS ABOUT COMPETITOR — pricing, complexity, missing features]
- Your positioning: [HOW YOU SOLVE THOSE COMPLAINTS]
- Other alternatives to mention: [LIST 2-3 OTHER COMPETITORS FOR FAIRNESS]

Write:
1. SEO-optimized title (include "[COMPETITOR] alternative")
2. Meta description
3. Introduction: Why people are looking for alternatives (validate their frustration without bashing)
4. "Why people switch from [COMPETITOR]" — 3-5 common reasons with detail
5. "[PRODUCT NAME] as an alternative" — detailed section showing how you address each reason
6. "Other alternatives worth considering" — brief, fair mention of 2-3 others
7. Migration section: "How to switch from [COMPETITOR] to [PRODUCT NAME]" (3-5 steps)
8. Feature comparison table
9. Pricing comparison
10. FAQ: 5 questions about switching
11. CTA: Start free trial / see a demo

Requirements:
- Never be petty or personal about the competitor
- Acknowledge what they do well
- Focus on specific pain points you genuinely solve
- Include migration support details (reduces switching friction)
```

### Category Comparison Round-Up

```
Write a "[PRODUCT CATEGORY] Tools Compared" round-up page for [PRODUCT NAME]'s blog.

Target keyword: "best [PRODUCT CATEGORY] tools [YEAR]"
Products to include: [LIST 5-8 PRODUCTS INCLUDING YOUR OWN]

For each product, write:
1. Product name and one-sentence description
2. Best for: [SPECIFIC USE CASE OR USER TYPE]
3. Key features (3-5 bullets)
4. Pricing overview
5. Pros (2-3)
6. Cons (1-2 — be fair even for your own product)
7. Rating or recommendation level

Also include:
- Introduction: How to choose the right [CATEGORY] tool (buying criteria)
- Comparison table with all products across key features
- "Our pick" section (your product, with honest positioning)
- FAQ section (5 questions about choosing)
- Methodology section explaining how you evaluated

Requirements:
- Place your product 1st or 2nd in the list (readers expect it on your blog)
- Be genuinely helpful — if another tool is better for a specific use case, say so
- Include screenshots or video suggestions for each product
- This should be 3,000+ words to compete for the keyword
```

## Case Study Prompts

### Case Study Interview Questions

```
Generate case study interview questions for a [PRODUCT NAME] customer.

Customer context:
- Company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Role of interviewee: [THEIR TITLE]
- How they use the product: [PRIMARY USE CASE]
- Result: [KEY OUTCOME — metric, time saved, revenue impact]
- Time as customer: [DURATION]

Generate 15-20 interview questions organized in 4 sections:

Section 1 — Before [PRODUCT NAME]:
- What was the problem? (5 questions)

Section 2 — Evaluation & Decision:
- Why did they choose you? (3-4 questions)

Section 3 — Implementation & Usage:
- How do they use it day-to-day? (4-5 questions)

Section 4 — Results & Impact:
- What changed? (4-5 questions)

Requirements:
- Questions should be open-ended (no yes/no)
- Include follow-up probes for key questions
- Focus on extracting specific numbers and stories
- Include a "pull quote" question designed to get a quotable line
- Final question: "What would you tell someone considering [PRODUCT NAME]?"
```

### Case Study Write-Up

```
Write a case study for [PRODUCT NAME] based on the following interview notes.

Customer: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Interviewee: [NAME, TITLE]
Product used: [PRODUCT NAME] [PLAN NAME]
Time as customer: [DURATION]

Interview notes / key points:
- Problem before: [DESCRIBE THE PAIN POINT IN DETAIL]
- Why they chose us: [DECISION FACTORS]
- How they use it: [DAILY/WEEKLY WORKFLOW]
- Key result: [PRIMARY METRIC — e.g., "saved 15 hours/week", "increased conversion by 32%"]
- Secondary results: [OTHER OUTCOMES]
- Favorite feature: [SPECIFIC FEATURE AND WHY]
- Quote: [DIRECT QUOTE FROM CUSTOMER]

Write the case study in this format:
1. Headline: Result-focused (e.g., "How [COMPANY] [ACHIEVED RESULT] with [PRODUCT NAME]")
2. Quick stats sidebar: 3 key metrics in large format
3. Company overview (2-3 sentences)
4. The Challenge (200-300 words)
5. The Solution (200-300 words — how they implemented and use the product)
6. The Results (200-300 words — specific metrics and outcomes)
7. Pull quote (formatted prominently)
8. What's next (1-2 sentences about future plans with the product)
9. CTA: "Get similar results — start your free trial"

Total: 800-1,200 words
Tone: Professional storytelling, let the customer's voice shine through
```

> **Pro tip:** The best case studies read like stories, not testimonials. The "before" should make readers nod in recognition of their own pain. The "after" should make the result feel achievable, not miraculous.

```
Condense the following case study into a one-page sales enablement document for [PRODUCT NAME].

Full case study details:

- Results: [KEY METRICS]

Write a one-page document with:
1. Headline (result-focused, 8-12 words)
2. Customer snapshot: Company, industry, size, plan (formatted as a sidebar)

4. Solution section (3-4 sentences with 2-3 bullet points of features used)

- Must fit on one page when printed or viewed as PDF
- Scannable in 30 seconds — use bold, bullets, and white space

- Total copy: Under 300 words
```

## Feature Announcement Copy

### Major Feature Launch

```
Write copy for a major feature launch for [PRODUCT NAME].

Feature: [FEATURE NAME]
What it does: [DETAILED DESCRIPTION]
Why you built it: [USER REQUESTS, MARKET GAP, STRATEGIC REASON]
Who benefits most: [USER SEGMENT]
How to access it: [WHERE IN THE APP + ANY REQUIREMENTS]
Pricing impact: [INCLUDED IN ALL PLANS / PAID ADD-ON / HIGHER TIERS ONLY]

Write these assets:

1. Email announcement:
   - Subject line: 3 options (benefit, new, curiosity)
   - Body: 150-250 words
   - Structure: Problem > Announcement > How it works > CTA

2. Blog post outline:
   - Title (3 options)
   - 5-8 section outline
   - Key screenshots or visual suggestions

3. In-app announcement:
   - Banner: Headline (5-8 words) + body (one sentence) + CTA button
   - Modal: Headline + 3 bullet points + CTA + dismiss

4. Social media (3 platforms):
   - Twitter/X: 280-character announcement
   - LinkedIn: 200-300 word post
   - Product Hunt: 100-word "Maker Update" post

5. Changelog entry:
   - Title, category tag, 3-5 sentence description
```

### Minor Feature Update

```
Write copy for a minor feature improvement in [PRODUCT NAME].

Feature updated: [FEATURE NAME]
What changed: [SPECIFIC CHANGE — new option, performance improvement, UI update]
Why: [BRIEF REASON — user feedback, bug fix, optimization]
Who it affects: [ALL USERS / SPECIFIC PLAN / SPECIFIC WORKFLOW]

Write:
1. In-app toast notification: One sentence (under 15 words)
2. Changelog entry: Title + 2-3 sentences
3. Release notes bullet point: One line
4. Support article update suggestion: What to add/change in existing docs
```

### Feature Deprecation Notice

```
Write a feature deprecation notice for [PRODUCT NAME].

Feature being deprecated: [FEATURE NAME]
Why: [HONEST REASON — replaced by something better, low usage, technical debt]
Replacement: [NEW FEATURE OR WORKFLOW THAT REPLACES IT]
Timeline: [DEPRECATION DATE]
Migration path: [HOW USERS TRANSITION — automatic, manual steps needed]
Users affected: [NUMBER OR PERCENTAGE]

Write:
1. Email to affected users:
   - Subject line: 3 options (direct, helpful, action-needed)
   - Body: 200-300 words
   - Explain what's changing and why
   - Detail the migration path step by step
   - Emphasize the improvement (new > old)
   - Offer help: support email, migration guide link, 1:1 call booking
   - Timeline: Clear dates for deprecation

2. In-app banner (for affected users):
   - Headline + body + "Learn more" link + dismiss option

3. Documentation page outline:
   - Migration guide structure

Requirements:
- Be transparent about why
- Never minimize the impact on affected users
- Provide ample notice (minimum 30 days for significant features)
- Make migration as painless as possible
```

## Changelog & Release Notes

### Weekly Changelog Entry

```
Write a changelog entry for [PRODUCT NAME]'s weekly update.

Changes this week:
- New: [LIST NEW FEATURES]
- Improved: [LIST IMPROVEMENTS]
- Fixed: [LIST BUG FIXES]

Requirements:
- Title: "Week of [DATE] — [CATCHY SUMMARY]"
- Organize by: New Features, Improvements, Bug Fixes
- For new features: 3-5 sentence description with benefit
- For improvements: 1-2 sentences each
- For bug fixes: One line each
- Use action verbs: "Added", "Improved", "Fixed", "Removed"
- Tag each item: [New] [Improved] [Fixed]
- Include one highlight callout for the most important change
- Keep total under 300 words
```

### Monthly Product Update

```
Write a monthly product update email for [PRODUCT NAME].

Month: [MONTH, YEAR]
Key highlights:
1. [BIGGEST LAUNCH OR CHANGE]
2. [SECOND BIGGEST UPDATE]
3. [THIRD UPDATE]

Additional changes: [LIST 3-5 SMALLER UPDATES]
Upcoming preview: [1-2 THINGS COMING NEXT MONTH]
Metrics to share: [USER GROWTH, UPTIME, FEATURES SHIPPED — whatever you're comfortable sharing]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (recap, what's new, progress)
- Opening: One sentence on the month's theme or biggest win
- Highlight section: 2-3 sentences per major update, each with a benefit focus
- "Also shipped" section: Bullet list of smaller updates
- "Coming soon" teaser: 2-3 sentences
- Optional: Share a company metric that builds transparency and trust
- CTA: "Explore what's new" or link to full changelog
- 200-300 words
- Tone: Proud but humble, building-in-public energy
```

> **Pro tip:** Monthly product updates are one of the highest-engaging emails you can send. Users want to know the product they're paying for is actively improving. Sharing metrics (features shipped, uptime, growth) builds trust and reduces churn.

### Release Notes Template

```
Write release notes for [PRODUCT NAME] version [VERSION NUMBER].

Release date: [DATE]
Release type: [MAJOR / MINOR / PATCH]

Changes:
- [CHANGE 1 — type: new/improved/fixed, description]
- [CHANGE 2 — type: new/improved/fixed, description]
- [CHANGE 3 — type: new/improved/fixed, description]
- [CHANGE 4 — type: new/improved/fixed, description]
- [CHANGE 5 — type: new/improved/fixed, description]

Write in two formats:

Format 1 — Developer/Technical:
- Use technical language
- Include API changes, breaking changes, migration notes
- List dependencies updated
- Note any environment requirements

Format 2 — Customer/Non-Technical:
- Use benefit-focused language
- Explain what changed in plain English
- Focus on what users can now do differently
- Skip internal/technical changes users don't see
```

## Webinar & Demo Scripts

### Product Demo Script

```
Write a product demo script for [PRODUCT NAME].

Demo context:
- Audience: [WHO — prospects, new users, specific segment]
- Duration: [LENGTH — 15 min, 30 min, 45 min]
- Goal: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO AFTER — sign up, upgrade, buy]
- Key features to show: [LIST 4-6 FEATURES IN ORDER]
- Common objections to address: [LIST 2-3]

Script structure:
1. Opening hook (30 seconds): Start with the PROBLEM, not the product
2. Agenda preview (15 seconds): Tell them what they'll see
3. Feature demonstrations:
   For each feature:
   - Setup: The scenario or problem (15-30 seconds)
   - Demo: What to show and click (describe the actions)
   - Payoff: The result and benefit (15-30 seconds)
   - Transition: Bridge to next feature
4. Social proof moment: Customer quote or metric (30 seconds)
5. Pricing overview: Brief, confident, value-framed (60 seconds)
6. Q&A transition: How to invite questions
7. Closing CTA: Clear next step

Include:
- Suggested talk track for each section (what to SAY while showing)
- "If they ask..." responses for 5 common questions
- Technical backup plan: What to do if a live demo fails
```

### Webinar Presentation Outline

```
Write a webinar outline for [PRODUCT NAME].

Webinar topic: [TOPIC — should be educational, not a product pitch]
Target audience: [WHO WILL ATTEND]
Duration: [30 / 45 / 60 MINUTES]
Goal: [EDUCATE AND GENERATE LEADS / CONVERT TRIAL USERS / SHOWCASE NEW FEATURE]
Co-presenter: [GUEST SPEAKER NAME AND TITLE, OR "SOLO"]

Write:

1. Webinar title: 3 options (curiosity, benefit, contrarian)
2. Landing page description: 150 words explaining what attendees will learn
3. 3 bullet points for "What you'll learn" section

4. Full outline:
   - Opening (3-5 min): Hook, introduce speakers, set expectations
   - Section 1 (10-15 min): [EDUCATIONAL CONTENT — framework, strategy, or insights]
   - Section 2 (10-15 min): [DEEPER DIVE — examples, data, or case study]
   - Section 3 (5-10 min): [PRODUCT TIE-IN — how [PRODUCT NAME] helps, live demo]
   - Q&A (10-15 min): Facilitation tips
   - Closing (2-3 min): Recap, CTA, next steps

5. Talk track snippets: Key transition phrases between sections
6. Engagement tactics: 3 polls or interactive moments to include
7. Follow-up email: For attendees (100 words) and no-shows (80 words)
```

### Customer Webinar (Feature Deep-Dive)

```
Write a customer webinar outline for existing [PRODUCT NAME] users.

Topic: Deep dive into [FEATURE NAME]
Audience: Current users who want to get more from the product
Duration: [30 / 45 MINUTES]
Presenter: [NAME, TITLE]

Write:
1. Webinar title (3 options — learning-focused)
2. Invite email copy (100-150 words)
3. Outline:
   - Overview: What [FEATURE] does and why it matters (5 min)
   - Basic usage: Getting started walk-through (10 min)
   - Advanced techniques: 3 power-user workflows (15 min)
   - Live Q&A: Audience questions (10 min)
   - Bonus: A template or workflow they can copy and use immediately
4. 5 poll questions to ask during the webinar
5. Follow-up email with recording link and key resources (100 words)
```

### Quarterly Content Plan

```
Create a quarterly content plan for [PRODUCT NAME].

Quarter: [Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 YEAR]
Goals: [LIST 2-3 MARKETING GOALS — e.g., "increase organic traffic 50%", "generate 200 MQLs", "launch in new market"]

Top-performing content: [LIST 2-3 BEST POSTS AND WHY THEY WORK]
Content team size: [NUMBER OF WRITERS/MARKETERS]

Create a plan with:
1. Content themes for each month (aligned with goals and product roadmap)
2. 12 blog posts (4 per month):

3. 2 comparison pages
4. 1 pillar page / comprehensive guide
5. 3 case studies
6. 1 webinar
7. Publishing calendar with dates and owners
8. Distribution plan: Where to promote each piece

```

```
Create a content repurposing plan for [PRODUCT NAME].

Generate a plan to repurpose this into:
1. Twitter/X thread (7-10 tweets)
2. LinkedIn post (200-300 words)
3. Short-form video script (60-90 seconds for TikTok/Reels/Shorts)
4. Email newsletter edition (150-200 words)
5. Infographic outline (5-7 data points or steps to visualize)
6. Podcast talking points (if you have a podcast)
7. Community post for [PLATFORM — Reddit, Indie Hackers, Hacker News]

For each format:
- What angle to lead with (can be different per platform)
- Key points to include
- CTA for that platform
- Best time to publish
```
