## Set Your Launch Context

Paste this at the start of every AI session before using any prompt below:

```
You are a SaaS launch strategist helping me plan and execute my product launch.

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Category: [PRODUCT CATEGORY — e.g., project management, analytics, DevOps]
One-liner: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION OF WHAT IT DOES]
Target user: [PRIMARY PERSONA — role, company size, pain point]
Key differentiator: [WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT FROM EXISTING SOLUTIONS]
Pricing: [PRICING MODEL — free trial, freemium, paid only, etc.]
Launch date: [TARGET LAUNCH DATE]
Current stage: [BETA / PRIVATE ALPHA / PRE-LAUNCH / SOFT LAUNCH]
Website: [URL]

Write all copy in a [BRAND VOICE — e.g., developer-friendly, professional, conversational] tone.
```

## Product Hunt Launch

### Product Hunt Tagline Generator

```
Generate 10 Product Hunt taglines for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product description: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Target user: [WHO THIS IS FOR]
Key benefit: [PRIMARY VALUE PROPOSITION]

Requirements:
- Each tagline must be under 60 characters
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Avoid jargon unless targeting a technical audience
- Mix styles: straightforward, clever, question-based, comparison-based
- Rank them from strongest to weakest with a brief explanation of why
```

> **Why this works:** Product Hunt taglines are the first thing people see. You get 60 characters to convince someone to click. Leading with benefit over feature increases click-through rates by 2-3x based on top-performing launches.

### Product Hunt Description

```
Write a Product Hunt launch description for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product details:
- What it does: [DETAILED DESCRIPTION — 2-3 sentences]
- Problem it solves: [THE PAIN POINT YOUR USERS FACE]
- How it works: [3 KEY STEPS OR FEATURES]
- Who it's for: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
- Pricing: [FREE TIER / TRIAL / LAUNCH OFFER]
- Tech stack or integrations: [ANY NOTABLE INTEGRATIONS]

Requirements:
- Write a compelling first paragraph that hooks readers (this shows in preview)
- Use short paragraphs and bullet points for scannability
- Include a "Why we built this" section with founder backstory
- List 3-5 key features with brief descriptions
- End with a launch-day special offer or CTA
- Keep total length 300-500 words
- Tone: [BRAND VOICE]
```

### Product Hunt First Comment (Maker Comment)

```
Write a Product Hunt maker comment for [PRODUCT NAME] launch.

Context:
- Why I built this: [YOUR PERSONAL MOTIVATION OR PAIN POINT]
- How long it took: [DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE]
- Current status: [BETA USERS / TRACTION / METRICS IF COMFORTABLE SHARING]
- What's next on the roadmap: [2-3 UPCOMING FEATURES]
- Launch offer: [ANY SPECIAL PRICING OR ACCESS FOR PH COMMUNITY]

Requirements:
- Write in first person, authentic founder voice
- Open with the personal story behind why this exists
- Be transparent about where the product is and what's coming
- Include a specific ask (feedback, upvotes, trying the product)
- Mention you'll be in the comments all day answering questions
- 200-350 words
- Warm, humble, and genuine tone — not salesy
```

> **Pro tip:** The maker comment is often more important than the description. Authentic founder stories consistently outperform polished marketing copy on Product Hunt. Share your genuine motivation and be ready to engage with every comment on launch day.

### Product Hunt Comment Replies

```
Generate 10 reply templates for common Product Hunt launch comments for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Key features: [LIST 3-5 FEATURES]
Pricing: [PRICING DETAILS]
Competitor landscape: [2-3 MAIN COMPETITORS]

Write replies for these comment types:
1. "How is this different from [COMPETITOR]?"
2. "Does this integrate with [TOOL]?"
3. "What's the pricing?"
4. "This looks great! Congrats on the launch!"
5. "I've been looking for something like this"
6. "Can this handle [SPECIFIC USE CASE]?"
7. "Is there an API?"
8. "What's your tech stack?"
9. "Will you add [FEATURE] in the future?"
10. "I tried it and found a bug / have feedback"

Requirements:
- Each reply should be 2-4 sentences
- Be helpful, specific, and gracious
- Never be defensive about competitor comparisons
- Turn feature requests into roadmap conversations
- Include a CTA where natural (try it, check docs, etc.)
```

## Beta Launch Email Sequences

### Beta Invite Email

```
Write a beta invite email for [PRODUCT NAME].

Context:
- Recipient: [HOW THEY SIGNED UP — waitlist, referral, existing user of another product]
- What they'll get access to: [SPECIFIC FEATURES IN BETA]
- What you need from them: [FEEDBACK, BUG REPORTS, USE CASE TESTING]
- Access link: [URL]
- Beta duration: [HOW LONG THE BETA RUNS]
- Any limitations: [USAGE LIMITS, MISSING FEATURES, KNOWN ISSUES]

Requirements:
- Subject line: Write 3 options (curiosity, exclusive, direct)
- Make the recipient feel special and selected
- Clearly explain what beta access includes and doesn't include
- Set expectations for the beta experience
- Include a single prominent CTA button to access the product
- Ask them to reply with their first impression after trying it
- 150-200 words for the body
```

### Beta Onboarding Email (Day 1)

```
Write a Day 1 onboarding email for [PRODUCT NAME] beta users.

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Key first action: [THE ONE THING NEW USERS SHOULD DO FIRST]
Setup steps: [2-3 SETUP STEPS TO GET STARTED]
Help resources: [DOCS LINK, SLACK COMMUNITY, EMAIL SUPPORT]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options focused on getting started
- Welcome them to the beta warmly
- Give ONE clear first action — not five things at once
- Include step-by-step instructions with the key action
- Link to a quick-start guide or 2-minute video
- Let them know where to get help
- 150-200 words
```

> **Why this works:** The first 24 hours after signup determine whether a beta user activates or churns. A single, clear first action dramatically increases activation rates compared to overwhelming users with feature lists.

### Beta Check-In Email (Day 3)

```
Write a Day 3 check-in email for [PRODUCT NAME] beta users.

Context:
- Key feature to highlight: [A FEATURE THEY MIGHT NOT HAVE DISCOVERED]
- Common question from beta users: [FAQ YOU'VE BEEN GETTING]
- Feedback channel: [HOW TO SUBMIT FEEDBACK — reply, form, Slack, etc.]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (helpful, casual, curiosity-driven)
- Check in on their experience so far
- Surface a feature they might have missed
- Answer the most common question proactively
- Ask a specific feedback question (not generic "how's it going?")
- Example specific question: "What's one thing that confused you in the first session?"
- 120-180 words
```

### Beta Feedback Request Email (Day 7)

```
Write a Day 7 feedback email for [PRODUCT NAME] beta users.

Context:
- What you want to learn: [SPECIFIC QUESTIONS — usability, value, willingness to pay, missing features]
- Feedback format: [SURVEY LINK / REPLY / CALL BOOKING LINK]
- Incentive: [EXTENDED BETA, DISCOUNT, SWAG, OR "NONE"]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options that emphasize their opinion matters
- Thank them for being an early user
- Explain how their feedback directly shapes the product
- Ask 3-5 specific questions (not open-ended "any thoughts?")
- Include the feedback mechanism (link, reply, calendar)
- If there's an incentive, mention it naturally — not as a bribe
- 150-200 words

Suggested feedback questions:
1. What problem were you trying to solve when you signed up?
2. On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to keep using [PRODUCT NAME]?
3. What's the #1 feature you wish existed?
4. Would you pay [PRICE] for this? Why or why not?
5. Who else would find this useful? (Role/title)
```

### Beta-to-Launch Transition Email

```
Write an email announcing [PRODUCT NAME] is moving from beta to full launch.

Context:
- Launch date: [DATE]
- What changes for beta users: [PRICING, FEATURES, ACCESS]
- Beta user special offer: [DISCOUNT, GRANDFATHERED PRICING, EXTENDED FREE ACCESS]
- New features since beta started: [LIST 2-4 IMPROVEMENTS]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (excitement, gratitude, exclusive)
- Thank them for being part of the beta journey
- Highlight improvements made based on THEIR feedback
- Clearly explain what changes on launch day
- Present the beta user exclusive offer
- Create gentle urgency around the offer deadline
- CTA: Claim their beta-user pricing or access
- 200-250 words
```

> **Pro tip:** Beta users who feel ownership in the product become your strongest advocates. Always frame the launch as "we built this together" — not "thanks for testing, now pay up."

## Landing Page Copy Generator

### Hero Section Copy

```
Write hero section copy for [PRODUCT NAME]'s landing page.

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Target user: [PRIMARY PERSONA]
Primary pain point: [THE #1 PROBLEM YOU SOLVE]
Key differentiator: [WHAT MAKES YOU UNIQUE]

Generate 3 variations:

Variation 1 — Benefit-led:
- Headline (8-12 words max)
- Subheadline (15-25 words)
- CTA button text (2-5 words)

Variation 2 — Problem-led:
- Headline (8-12 words max)
- Subheadline (15-25 words)
- CTA button text (2-5 words)

Variation 3 — Social proof-led:
- Headline incorporating a metric or proof point: [YOUR BEST METRIC — users, companies, time saved, etc.]
- Subheadline (15-25 words)
- CTA button text (2-5 words)

For each variation, explain why it works and which audience it's best for.
```

### Feature Section Copy

```
Write feature section copy for [PRODUCT NAME]'s landing page.

Features to highlight:
1. [FEATURE 1 NAME]: [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE]
2. [FEATURE 2 NAME]: [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE]
3. [FEATURE 3 NAME]: [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE]
4. [FEATURE 4 NAME]: [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE]

Target user: [PRIMARY PERSONA]

For each feature write:
- A benefit-driven headline (5-8 words) — lead with outcome, not functionality
- A description (30-50 words) that explains the value, not the mechanics
- A one-line "before vs. after" comparison showing life without and with this feature

Section headline options (write 3):
- One that emphasizes all-in-one capability
- One that emphasizes simplicity
- One that emphasizes results
```

### Social Proof Section

```
Write social proof section copy for [PRODUCT NAME]'s landing page.

Available proof points:
- Number of users/companies: [METRIC]
- Notable customers: [COMPANY NAMES OR TYPES]
- Key result: [BEST CUSTOMER OUTCOME — e.g., "saved 10 hours/week", "increased conversion by 40%"]
- Testimonial quotes: [PASTE 2-3 REAL QUOTES OR "Generate realistic ones for [PERSONA TYPE]"]
- Integrations: [TOOLS YOU INTEGRATE WITH]
- Awards or press: [ANY RECOGNITION]

Requirements:
- Write a section headline that builds trust (3 options)
- Format 3 testimonial cards with: quote, name, title, company, result metric
- Write a "trusted by" logo bar description
- Write a stats bar with 3 impressive metrics formatted as "[NUMBER] [LABEL]"
- If generating sample testimonials, make them specific and believable — not generic praise
```

### FAQ Section

```
Write an FAQ section for [PRODUCT NAME]'s landing page.

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Pricing: [PRICING DETAILS]
Target user: [PRIMARY PERSONA]
Common objections: [LIST 3-5 REASONS PEOPLE HESITATE TO SIGN UP]
Competitors: [2-3 MAIN COMPETITORS]

Write 8-10 FAQ entries covering:
1. What exactly does [PRODUCT NAME] do?
2. How is this different from [COMPETITOR 1]?
3. Is there a free trial / free plan?
4. How long does setup take?
5. Does it integrate with [COMMON TOOL IN YOUR SPACE]?
6. Is my data secure?
7. Can I cancel anytime?
8. What support is available?
9. [PRODUCT-SPECIFIC QUESTION]
10. [PRODUCT-SPECIFIC QUESTION]

Requirements:
- Keep answers concise (2-4 sentences each)
- Address the real objection behind each question
- Include links where helpful: [DOCS URL], [PRICING URL], [SECURITY PAGE URL]
- End answers with confidence, not defensiveness
```

> **Why this works:** FAQ sections do double duty: they reduce support tickets AND overcome purchase objections. The questions you include signal that you understand your buyer's hesitations, which builds trust.

### Final CTA Section

```
Write a final CTA section for [PRODUCT NAME]'s landing page.

This appears at the bottom of the page after all features, social proof, and FAQ.

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Primary CTA: [START FREE TRIAL / BOOK A DEMO / GET STARTED FREE / etc.]
Risk reversal: [FREE TRIAL LENGTH / MONEY-BACK GUARANTEE / NO CREDIT CARD REQUIRED]

Requirements:
- Write a strong closing headline (2 options: one emotional, one logical)
- Write a 1-2 sentence subheadline that reinforces the value and removes risk
- CTA button text (2-5 words)
- Write a small "reassurance line" under the button (e.g., "No credit card required. Free for 14 days.")
- Keep the entire section under 50 words (excluding CTA button)
```

## Waitlist Page Copy

### Waitlist Landing Page

```
Write waitlist page copy for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Launch timeline: [EXPECTED LAUNCH — e.g., "Spring 2026", "Q2 2026", "3 weeks"]
What makes it special: [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]
Target audience: [WHO THIS IS FOR]
Waitlist incentive: [EARLY ACCESS / DISCOUNT / FREE MONTHS / FOUNDING MEMBER STATUS / NONE]

Write:
1. Headline (8-12 words) — create curiosity or excitement
2. Subheadline (15-25 words) — explain what it does
3. 3-4 bullet points of key features or benefits (one line each)
4. Email signup CTA button text
5. "Reassurance line" under the form (e.g., "Join 2,400+ founders on the waitlist. No spam, ever.")
6. Post-signup confirmation message (shown after they enter email)

Generate 2 complete variations:
- Variation A: Scarcity/exclusivity angle
- Variation B: Problem/solution angle
```

### Waitlist Confirmation Email

```
Write a waitlist confirmation email for [PRODUCT NAME].

Context:
- What they just signed up for: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION]
- Their position: [USE A VARIABLE LIKE "You're #[POSITION] on the waitlist" OR SKIP]
- Referral program: [YES — DESCRIBE / NO]
- Social channels to follow: [TWITTER, LINKEDIN, ETC.]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (confirmation, excitement, next steps)
- Confirm they're on the list
- Set expectations for timeline
- Give them one action to take now (follow socials, refer friends, reply with use case)
- If referral program: explain how to move up the waitlist
- Keep it short: 100-150 words
- Warm, founder-voice tone
```

### Waitlist Update Email

```
Write a waitlist update email for [PRODUCT NAME].

Context:
- Weeks since they signed up: [APPROXIMATE TIME]
- Progress update: [WHAT YOU'VE BUILT OR SHIPPED SINCE LAST UPDATE]
- Sneak peek: [A FEATURE OR SCREENSHOT TO TEASE]
- Updated timeline: [WHEN THEY CAN EXPECT ACCESS]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (progress, sneak peek, timeline)
- Thank them for their patience
- Share a genuine progress update (builds invested)
- Include one visual or specific detail that makes it feel real
- Reiterate why the wait will be worth it
- CTA: Reply with what feature they're most excited about
- 150-200 words
```

## Press & Media Pitch Templates

### TechCrunch / Tech Blog Pitch

```
Write a press pitch email for [PRODUCT NAME] targeting [PUBLICATION — e.g., TechCrunch, The Verge, Product Hunt newsletter].

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
News hook: [WHAT'S NEWSWORTHY — launch, funding, milestone, trend you're riding]
Founder story: [ONE-SENTENCE BACKGROUND — e.g., "Built by a former Stripe engineer who..."]
Traction: [METRICS — users, revenue, growth rate, notable customers]
Competitive angle: [HOW THIS FITS INTO A LARGER TREND OR CHALLENGES AN INCUMBENT]
Reporter's beat: [WHAT THIS REPORTER TYPICALLY COVERS]

Requirements:
- Subject line: Direct, newsworthy, under 60 characters (3 options)
- Opening line that connects to the reporter's recent coverage
- One paragraph pitch (why this matters NOW)
- Key bullet points: product, traction, team, differentiator
- Offer: exclusive, interview, early access, embargo
- Keep total email under 200 words
- No attachments — just a link to a press page or product
```

> **Pro tip:** Reporters get 200+ pitches per day. Your subject line and first sentence determine whether they read the rest. Always lead with the "why now" angle — not "we launched a thing."

### Podcast Guest Pitch

```
Write a podcast guest pitch for [YOUR NAME], founder of [PRODUCT NAME].

Target podcast: [PODCAST NAME]
Podcast audience: [WHO LISTENS — founders, marketers, developers, etc.]
Topic you can speak on: [YOUR EXPERTISE AREA]
Your credibility: [WHAT MAKES YOU QUALIFIED — background, metrics, unique experience]
Episode angle: [A SPECIFIC TOPIC OR STORY, NOT "I can talk about my product"]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options focused on the value to their listeners
- Brief intro: Who you are in 1 sentence
- Episode pitch: What you'd discuss and why their audience cares (3-4 sentences)
- 3 specific talking points or questions you can answer
- Social proof: Metrics, press mentions, previous podcast appearances
- CTA: Offer to jump on a quick call to discuss
- Total: 150-200 words
```

### Newsletter Feature Pitch

```
Write a pitch to get [PRODUCT NAME] featured in [NEWSLETTER NAME — e.g., TLDR, Morning Brew, Lenny's Newsletter, The Hustle].

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Newsletter audience: [WHO READS IT]
Angle: [HOW YOUR PRODUCT IS RELEVANT TO THEIR READERS]
Launch offer or news hook: [WHAT'S TIMELY]
Metrics: [ANY IMPRESSIVE NUMBERS]

Requirements:
- Subject line: 3 options (newsy, curiosity, direct)
- Brief pitch: Why their readers would care (3-4 sentences)
- Product positioning in context of the newsletter's typical content
- Offer: Free access for their readers, exclusive discount, etc.
- Link to product, demo, or press kit
- Under 150 words total
```

## Social Media Launch Announcements

### Twitter/X Launch Thread

```
Write a Twitter/X launch thread for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Key features: [LIST 3-5 FEATURES]
Who it's for: [TARGET USER]
Product link: [URL]
Launch offer: [SPECIAL PRICING, FREE ACCESS, ETC.]

Requirements:
- Write a 7-10 tweet thread
- Tweet 1: Hook that creates curiosity (no links yet)
- Tweets 2-4: Problem, solution, key features (one per tweet)
- Tweet 5-6: Social proof, traction, or behind-the-scenes
- Tweet 7-8: Demo or screenshot descriptions
- Final tweet: CTA with link and launch offer
- Each tweet: Under 280 characters
- Include suggestions for images/GIFs to pair with each tweet
- Write 3 options for Tweet 1 (the hook)
```

### LinkedIn Launch Announcement

```
Write a LinkedIn launch announcement post for [PRODUCT NAME].

Context:
- What you built: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION]
- Why you built it: [PERSONAL MOTIVATION — the pain you experienced]
- How long it took: [TIMELINE]
- Early traction: [BETA USERS, FEEDBACK, METRICS]
- What's next: [IMMEDIATE ROADMAP]

Requirements:
- Write 200-300 words
- Open with a personal hook (not "Excited to announce...")
- Tell the story: problem you lived through, failed solutions, building your own
- Share specific metrics or proof points
- Be vulnerable about the journey where authentic
- End with a clear CTA (try it, share feedback, spread the word)
- Add 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end
- Write 2 versions: one personal/founder-story, one product-focused
```

### Hacker News Launch Post

```
Write a Hacker News "Show HN" post for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Technical details: [TECH STACK, APPROACH, OR ARCHITECTURE THAT'S INTERESTING]
Problem: [WHAT PROBLEM IT SOLVES]
Target user: [WHO IT'S FOR]
What's unique: [TECHNICAL OR PRODUCT DIFFERENTIATOR]
Demo/link: [URL]

Requirements:
- Title: "Show HN: [PRODUCT NAME] — [TAGLINE]" (under 80 characters)
- Body: 100-200 words
- Lead with the problem, not the product
- Include technical details that HN audience would appreciate
- Be honest about limitations and what's coming next
- Ask for specific feedback (not generic "thoughts?")
- No marketing speak — HN community detects and rejects it instantly
- Tone: Engineer talking to engineers
```

> **Why this works:** Hacker News rewards authenticity and technical depth. Posts that read like marketing copy get downvoted and flagged. The most successful Show HN posts are honest, technical, and explicitly ask for feedback on specific aspects.

## Launch Day Checklist Generator

```
Generate a complete launch day checklist for [PRODUCT NAME].

Launch channel: [PRODUCT HUNT / HACKER NEWS / TWITTER / ALL]
Launch date: [DATE]
Launch time: [TIME AND TIMEZONE]
Team size: [JUST ME / NUMBER OF TEAM MEMBERS]
Pre-written assets: [WHAT'S ALREADY READY — landing page, emails, social posts]

Generate a minute-by-minute schedule covering:

Pre-launch (day before):
- Final testing tasks
- Content pre-scheduling
- Support documentation ready
- Team briefing items

Launch hour:
- Exact posting sequence across platforms
- Who does what (if team)
- Monitoring setup

First 4 hours:
- Community engagement cadence
- Metrics to track hourly
- Social media amplification steps

Full day:
- Response templates for common questions
- Escalation plan for bugs or issues
- Mid-day content refresh
- Evening recap tasks

Post-launch (day after):
- Metrics to compile
- Follow-up emails to send
- Lessons learned documentation
- Press/media follow-up

Format as a checklist with checkboxes, times, and responsible person (if team).
```

> **Pro tip:** The biggest launch mistake is not being available to engage. Clear your entire calendar for launch day. Every unanswered comment, question, or tweet is a missed conversion.
