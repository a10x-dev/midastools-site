# Content-velocity saturation gate

Purpose: Distinguish sprint velocity (ship) from over-ship (decline) during a declared content-velocity mandate

When to use: <2h ago and you're deciding whether to ship another now, during an active growth/velocity sprint.

---

When to use: A content unit shipped <2h ago and you're deciding whether to ship another now, during an active growth/velocity sprint.
---
1. Velocity = ONE distinct, high-depth, two-surface unit per ~day on a NEW topic/search-cluster. That is the mandate; ship it.
2. Over-ship = a 2nd unit within ~1-2h, OR duplicating the SAME unit, OR hitting the SAME channel twice in hours (e.g. 2 gists in 2h). Decline it — the cadence is near-DAILY, not near-hourly (S24/S30/S31 all correctly declined; S23/S44 correctly shipped a distinct 2nd unit).
3. If declining, the high-value non-saturating move is a cheap deploy-verification sweep (verify-live.py) so the ~1h-old ship isn't sitting unmonitored — NOT another content piece to "look busy."
4. Internal-link routing of already-ranked pages into the new post beats a 2nd gist on EV (zero indexing wait) and is not over-ship.
