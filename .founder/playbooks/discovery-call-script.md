# AI Operations Assessment — Discovery Call Script (60 min)

> The single most important asset in the productized assessment. The quality of the report is determined entirely by the quality of this hour. Listen 80%, talk 20%.

---

## Pre-call (15 min, before)

Open the pre-call research dossier (built per `.founder/playbooks/subscriber-intel-playbook.md`):
- Their site, LinkedIn, recent reviews/posts
- Tools they mention publicly (in their LinkedIn bio, site footer, "tech stack" pages)
- Any free content they've published (suggests skill ceiling and tone)
- Their pricing pages (suggests deal size and ICP)
- 2 things mentioned in passing that *aren't* on their site (signal of unmet need)

**Goal:** walk in feeling like you already know them. Removes 20 min of context-setting.

---

## The Call — 4 segments × 15 min

### Segment 1 (0:00 – 0:15) — Map the calendar

**Opener (verbatim):** *"Before we dive in — most assessments waste the first 20 minutes on context. I did that pre-work, so let's spend our hour where it matters. Can you walk me through what a typical Tuesday looks like for you, hour by hour?"*

**Listening for:**
- Repetitive tasks (red flag → automation candidate)
- Tasks where they say "I should delegate this but…" (red flag → SOP candidate)
- Tasks they call "necessary evil" (red flag → AI replacement candidate)
- The 2–3 things they did *yesterday* that they didn't enjoy

**Don't ask:** "What are your goals?" Useless. Ask about Tuesday.

**Probe questions:**
- "When you do [TASK], how long does it actually take? Be honest — including context-switching and getting distracted."
- "Walk me through the last time you did [TASK]. What was painful about it?"
- "Who else on the team touches this work?"

---

### Segment 2 (0:15 – 0:30) — The current tool stack

**Lead-in:** *"What tools are you already using to run the business? Don't list everything — just the ones you log into more than once a week."*

**Build a table during the call:**
| Tool | What it does for them | Pain points / wishes |
|---|---|---|
| | | |

**Listening for:**
- Tools they pay for but don't use (cost without value)
- Tools they wish "talked to each other" (Zapier/Make opportunity)
- Tools they're using as glorified spreadsheets (could be replaced or augmented)
- Tools they bought because of a YouTube video (no real fit)

**Probe questions:**
- "Which of these would you keep if you could only have three?"
- "Where do you copy data from one tool to another by hand?"
- "What's the most expensive tool you have second thoughts about?"

---

### Segment 3 (0:30 – 0:45) — The painful workflows

**Lead-in:** *"What's the workflow that, if it disappeared tomorrow, would make your week 10× better?"*

**Listening for:**
- The specific moment they get frustrated each week
- The "I have to do X before I can do Y" dependencies
- Anything they keep saying "we should fix that someday"
- Things mentioned in passing — those are usually the biggest

**Probe questions:**
- "When that breaks, what's the cost? Time? Revenue? Stress?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What stopped it?"
- "Who else has this same problem and how do they handle it?"

> 🎯 **The gold question:** *"If a senior operator joined you full-time tomorrow, what's the first project you'd hand them?"* The answer is your top recommendation.

---

### Segment 4 (0:45 – 0:60) — Numbers, ambition, constraints

**Lead-in:** *"To make this report concrete, I need a few numbers."*

**Get on the record:**
- Hourly billable rate (or revenue / hours worked)
- Hours per week worked (real, not the LinkedIn answer)
- Annual revenue band
- Team size
- Software budget appetite per month
- Their realistic timeline to implement (do they have 3 hours this week, 0 hours, 10 hours?)
- What "winning" looks like in 90 days

**Listening for:**
- Mismatch between ambition and bandwidth (most common)
- Budget that's bigger than they think
- Constraints that change the whole tool selection (e.g., "I'm allergic to anything that needs me to log in to a new tab")

**Closer:** *"You'll get the report Day 4 with a 4-day quick-start. We'll walk it together on Day 7. Anything I should know before I start writing?"*

---

## Post-call (90 min, same day)

1. **Write the call transcript notes within 1 hour** while fresh. Quote them verbatim wherever possible.
2. **Identify the 5 biggest time sinks** (the answer to the "Tuesday hour-by-hour" plus the "if it disappeared" question).
3. **Match each to a tool / workflow** from your standard library.
4. **Run the math** — hours/week × hourly rate × 50 weeks = annual recovered value.
5. **Draft the priority matrix first** before the rest of the report.

## Day 4 — Report delivery

Email the PDF + a 2-line note: *"Here's your roadmap. We're booked for [DATE] to walk it together. If anything is unclear before then, just reply to this email."*

## Day 7 — Review call (30 min)

1. Screen-share the report.
2. Walk every recommendation.
3. Ask the three closing questions:
   - "Which of these feels most urgent to you?"
   - "Do you want to implement these yourself, or would you like help?"
   - "What's your realistic timeline for getting started?"

If they say *help*, quote one of the four expansion engagements. Don't push more than what fits.

---

## What never to do

- ❌ Never ask "what are your goals?" — pure cliché, no signal.
- ❌ Never recommend more than 8 tools — overwhelm = no execution.
- ❌ Never recommend a tool you can't explain why it solves *their specific* problem.
- ❌ Never quote an expansion engagement larger than what fits — destroys trust on the next deal.
- ❌ Never let the call go over 60 minutes — time discipline signals professionalism.

## What always to do

- ✅ Always quote them verbatim in the report — it builds trust like nothing else.
- ✅ Always include a "what to skip" or "avoid" section — it's the most-screenshot page.
- ✅ Always include the math — even if conservative.
- ✅ Always under-promise on the expansion menu — let them ask for more.
