## Is AI art allowed on Etsy in 2026? Yes — with rules.

Etsy treats AI as a production tool (like a drawing tablet). You can sell AI art **you** created and curated, if you're honest about it:

- **Disclose** that AI was used (description + process fields). Hiding it gets you removed.
- **You** must be the designer — no reselling someone else's images.
- **Don't** call it "handmade." List digital art as digital art.
- **No mass spam** — Etsy suppresses shops that dump hundreds of undifferentiated images.

Note: purely AI-generated images **can't be copyrighted** in the US — so your moat is niche, curation, mockups, branding, and SEO, not the raw pixels.

## The 5-step workflow

1. **Make the art** in your niche (prompts above, or the Art Machine).
2. **Upscale** to 300 DPI / 4000px+ long edge — buyers print these.
3. **Package** several frame ratios (2:3, 3:4, 4:5, ISO, 11x14) + a 1-page print-instructions PDF.
4. **Mockups** — show it framed on a wall. Nobody buys a bare PNG.
5. **Write a listing that ranks** (next section).

## Etsy SEO that actually ranks

- **Title:** front-load the exact search phrase. "Boho Abstract Wall Art Set of 3, Printable Terracotta Arch Prints, Digital Download" beats "Sunset Dreams Trio."
- **Use all 13 tags** — mix broad ("printable wall art") + specific ("terracotta boho print set"). Never waste one on a single generic word.
- **Match title and tags** — Etsy boosts listings where the title phrase repeats in tags.
- **First 2 description lines:** what it is, what they get (sizes/files), how it's delivered.

## Common mistakes

1. Mass-uploading random images — Etsy suppresses spam shops.
2. Low-res files — blurry prints = refunds. Upscale to 300 DPI.
3. Bare PNGs, no mockups — buyers need to see it framed.
4. Cute titles instead of searchable ones.
5. Hiding the AI — disclose it; it keeps listings live + builds trust.
6. Only selling $3 singles — bundle into sets to lift order value.
