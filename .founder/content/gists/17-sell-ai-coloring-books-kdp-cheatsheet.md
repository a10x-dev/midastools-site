## Is selling AI coloring books on KDP allowed in 2026? Yes — with rules.

Amazon KDP treats AI as a production tool, but it requires disclosure and quality:

- **Disclose AI** during upload — KDP asks "Did you use AI?" Answer honestly (AI-generated images = yes). Hiding it risks account termination.
- **You** curate and assemble the book — no reselling someone else's file.
- **No low-effort spam** — KDP suppresses accounts that dump dozens of near-identical books. Quality + a real niche beats volume.
- **Clean line art only** — pages must be pure black-line on white, print-ready at 300 DPI, or you get rejections and 1-star "won't print" reviews.

Note: purely AI-generated images **can't be copyrighted** in the US — your moat is the niche, the theme cohesion, the cover, and the keywords, not the raw pixels.

## The 6 coloring-book niches that actually sell

| Niche | Why it sells | Page count |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Kids' animals / dinosaurs | Evergreen gift demand, repeat buyers | 30–50 bold-line pages |
| Adult mandalas / florals | "Relaxation" buyers, high repeat | 40–60 intricate pages |
| Toddler "first" books (big, simple) | Parents buy in multiples | 20–30 thick-line pages |

| Niche hobbies (trucks, mermaids, space) | Low competition, devoted buyers | 30–50 pages |

**Pick ONE niche and one age band.** A focused "Bold & Easy Dinosaurs for Kids 4–8" outsells a random 50-animal grab-bag — Amazon's search and buyers both reward a clear promise on the cover.

## What makes a page actually printable (the part beginners get wrong)

- **Pure black line art on white** — no grayscale, no shading, no color. Shaded "coloring" pages print muddy and get refunds.
- **300 DPI**, sized to your trim (most common: **8.5 × 11 in**).
- **Bold, closed outlines** for kids; thinner intricate lines for adults.
- **Single-sided friendly** — color bleeds through, so design assuming one image per leaf.
- **Consistent style** across all pages — one cohesive look, not 40 different art styles.

## The 6-step KDP workflow

1. **Pick niche + age band** (table above) and a clear promise ("Bold & Easy Ocean Animals, Ages 4–8").
2. **Generate 30–50 cohesive pages** at 300 DPI / 8.5×11 (prompts above, or the Coloring Book Machine).
3. **Assemble the interior PDF** — one image per page, plus a title page. (The Machine outputs this print-ready.)
4. **Design the cover** to KDP's calculated spine width for your page count. A strong cover is most of the sale.
5. **Upload to KDP** as a paperback, disclose AI, set categories + 7 keywords (see below), price **$6.99–$8.99**.
6. **Order one proof copy** — verify it physically prints clean before you go live.

## KDP keywords + pricing that convert

- **7 keyword slots** — use buyer phrases, not adjectives: `coloring book for kids ages 4-8`, `dinosaur coloring book`, `gift for boys`, `bold and easy coloring`, `toddler activity book`, etc.
- **Title carries the promise**: `[Theme] Coloring Book for [Audience]: [Count] Bold & Easy Designs`.
- **Price $6.99** for ~30–50 pages (sweet spot for impulse + gift buys); $8.99–$9.99 for thicker adult books.
- **Royalty**: KDP pays ~60% minus print cost — a $6.99 book nets roughly **$2–3 per copy**, and it sells while you sleep.
- **Series it**: once one book sells, ship 5 more in the same niche. Backlist compounds — that's the real money.

## 5 common mistakes that kill coloring-book sales

1. **Shaded or grayscale "coloring" pages** — they print muddy; buyers refund and leave 1-stars. Pure line art only.
2. **Wrong trim / low DPI** — uploading 72 DPI screen images = blurry print. Always 300 DPI at your trim size.
3. **No niche** — a random 40-animal book ranks for nothing. One theme, one age band, one promise.
4. **Weak cover** — the cover is 80% of the click. Don't ship a plain text cover; show a colored sample of an interior page.
5. **One-and-done** — a single book rarely earns. The sellers who make $500+/mo have 10–30 books in one tight niche.
