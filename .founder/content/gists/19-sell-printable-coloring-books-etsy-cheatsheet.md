## Is selling printable coloring books on Etsy allowed in 2026? Yes — with rules.

- **You are the designer** — you used AI as a tool and assembled the book. List yourself as the creator; no reselling someone else's file.
- **Disclose AI** where Etsy asks. Be honest; don't imply a famous artist drew it.
- **No trademarks or protected characters** — no Disney, no Pokémon, no branded logos, no living-artist styles. Fast takedown. Original cartoon subjects only.
- **Describe the download accurately** — page count, trim size (8.5 × 11"), PDF format, "instant download, print at home, nothing ships."
- **Print quality** — clean black-line art on white at print resolution. Gray/fuzzy/low-res pages are the #1 cause of refunds and 1-stars.

Note: purely AI-generated images **can't be copyrighted** in the US — your moat is the niche, the cohesion, the cover, and the keywords, not the raw pixels.

## What makes a page actually printable (the part beginners get wrong)

- **Pure black line art on white** — no grayscale, no shading, no color. Shaded "coloring" pages print muddy and get refunds.
- **Print resolution** sized to trim (most common **8.5 × 11"**).
- **Bold closed outlines** for kids; thinner intricate lines for adults.
- **Consistent line weight** across the whole book — one cohesive style, not 30 different ones.

## 5 mistakes that kill printable-coloring-book sales

1. **Gray or shaded pages** — not colorable, eats the buyer's ink. "No shading, no gray, pure black and white" — every page.
2. **Low resolution** — blurry prints = refunds + 1-stars. Export at print resolution; test-print before listing.
3. **Selling a single page as a "book"** — buyers want a cohesive set. 15+ pages with a cover and title page, or it reads as low-effort.
4. **Trademarked characters** — fast takedown. Original subjects only.
5. **One book, then waiting** — a single listing isn't a business. The shops that earn have a *shelf*, each book ranking for its own search.
