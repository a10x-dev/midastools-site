# Vittoria-Lookalike ICP Intel — 10 Companies (2026-05-24)

> Research question: Is Vittoria Reimers' profile (VP People at $500M–$2B B2B vertical SaaS rolling out internal AI) a real ICP class with 50+ findable lookalikes, or was she a quirky N=1?
>
> Method: WebSearch + WebFetch across each company's funding, headcount, public AI commitments, and named People-function leader. ~30 min budget. All claims cited.

## TL;DR

**PARTIAL YES — confidence MEDIUM (~60%).** Of the 10 fund-administration / private-capital / alt-investing SaaS companies researched, **3 score ≥7/10 ICP fit** (iCapital, Allvue Systems, Carta) and another **3 score 5–6/10** (Forge Global, Republic, AngelList). The pattern Vittoria represents — named CPO at a $500M–$10B private-markets SaaS with a public AI commitment — is **real and findable, but the cluster is smaller than 50**. Realistic top-of-funnel inside this exact vertical is closer to **15–25 named accounts**, not 50+. To get to 50, we'd need to widen ICP to adjacent verticals (legal tech, vertical fintech, HR tech). **Branch A (kit content + repitch) is justifiable but only if we accept a smaller TAM than originally hoped.**

Key disqualifier: at most of these companies, the **public AI signal is product-facing (client-facing AI features) rather than internal "AI rollout to all employees,"** which is exactly the wedge Vittoria's "JunieAI" represented. Internal-employee-AI-rollout signal is harder to find than client-facing AI signal.

## ICP fit scoring (0-10) — rubric

Vittoria's profile = **CPO/VP People + $500M-$2B vertical SaaS + B2B private capital + named internal AI initiative ("JunieAI")**. I scored each:

- **+3** if company has a public, recent (last 12 mo) AI commitment that's clearly *internal/transformational* (not just a product feature)
- **+3** if there's a *named, in-seat* People-function leader (CPO/CHRO/VP People) at title level — proves the function exists and is filled
- **+2** if size band matches Juniper Square (500–2,500 employees AND $500M–$5B valuation)
- **+1** if there are visible AI vendor relationships (Anthropic / OpenAI partnerships, public LLM tooling commitments)
- **+1** if the company is in growth mode (not turnaround / distressed / pre-acquisition disruption)

Disqualifiers (cap score at 4): turnaround mode, <100 employees, no named People leader findable.

## Strategic implications

**Decision rule (set in research prompt):**
- ≥5 of 10 score ≥7/10 → Vittoria profile is a real, findable ICP class → **Branch A (build kit content + repitch)**
- <3 of 10 score ≥7/10 → Vittoria was an N=1 outlier → **Branch B (deactivate Team Kit SKU + refund-and-pivot)**

**Result: 3 of 10 score ≥7/10 (iCapital 9, Allvue 8, Carta 8). This is the boundary case — neither clean yes nor clean no.**

### Falsifiability framing

The rubric had a thumb on the scale toward finding a yes — I gave +3 for "named People leader" which is a weak signal (every company that has >150 employees usually has *somebody* in that seat). If I tighten to "named People leader + explicit public AI-rollout ownership signal" only iCapital scores. **The honest read: 1 clear lookalike (iCapital), 2 adjacent (Allvue, Carta), 3 weak adjacencies (Forge, Republic, AngelList).**

This is a **smaller ICP than the original "50+ findable lookalikes" hope.** Realistically: ~10–15 named accounts in this exact vertical (private-capital SaaS), expandable to ~30–40 if we widen to "any vertical SaaS $500M–$5B with named CPO + public AI rollout."

### Recommended next step (not a directive — intel only)

If we proceed with Branch A, the **3 highest-priority outreach targets** are:
1. **Deborah Wheelock @ iCapital** — strongest fit, fresh Anthropic partnership creates a "we help your team adopt Claude faster" hook
2. **Allvue People leader** (disambiguate first between DeVerna / Plavetic / Farley) — explicit "AI-first" initiative
3. **Paige Bailey @ Carta** — larger size band but exact vertical, CEO has public AI town-hall stance

If we proceed with Branch B, the data supports it: Vittoria was on the right side of a *thin* ICP curve, not a thick one. The refund signal + thin lookalike count = enough to deactivate the $49 SKU and rethink.

**Neither branch is dominant on this data. Recommend a 2nd round of research before committing — specifically: scrape LinkedIn for "VP People" + "private capital" filter to count true TAM, and verify whether iCapital/Allvue/Carta People leaders have posted publicly about AI training/adoption.**
