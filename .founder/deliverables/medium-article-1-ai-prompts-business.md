# I Tested 200+ AI Prompts for Business — These 7 Were Worth More Than Any Course I've Taken

I spent the last few months doing something most people would call obsessive: testing hundreds of AI prompts across real business scenarios.

Not the generic "write me a blog post" stuff. I'm talking about prompts designed to replace actual business processes — the kind of work you'd normally pay a consultant $5,000 for.

Here are the 7 prompts that genuinely changed how I work. I'm sharing them in full so you can test them yourself.

---

## 1. The "Business Autopsy" Prompt (Strategy)

This one replaced a $3,000 business consultant for me:

```
You are a seasoned business strategist with 20 years of experience advising startups and small businesses. Analyze my business:

Business: [YOUR BUSINESS]
Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY]
Current revenue: [AMOUNT]
Biggest challenge: [DESCRIBE]

Provide:
1. Three revenue opportunities I'm probably missing
2. The single biggest bottleneck holding me back
3. A 30-day action plan with specific weekly milestones
4. Two competitors doing something I should steal (and how to do it better)

Be brutally honest. I need truth, not motivation.
```

**Why it works:** The "be brutally honest" instruction and the specificity of the output format forces the AI to give you actionable advice instead of generic platitudes. I ran this for three different projects and each time got at least one insight I hadn't considered.

## 2. The "Sales Page in 10 Minutes" Prompt (Copywriting)

```
Write a high-converting sales page for:

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Price: [PRICE]
Target customer: [WHO BUYS THIS]
Main problem it solves: [PROBLEM]
Top 3 features: [LIST THEM]

Structure:
- Headline that calls out the pain
- "Imagine this..." section painting the after-state
- 3 benefit blocks (not features — BENEFITS)
- Social proof section (suggest what to include)
- FAQ with 5 objection-handling questions
- Strong CTA with urgency

Tone: conversational, confident, zero fluff. Like talking to a smart friend who needs convincing.
```

**Why it works:** Most people prompt AI with "write copy for my product." This prompt forces a complete sales page structure with psychological triggers built in. The "like talking to a smart friend" instruction nails the tone every time.

## 3. The "Content Multiplier" Prompt (Social Media)

This turned one idea into a week of content:

```
I have one piece of content:
[PASTE YOUR BLOG POST, ARTICLE, OR VIDEO SCRIPT]

Turn this into:
1. A Twitter/X thread (8-10 tweets, hook first tweet hard)
2. A LinkedIn post (professional tone, personal story angle)
3. An Instagram carousel script (10 slides, punchy text)
4. A TikTok/Reels script (60 seconds, pattern interrupt opening)
5. An email newsletter (subject line + 300-word body)

Rules:
- Each format should feel NATIVE to the platform
- Don't just shorten — re-angle for each audience
- First line of each MUST stop the scroll
```

**Why it works:** The "don't just shorten — re-angle" instruction is the key. Without it, AI gives you five versions of the same text. With it, you get genuinely different content for each platform.

## 4. The "Email That Gets Replies" Prompt (Cold Outreach)

```
Write a cold outreach email:

I am: [YOUR ROLE] at [YOUR COMPANY]
I'm reaching out to: [THEIR ROLE] at [TYPE OF COMPANY]
I want them to: [DESIRED ACTION — call, demo, reply, etc.]
My unique value: [WHY SHOULD THEY CARE]

Rules:
- Subject line under 6 words (curiosity, not clickbait)
- First sentence must NOT be about me
- Under 100 words total
- End with a low-friction CTA (not "Let me know when you're free for a 30-min call")
- Sound human. No "I hope this email finds you well."
```

**Why it works:** The constraints are everything. "Under 100 words" and "first sentence must NOT be about me" eliminate 90% of what makes cold emails terrible. I've used variations of this to book meetings with people who normally ignore cold outreach.

## 5. The "Profit Finder" Prompt (Revenue)

```
Analyze my business for hidden revenue opportunities:

Business: [DESCRIBE YOUR BUSINESS]
Current products/services: [LIST THEM]
Average customer spends: [AMOUNT]
Customer base size: [NUMBER]

Find:
1. Three upsell/cross-sell opportunities I'm missing
2. A productized service I could launch this week
3. One partnership model that could 2x my reach
4. A pricing change that would increase revenue without losing customers
5. One "info product" I could create from what I already know

For each, estimate potential monthly revenue and effort to implement (1-10 scale).
```

## 6. The "Hiring Without HR" Prompt (Operations)

```
I need to hire a [ROLE] for my [TYPE OF BUSINESS].

Budget: [SALARY RANGE]
Location: [REMOTE/LOCAL/HYBRID]
Must-have skills: [LIST 3-5]

Create:
1. A job description that attracts A-players (not corporate-speak)
2. Five interview questions that reveal character, not just skills
3. A paid test project I can give finalists (under 2 hours of work)
4. Red flags to watch for specific to this role
5. An onboarding checklist for their first 7 days

Make the job description sound like a real human wrote it. No "fast-paced environment" or "rockstar" language.
```

## 7. The "Weekly CEO Brief" Prompt (Productivity)

This is the one I use literally every Monday:

```
Act as my virtual chief of staff. Here's my business situation this week:

Wins from last week: [LIST 2-3]
Challenges: [LIST 2-3]
Revenue this month: [AMOUNT vs. TARGET]
Top 3 priorities: [LIST THEM]
Time available: [HOURS THIS WEEK]

Give me:
1. A brutally honest assessment of my priorities (am I working on the right things?)
2. The ONE thing that will move the needle most this week
3. Three things I should STOP doing or delegate
4. A Monday-to-Friday schedule blocking my top priorities
5. One blindspot I'm probably not seeing
```

## The Pattern Behind All of These

Notice what makes these prompts different from the generic ones you see everywhere:

1. **Context before request** — Every prompt gives the AI your specific situation
2. **Output structure** — Numbered lists and specific formats force useful answers
3. **Constraints** — "Under 100 words," "be brutally honest," "no corporate-speak" — the constraints are where the magic happens
4. **Follow-up ready** — Each output gives you something to iterate on

The difference between a $0 prompt and a $1,000 prompt is specificity.
