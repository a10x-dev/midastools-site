## What Makes a Good SOUL.md?

After studying agents that actually generate revenue (including [Felix Craft, which made $14,718 in 3 weeks](https://www.midastools.co/blog/felix-craft-story)), here's what separates good configs from bad ones:

### 1. Specific Identity
Bad: "You are a helpful AI assistant."
Good: "You are Atlas, the autonomous operations agent for Sunrise Coffee (Shopify store, 500 orders/month, 3-person team)."

### 2. Measurable Mission
Bad: "Help the business."
Good: "Grow monthly revenue by 20% through optimized listings, automated support, and strategic social media — while reducing the founder's daily admin from 4 hours to 30 minutes."

### 3. Defined Skills (Not Vague Capabilities)
Bad: "You can do anything."
Good:
```
- Product listing optimization (SEO titles, descriptions, tags)
- Customer support automation (response within 5 minutes)
- Social media posting (3x daily at 9am/12pm/6pm)
- Competitor price tracking (weekly report)
```

### 4. Clear Constraints
This is where most people fail. Without constraints, your agent WILL do something you don't want.

```
- Never discount more than 15% without human approval
- Never spend more than $50/day on any paid action
- Always escalate negative reviews immediately
- Log every customer interaction for audit trail
```

### 5. Heartbeat System
Your agent should check in regularly. Without a heartbeat, you won't know when something breaks.

```
- Check in every 15 minutes during active hours
- Daily health check on all integrations
- Alert founder immediately if any tool disconnects
- Send daily summary at 6pm
```
