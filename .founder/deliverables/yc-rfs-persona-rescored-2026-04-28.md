## Section A — Top 3 RFS Items That Survive the Persona Filter

### 1. RFS #2 — AI-Native Service Companies (34/40) — THE ONE THAT MATTERS

**Why it works:** Our actual paying customers will be SMBs who want **work delivered**, not software accessed. A 45+ church-administrator who landed on midastools.co does not want to "integrate an MCP server." She wants someone to write her sermon-prep prompts, build her newsletter sequence, ship her a finished asset for $500. RFS #2 is the only thesis where the YC partner literally describes our customer.

**Specific midastools angle:** Pick **ONE** SMB vertical from the 5 real businesses already in our list and own it. Strongest candidate: **"Done-for-you AI content ops for solo professionals and small ministries."** Lawyers, churches, consultants, regional businesses — the persona we already attract organically through Felix Craft / SOUL.md. Starting price $497/mo retainer, $1,997 one-shot landing-page-plus-email-sequence package.

**Concrete deliverable (not a developer tool):** A productized DFY service page at `/dfy/smb-content-ops` with three packages, calendar booking, intake form, and 5 case studies built from sample work for the 5 known SMBs in our list (free first deliverable as proof — converts trust into testimonial).

**YC narrative:** "We are RFS #2. We sell DFY AI content operations to mainstream SMBs that AI tooling has left behind. Our 23 organic signups skew 45+ Yahoo/AOL — exactly the unserved 80% of the SMB market. We turn $40/hr human service work into $400 productized AI deliverables."

**Specific midastools angle:** Reposition the entire catalog under one banner: **"Lifetime AI tools — no subscriptions, ever."** Lead the homepage with this, not with the kits themselves. The Midas/wealth framing maps cleanly: "Stop renting AI. Own it."

**Concrete deliverable:** Homepage A/B test of the "no subscriptions" hero + a comparison table: midastools $97-once vs. Jasper $360/yr vs. Copy.ai $588/yr vs. ChatGPT Teams $300/yr. One blog post: "I cancelled $1,248/yr in AI subscriptions and replaced them with $97 of prompt kits." Mainstream-friendly framing, not developer-friendly.

**YC narrative:** Secondary, supports #2 as a moat. "We also kill the recurring-revenue SaaS model for non-technical buyers."

### 3. RFS #4 — Company Brain (19/40, with caveat)

**Why it works:** The 5 real businesses in our subscriber list each have a "company brain" problem at small scale — the law firm needs intake-letter templates, the hospital needs patient-comms scripts, the church needs sermon and donor-letter prompts. Each is a 45+ owner-operator who wants the brain *delivered finished*, not built via Linear/Slack/GitHub integration.

**Specific midastools angle:** "Your Business in 100 Prompts" — a $1,500 one-shot productized service. Intake call → we audit the business's voice, processes, customer types → ship a 100-prompt private library tailored to them, hosted as a private Notion workspace. No SaaS, no integration, no MCP.

**Concrete deliverable:** Landing page `/your-business-in-100-prompts`, intake form, 1 pilot delivery for free to a current SMB subscriber (the law firm or church) → use as flagship case study.

**YC narrative:** This is the upsell from the #2 retainer. RFS #4 says "domain knowledge is the bottleneck" — we sell the bottleneck-removal as a service, not as software.

## Section B — Explicitly Reject

### RFS #12 "Software for Agents" — REJECTED (11/40)

**Why the prior memo was wrong:** Both prior analyses recommended `midastools-mcp`. They scored it on *YC thesis fit* and *engineering feasibility* — not on **whether our actual audience would touch it**. They wouldn't. A 45+ Yahoo-email user has never opened Claude Code, never installed an MCP server, never heard the word "Cursor." Building distribution channels for a customer base we do not have is a developer-founder ego move dressed as strategy.

**Learning for the co-founder:** Every YC thesis is written from the perspective of YC-funded developer-tooling companies' likely customers. RFS #12 is *for* developer-tools companies. We are not one. The "thesis fit" score in prior memos was conflating "this is a real trend" with "this is *our* trend." It is not ours.

**Other items to flag as over-optimistic in the prior analyses:**
- **RFS #13 (Sell to Huge Companies)** was scored 🟢/🟡 in the Apr 27 memo. Realistic score is 17/40. F100 buyers do not buy from a 33-day-old solo company with $0 revenue. Using the RFS as cold-email name-drop is fine; treating it as a strategic vector is fantasy.
- **RFS #15 (AI OS for Companies)** was kept warm in both prior memos. Drop it. We have neither the engineering team nor the enterprise sales motion. 16/40.
- **RFS #11 (SaaS Challengers)** was scored 🟡 prior. The framing angle is real but the prior memo over-indexed on "thesis fit" without asking whether our 21 PDF/Notion kits are actually replacing SaaS at the workflow level. They're not — they're cheaper alternatives, which is a marketing claim, not a moat.

## Section D — Counter-Position: Should We Apply to YC At All?

**Case for applying:**
- RFS #2 is genuinely the closest-fitting YC thesis to our pivot in 5+ years of YC RFS history
- Free $500K (current YC standard deal) buys 18-24 months of runway and removes the May-15 kill date
- YC stamp solves the credibility problem with the 45+ SMB buyer ("YC-backed" = trust signal)
- Application is a 4-hour write-up, not a strategic pivot

**Case against:**
- YC funds companies with **revenue + signal**. We have $0 revenue and 23 subs. The honest probability of acceptance is 1-3%.
- The 4 hours spent on the application is 4 hours not spent on the 25 DFY pitches/week ramp that would actually get us paying customers
- Even if accepted, YC accelerates a B2B/enterprise motion. The mainstream-SMB-DFY play is closer to a Lifestyle/Bootstrap/Indie Hackers shape than a $1B venture-scale company. YC may push us toward a worse-fit ICP.
- Applying *before* having $1K MRR is asking YC to bet on a story. They prefer numbers.

**Recommendation:** **Don't apply yet. Apply to S27 (deadline ~Apr 2027) after hitting $5K MRR.** Spend the next 30 days on the RFS #2 DFY ramp instead — 25 pitches/week, one productized vertical, one paid pilot by May 15. If MRR materializes, apply with traction. If it doesn't, the May-15 kill date answers itself and YC is moot.

The YC-thesis alignment is the second-best free marketing asset we have (after the domain). Use it as **language**, not as a destination, until the numbers are there.
