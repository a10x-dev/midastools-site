# AI Audit ICP Intel — May 10 Decision Brief
**Date:** 2026-05-04 | **Author:** Research analyst | **For:** May 10 kill/reprice/pivot call
**SKU under review:** AI Clarity Assessment, $997, 6 days live, 3 cold pitches sent Apr 28, 0 replies T+~43h.

---

## Executive Summary

1. **A real $500–$5,000 SMB AI audit market exists.** Multiple named operators sell into it today. It is not a phantom category. (🟢 high)
2. **$997 is in a structural dead zone.** The fixed-price productized audit market clusters at two poles: **$500–$1,500 "starter audit"** (e.g., Promptful $750, AgentsLabs €97) and **$1,999–$5,000 "real audit"** (ChatDoctor $1,999/$4,999, ConsultKit recommends $5,000 anchor, Discovery & Strategy $1,500–$3,000). $997 is too expensive to be impulse, too cheap to signal expertise. (🟢 high)
3. **The dominant buyer objection is NOT price — it's "vague deliverables and generic advice you could have Googled."** Trust + scope clarity > dollar amount. (🟢 high)
4. **The ICP does not aggregate on LinkedIn first.** They aggregate on **SmarterX/Marketing AI Institute Slack (10K+ members), Coachvox AI's coaching newsletter, AI For Small Business Substack, and r/smallbusiness comment threads on "how are you using AI"** posts. LinkedIn is a *closing* channel, not a discovery channel. (🟡 moderate)
5. **Strategic call: the data points to NEITHER Plan A nor B as currently scoped.** Plan A ($297 reprice) survives the dead-zone problem but kills positioning vs. the $750–$1,999 productized tier. Plan B (cold LinkedIn) is wrong channel for cold discovery. **Recommend: kill the $997 SKU as-is, reposition as a $1,499 productized audit with a tightly-scoped 4-deliverable spec, distribute through Slack communities + Substack guest posts, NOT cold LinkedIn.** (🟡 moderate)

## Q3 — Dominant objections (🟢 high)

The signal is consistent across 4 independent sources:

1. **"Vague deliverables / sellers disappear / generic advice I could have Googled."** Direct buyer language from a Fiverr AI-consultant review (https://minsjohnfrancis.com/i-booked-an-ai-consultant-on-fiverr-for-my-business-heres-my-honest-review/ — the review framed AI consulting as a horror-story category). (🟢 high)
2. **"Six-month experts charging enterprise rates."** Public skepticism that AI consultants are people who watched YouTube tutorials last quarter. https://medium.com/@tonimaxx/the-ai-training-scam-when-six-month-experts-become-corporate-educators-ce84861843b6 (🟢 high)
3. **"I don't even know what I'd ask AI to do."** SMB owners report opening ChatGPT, typing "help me with my business," getting useless output, and concluding AI isn't for them. https://www.entrepreneur.com/growing-a-business/is-your-small-business-invisible-to-chatgpt-and-google-ai/502677 — the problem is *prompt-shaped*, not *audit-shaped*. This is the most dangerous objection: the buyer doesn't believe an outsider can fix it for $997. (🟢 high)
4. **Premature-buyer signal:** "If you're under $100K ARR and still figuring out core offering, AI consulting is premature." https://king-intelligence.com/blog/ai-consulting-small-business-worth-it.html — much of midastools' 20-sub list is likely below this threshold. (🟡 moderate)

**Synthesis:** The objection blocking the 3 unanswered pitches is almost certainly NOT $997 being too high. It's **"I don't trust this is different from generic advice"** + **"I'm not sure what I'd actually receive"**. Repricing to $297 makes this *worse*, not better — cheaper price reinforces "this is generic advice." (🟢 high)

## Q4 — Pricing landscape reality check (🟢 high)

**Is $997 sane?** No. Not at the current scope/positioning.

Three converging price clusters:

- **Tripwire / under-$500:** AgentsLabs €97, Promptful $175, levelupwithai's low end $500. Buyer expectation: 60–90 min, async report, low risk. (🟢 high)
- **Productized real audit / $750–$1,999:** Promptful $750, levelupwithai $1,500, ChatDoctor $1,999. Buyer expectation: half-day engagement, named deliverable artifacts, follow-up call. (🟢 high)
- **Premium audit / $2,500–$5,000+:** ConsultKit $5K anchor, ChatDoctor $4,999, RSM/Big-4 starting $25K. Buyer expectation: 1–2 weeks, multi-document report, executive presentation. (🟢 high)

**$997 sits in the gap between cluster 2 and the no-man's-land below cluster 3.** It's $250 more than Promptful's $750 (the dominant productized comparable) but $1,000 less than ChatDoctor's $1,999 (the dominant premium-entry comparable). Buyer can't place it.

**The dominant productized price** for solo/SMB AI audit appears to be **$750–$1,999**, with $1,500 as the modal price (Discovery & Strategy industry benchmark; levelupwithai upper bound). (🟢 high)

## Strategic Implication for May 10

**Plan A — reprice to $297:** ❌ NOT supported. The data says price is not the blocker; trust + deliverable clarity is. Dropping to $297 lands the SKU below AgentsLabs' equivalent tripwire while keeping the $997-tier scope, which is incoherent. Buyers in the under-$500 tier expect async-only.

**Plan B — cold LinkedIn:** ❌ NOT supported as a *discovery* channel. LinkedIn is a *closing* channel for already-warm-niche prospects (regulated SMB ops/team leads). Cold-spray LinkedIn into "solopreneurs" replicates the failed cold-pitch shape at higher volume.

**Plan C — kill:** ⚠️ Premature. The category is real and 6 days + 3 pitches is not a market test. Killing now means abandoning a category where named competitors are transacting.

**Recommended Plan D — reposition, don't reprice:**
1. **Raise to $1,499**, match the productized real-audit tier where Promptful/levelupwithai/ChatDoctor compete.
2. **Publish a 4-artifact deliverable spec on the /ai-audit page** (workflow map + AI-readiness scored matrix + top-3 recommendations with time-savings + human-in-the-loop design), copying the structure every named competitor uses. This kills the "generic advice" objection.
3. **Distribute through SmarterX Slack, AI For Small Business Substack, Coachvox AI newsletter, and r/smallbusiness comment-engagement** — not cold LinkedIn. LinkedIn DM only after a niche-specific warm signal.
4. **Ship a $97–$197 async tripwire audit** (matching AgentsLabs €97 / Promptful $175) as the front door for Midastools' 20-sub list and gist traffic. The current 3 pitches at $997 didn't reply because there was no $97 step before $997.
5. **Decision gate May 20** (not May 10): if no replies to 10+ pitches at $1,499 with the new deliverable spec + tripwire funnel by May 20, then kill.

The hard takeaway: **the market exists, the pricing is wrong, the channel is wrong, and the trust signal is missing. None of those are fixed by repricing to $297 or by cold LinkedIn.**
